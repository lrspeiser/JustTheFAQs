FAQs: John Tyler

Early Life and Education
Where was John Tyler born and who were his parents?
John Tyler was born on March 29, 1790, at the Greenway Plantation in Charles City County, Virginia. His father, John Tyler Sr., was a prominent plantation owner and served in several political roles, including a judge and governor of Virginia. His mother, Mary Marot Armistead, died of a stroke when John was seven years old. Tyler came from a family tied to the First Families of Virginia, with ancestral lineage to English settlers and colonial Williamsburg.
Related topics: Greenway Plantation
Political Career
How did John Tyler ascend to the presidency?
John Tyler became the 10th President of the United States after President William Henry Harrison died of pneumonia just a month into his term. Tyler, who was Harrison’s Vice President, assumed the presidency on April 4, 1841, setting precedence as the first Vice President to ascend to the presidency due to the death of a sitting President.
Related topics: Presidents of the United States
Legacy and Impact
What was John Tyler's impact on the precedent of presidential succession?
John Tyler set a crucial precedent in presidential succession by asserting that he had assumed the full powers and the position of the presidency, not just acting as a caretaker or vice president. This claim was crucial in establishing the principle that the Vice President fully becomes President in the event of the current President's death, which was eventually codified by the 25th Amendment.

Controversies and Policies
How did Tyler's policies lead to conflicts with his own party?
John Tyler faced substantial conflicts with the Whig Party, which he had been elected with. Although initially deferring to Whig leaders, he vetoed key party bills like the establishment of a national bank, believing in a strict interpretation of the Constitution. This led to most of his cabinet resigning and his subsequent expulsion from the party, earning him the nickname "His Accidency."

Foreign Relations
What were John Tyler's notable achievements in foreign policy?
John Tyler's administration is credited with notable achievements in foreign policy, including the signing of the Webster–Ashburton Treaty which settled the boundary between Maine and Canada. He also oversaw the Treaty of Wanghia with China, which opened trade to the United States on favorable terms. Additionally, he implemented the "Tyler Doctrine" related to Hawaii, asserting American dominance in the Pacific.
Related topics: Webster–Ashburton Treaty, Treaty of Wanghia
Personal Life and Legacy
How many children did John Tyler have, and how is his legacy viewed today?
John Tyler fathered a total of 15 children, more than any other American president, with two wives, Letitia Christian and Julia Gardiner. His legacy is seen as controversial, often ranked as a less effective president due to political divisions, although some praise his firm stance on constitutional issues. Historians appreciate his foreign policy achievements. He is remembered for setting the precedent for lawful presidential succession.
Related topics: Letitia Christian Tyler, Julia Gardiner Tyler
Post-Presidency
What did John Tyler do after his presidency?
After leaving the presidency, John Tyler retired to his Virginia plantation, Sherwood Forest, where he continued to focus on farming. He briefly returned to political life, opposing the Union and serving in the Confederate Congress during the onset of the American Civil War. Tyler remained active in Virginia politics, believing in the rights of the Southern states.
