FAQs: David Von Erich

Early life
What was David Von Erich's early life like?
David Von Erich, born David Alan Adkisson, was named after his mother's brother, who had died of a brain tumor before his birth in 1958. He was raised in a family that loved hunting and fishing, often participating in these activities with his father, Fritz Von Erich. David was a high school basketball and football star, earning a scholarship to North Texas State University for sports before deciding to pursue a career in professional wrestling.
Related topics: Brain tumor, University of North Texas
Professional wrestling career
How did David Von Erich start his wrestling career?
David Von Erich made his professional debut in June 1977, quickly establishing himself as a breakout star within the Von Erich family. He possessed a fiery temper and charisma that made him popular in interviews. His first major match was against NWA World Heavyweight Champion Harley Race, which ended in a 30-minute draw. He had several championship reigns in his early years, including the NWA Texas Heavyweight and Tag Team Championships, and also competed internationally.
Related topics: NWA World Heavyweight Champion, Harley Race, NWA Texas Heavyweight Champion
Championship Wrestling from Florida (1981–1982)
What notable events happened during David Von Erich's time in Championship Wrestling from Florida?
In Florida, David worked as a heel under the management of J. J. Dillon and became part of a stable that included Kendo Nagasaki and Jimmy Garvin. He was mentored by Dory Funk Jr. and often teamed with him. This period also saw a family storyline where David's brother Kerry joined as a vain muscleman counterpart to David's loudmouthed persona. By mid-1982, David returned to Texas, bringing a wealth of experience from his time in Florida.
Related topics: Championship Wrestling from Florida, J. J. Dillon, Kendo Nagasaki, Dory Funk Jr., Kerry Von Erich
World Class Championship Wrestling (1982–1984)
What were David Von Erich's famous feuds in World Class Championship Wrestling?
David Von Erich had significant feuds with Gorgeous Jimmy Garvin and The Freebirds during his tenure in World Class Championship Wrestling (WCCW). He and Garvin devised a storyline where they feuded over the NWA Texas Heavyweight Championship, culminating in Garvin serving as David's valet for a day. His feud with The Freebirds, particularly with Michael Hayes and Terry Gordy, became legendary and was a central storyline in WCCW's history.
Related topics: World Class Championship Wrestling, Gorgeous Jimmy Garvin, NWA Texas Heavyweight Championship, Fabulous Freebirds, Michael Hayes, Terry Gordy
Feud with Ric Flair (1983–1984)
What is the significance of David Von Erich's feud with Ric Flair?
David Von Erich's feud with Ric Flair is significant because many believed it was a precursor to David becoming the NWA World Heavyweight Champion. They had a highly publicized match on December 25, 1983, at Reunion Arena where Flair retained the title. This feud was characterized by intense interviews and storyline development, and it was closely watched by fans before David's untimely death.
Related topics: Ric Flair, NWA Missouri Heavyweight Championship, Reunion Arena, NWA World Heavyweight Championship
Death
What were the circumstances surrounding David Von Erich's death?
David Von Erich died on February 10, 1984, while on tour with All Japan Pro Wrestling. The official cause was acute enteritis leading to ruptured intestines, as stated in the Consular Report of Death. However, there were speculations of a possible painkiller overdose. These conflicting theories have fueled much discussion in wrestling circles.
Related topics: All Japan Pro Wrestling, Enteritis
Professional wrestling style and persona
What were David Von Erich's signature wrestling moves?
David Von Erich was known as 'the Yellow Rose of Texas' and was famous for his signature wrestling moves: the 'Iron Claw,' a type of clawhold, and the 'Dream Machine,' a sleeper hold. These moves became staples in his matches and contributed to his wrestling persona.
Related topics: Iron Claw
Personal life
What is known about David Von Erich's personal life?
David Von Erich was married twice. His first marriage to Candy McLeod ended in tragedy when their daughter, Natosha Zoeanna, died of SIDS at 13 weeks old. They divorced in 1979. In 1982, he married Patricia 'Trisha' Matter, and they remained married until his death. Trisha appeared in a posthumous tribute to David in a wrestling magazine, expressing gratitude to his fans for their support.
Related topics: SIDS
