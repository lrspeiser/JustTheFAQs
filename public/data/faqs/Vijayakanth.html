FAQs: Vijayakanth

Contributions and Influence
What were Vijayakanth's contributions to the Tamil film community and society?
Vijayakanth was a philanthropist who supported struggling producers and demanded food equality on film sets. As the President of Nadigar Sangam, he helped establish a charitable trust that paid pensions to low-income film industry members. He was likened to M.G. Ramachandran for his humanitarian work.
Related topics: Nadigar Sangam, M. G. Ramachandran, Charitable trust
Health Decline and Legacy
What happened during Vijayakanth's later years and how is he remembered?
During the mid-2010s, Vijayakanth's health declined due to thyroid problems and later diabetes, which confined him to a wheelchair. He passed away on 28 December 2023. He was posthumously awarded the Padma Bhushan in January 2024 and is remembered as a revered actor and leader in Tamil Nadu.
Related topics: Padma Bhushan, Government of India, Leader of Opposition
Background and Early Life
Who was Vijayakanth and when was he born?
Vijayaraj Alagarswami, popularly known as Vijayakanth, was an Indian actor, director, producer, philanthropist, and politician. He was born on 25 August 1952 in Madurai, Madras State, India.
Related topics: M. G. Ramachandran, Tamil cinema, Desiya Murpokku Dravida Kazhagam
Political Journey
How did Vijayakanth transition into politics?
Vijayakanth entered politics by founding the regional political party Desiya Murpokku Dravida Kazhagam (DMDK) in 2005. He contested in various elections and served as a Member of Legislative Assembly (MLA), leading his party to significant electoral successes, including becoming the Leader of the Opposition in Tamil Nadu.
Related topics: Desiya Murpokku Dravida Kazhagam, Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly, 2011 Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly election
Career in Film
What are some of the films and accolades associated with Vijayakanth?
Vijayakanth acted in over 150 Tamil films, including popular hits like "Vaidehi Kathirunthal" (1984), "Amman Kovil Kizhakale" (1986), and "Captain Prabhakaran" (1991). He was known as "Captain" due to his role in "Captain Prabhakaran". He won two Filmfare Awards South, three Tamil Nadu State Film Awards, and was awarded the Kalaimamani title in 2001.
