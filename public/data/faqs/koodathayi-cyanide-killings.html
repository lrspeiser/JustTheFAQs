FAQs: Koodathayi Cyanide Killings

Overview
What were the Koodathayi cyanide killings?
The Koodathayi cyanide killings were a series of six murders committed over 14 years in the village of Koodathayi, Kerala, India. The victims were killed by cyanide poisoning, and the incidents drew significant public and media attention leading to the arrest of Jolly Joseph.
Related topics: Cyanide poisoning, Koodathayi
Timeline of the Crimes
How did the murders unfold over the years?
The killings began in 2002 with the death of Annamma Thomas after drinking water at home. They continued with the deaths of Tom Thomas in 2008 and Roy Thomas in 2011, both under suspicious circumstances involving cyanide. In 2014, two more victims, Mathew Manjayadil and Alphine Shaju, died, followed by Sily Shaju in 2016. Investigations eventually pointed to Jolly Joseph.
Related topics: Manner of death, Autopsy
Suspects
Who was Jolly Joseph and what role did she play?
Jolly Joseph, the main suspect, was a member of the victim family through marriage. Described as sociable and religious, she was accused of murdering six family members using cyanide, acquired with the help of co-suspects M. S. Mathew and Praji Kumar. She had allegedly lurked around the sites of these deaths and was arrested in 2019 after a police inquiry.
Related topics: Arrest, Cyanide
In Popular Culture
How has the case been portrayed in media and entertainment?
The Koodathayi killings inspired various cultural portrayals, including a film by Antony Perumbavoor starring Mohanlal, a Malayalam TV serial called 'Krithyam,' and episodes in Sony TV's 'Crime Patrol.' Additionally, a Netflix documentary titled 'Curry & Cyanide: The Jolly Joseph Case' and a podcast on Spotify feature the case.
