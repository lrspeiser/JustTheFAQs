FAQs: AEW Worlds End

Overview
What is AEW Worlds End?
AEW Worlds End is a professional wrestling pay-per-view (PPV) event produced by All Elite Wrestling (AEW). Established in 2023, the event is held annually in late December, specifically on the weekend of or before New Year's Eve. It also hosts the finals of the Continental Classic (C2) tournament, with the inaugural tournament coinciding with the first version of Worlds End.
Related topics: All Elite Wrestling, Pay-per-view, Continental Classic
Event History
What significant events have happened during AEW Worlds End?
The inaugural AEW Worlds End event took place on December 30, 2023, at the Nassau Veterans Memorial Coliseum in Uniondale, New York. This marked AEW's first PPV held in New York state. The main event featured Maxwell Jacob Friedman (MJF) defending the AEW World Championship against Samoa Joe. Additionally, Eddie Kingston won the Continental Classic tournament at this event. The next installment of Worlds End is set for December 28, 2024, at the Addition Financial Arena near Orlando, Florida, establishing this PPV as an annual event.
Related topics: Worlds End (2023), Nassau Veterans Memorial Coliseum, Eddie Kingston, Worlds End (2024)
Continental Classic Tournament
What is the Continental Classic tournament and its significance at AEW Worlds End?
The Continental Classic is an annual tournament that occurs after AEW's Full Gear event in mid-November, culminating at AEW Worlds End. It features a round-robin format with Blue and Gold Leagues composed of six wrestlers each. The winner receives the AEW Continental Championship, and the current champion automatically qualifies for the tournament. Matches follow specific 'Continental Rules,' ensuring fair competition without outside interference, contributing to the tournament's prestige and excitement during the Worlds End PPV.
Related topics: Continental Classic, AEW Full Gear, AEW Continental Championship
