FAQs: Arsenal F.C.

Club Background
What is Arsenal Football Club and what are they known for?
Arsenal Football Club, commonly known as Arsenal, is a professional football club based in Holloway, North London, England. They compete in the Premier League, the highest level of English football. Known for their rich history, Arsenal has won 13 league titles, including an unbeaten title, a record 14 FA Cups, and several other domestic and European trophies. Arsenal is the third-most successful club in English football, known for their distinctive red and white kits.
Related topics: Premier League, List of English football champions, FA Cup
History and Achievements
How did Arsenal begin and what are some of its major achievements?
Founded in 1886 by munitions workers at the Royal Arsenal in Woolwich, the club was initially named Dial Square before being renamed Royal Arsenal. They became the first club from southern England to join the Football League in 1893. Arsenal has achieved major successes including 13 League Championships, 14 FA Cups, two League Cups, and several community shields, making it one of the most decorated clubs in England. It also became the first London club to reach the UEFA Champions League final in 2006.

Stadium and Facilities
Where does Arsenal F.C. play their home games?
Arsenal plays their home games at the Emirates Stadium, which has a capacity of 60,704. This state-of-the-art venue in Holloway, North London, replaced their previous home, Highbury, in 2006. The stadium is known for its modern facilities and is named after the airline company Emirates, which is also the club's sponsor.
Related topics: Emirates Stadium, Highbury
Supporters and Rivalries
Who are Arsenal's main rivals and how intense is their competition?
Arsenal's primary rivalry is with Tottenham Hotspur, with whom they contest the North London derby. Further rivalries include Chelsea and Manchester United, particularly fierce in times when both clubs competed for league titles. These derbies are some of the most anticipated fixtures in the English football calendar.

Mascot
Who is the mascot for Arsenal F.C.?
Arsenal's club mascot is Gunnersaurus Rex, a smiling, 7-foot-tall green dinosaur. Created based on a drawing by an 11-year-old competition winner in 1993, Gunnersaurus is a popular figure at matches and community events, renowned for his playful characteristics and love for the club.

Club Colours and Crest
What are the traditional colors of Arsenal, and how has their crest evolved?
Traditionally, Arsenal's home colors have been bright red shirts with white sleeves paired with white shorts. These colors were introduced by manager Herbert Chapman in the 1930s. Over the years, the club's crest has evolved from depicting three cannons to a single one, with the club's name and motto 'Victoria Concordia Crescit,' meaning 'Victory Through Harmony.'

Management and Ownership
Who owns Arsenal and who is their current manager?
Arsenal is predominantly owned by American sports tycoon Stan Kroenke through his KSE UK Inc. Currently, the team's manager is Mikel Arteta, a former club captain, who took over in December 2019. Arteta is known for his strategic management style, having previously worked under renowned manager Pep Guardiola.
