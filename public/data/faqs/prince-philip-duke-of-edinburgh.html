FAQs: Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh

Early Life and Background
Where was Prince Philip born and what is significant about his early life?
Prince Philip was born on 10 June 1921 on the dining room table in Mon Repos, a villa on the Greek island of Corfu. As a prince of both Greece and Denmark, Philip's early life was marked by exile from Greece when he was only eighteen months old. His family faced political turmoils, particularly during the Greco-Turkish War, leading to their evacuation onboard the British vessel HMS Calypso, with the infant Philip carried to safety in a fruit box. Despite these turbulent beginnings, Philip was related to several European royal families, including the Greek and Danish royal families, a fact that influenced his upbringing and future alliances.

Marriage to Queen Elizabeth II
How did Prince Philip and Queen Elizabeth II meet, and what were the circumstances of their marriage?
Prince Philip and Elizabeth II first met in 1934 at the wedding of Elizabeth’s uncle, but they were properly acquainted during King George VI's visit to the Royal Naval College in 1939. The young 13-year-old Elizabeth fell in love with Philip, and they began to exchange letters. Philip, having settled in Britain and adopted the surname Mountbatten, proposed to Elizabeth in 1946, and their engagement was officially announced in 1947. Despite some controversies regarding Philip’s foreign birth and financial standing, they were married at Westminster Abbey on 20 November 1947, a ceremony broadcast to millions globally. Philip was styled as the Duke of Edinburgh and was given the title of His Royal Highness.
Related topics: Elizabeth II, Wedding of Princess Elizabeth and Philip Mountbatten
Role as Royal Consort
What were Prince Philip's duties and contributions as the consort to Queen Elizabeth II?
As the consort to Queen Elizabeth II, Prince Philip supported her in numerous public duties and engagements, making over 22,219 solo engagements throughout his lifetime. He was instrumental in modernizing the perception of the monarchy and was involved in several organizations. His patronage spanned over 780 organizations, emphasizing youth, the environment, education, and industry. Philip also founded The Duke of Edinburgh’s Award, aimed at supporting young people in personal self-development and perseverance.
Related topics: Duke of Edinburgh's Award, World Wide Fund for Nature
Military Career and Honors
How did Prince Philip contribute to military efforts during World War II, and what honors did he receive?
During World War II, Prince Philip served with distinction in the Royal Navy, participating in key battles such as the Battle of Crete and the Battle of Cape Matapan. His role was pivotal in several operations, and he was noted for his innovative tactics. Philip was mentioned in dispatches, awarded the Greek War Cross, and rose to the rank of Commander by the time of Queen Elizabeth's ascension to the throne. Later, he was made Admiral of the Fleet, Field Marshal, and Marshal of the Royal Air Force in the UK, marking his esteemed military career.
Related topics: Royal Navy, Battle of Cape Matapan
Retirement and Final Years
When did Prince Philip retire from public duties, and what were some highlights of his final years?
Prince Philip retired from public duties on 2 August 2017 after completing 22,219 solo engagements. In his final years, he continued to influence public life, attending select events such as the wedding of Prince Harry and Meghan Markle. Despite a mishap in January 2019 when he was involved in a car accident near Sandringham Estate, Philip remained active and was seen driving on private estates. His resilience and continued presence in public life were notable, even as he dealt with health issues.
Related topics: Retirement of the Duke of Edinburgh, Prince Harry
Death and Legacy
What was the impact of Prince Philip's death, and how is he remembered?
Prince Philip passed away peacefully at Windsor Castle on 9 April 2021, at the age of 99. His death marked the end of an era, as he was the longest-serving royal consort in history. The funeral, held on 17 April 2021 at St George’s Chapel, was a subdued affair due to COVID-19 restrictions, with only 30 attendees. Philip is remembered for his dedicated service to the Queen and the Commonwealth, his candid nature, love of sports, and numerous contributions to environmental causes and youth programs.
