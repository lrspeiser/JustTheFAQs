FAQs: Tottenham Hotspur F.C.

Crest and Kit
What is the design of Tottenham Hotspur’s crest and how did it develop?
The crest features a cockerel standing on a football. Initially introduced in 1921, it symbolizes the fighting spirit associated with the club, inspired by Harry Hotspur. In the 1950s, a heraldic shield was incorporated into the design. The current modernized version, introduced in 2006, features a sleeker cockerel without the shield, though it was briefly reintroduced between 2017-2018.
Related topics: Crest (heraldry), Harry Percy (Hotspur), White Hart Lane
Stadium History
What were the previous grounds used by Tottenham Hotspur?
Tottenham Hotspur initially played their matches on the public lands of Tottenham Marshes, then moved to Northumberland Park, and eventually settled at White Hart Lane before relocating to their new stadium.
Related topics: Tottenham Marshes, White Hart Lane
General Information
Who owns Tottenham Hotspur F.C.?
Tottenham Hotspur is owned by ENIC International Ltd., an investment company run by Joe Lewis and Daniel Levy. ENIC owns 86.58% of the club.
Related topics: ENIC Group, Joe Lewis (British businessman), Daniel Levy (businessman)
Recent Developments
What were some of the changes and developments implemented at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium?
A new 62,850-capacity stadium was built as part of the Northumberland Development Project and opened in April 2019. The facility features state-of-the-art technology and aims to serve as a catalyst for local economic regeneration.
Related topics: Northumberland Development Project
Support
What kind of support does Tottenham Hotspur have and what controversies surround it?
Tottenham Hotspur has a large fan base, particularly in North London and the Home Counties. Historically, the club had a significant Jewish following, which led to controversial anti-Semitic chants from rival fans. In response, Tottenham supporters began to adopt the term "Yid Army" as an identity, a practice that has received mixed reactions from different groups.

Historic Achievements
What significant achievements does the club have in domestic and European football?
In domestic football, Tottenham has won two league titles, eight FA Cups, four League Cups, and seven FA Community Shields. In European competitions, they have won one European Cup Winners' Cup and two UEFA Cups. They were also runners-up in the 2018–19 UEFA Champions League.
