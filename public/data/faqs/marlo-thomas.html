Career Highlights
What was the significance of Marlo Thomas's role in "That Girl"?
Marlo Thomas's role in "That Girl," where she portrayed Ann Marie, was significant for its portrayal of a single, independent woman pursuing a career in New York City. The show was one of the first to focus on a working single woman who did not live with her parents, paving the way for future series featuring similar themes. Thomas was also a pioneer as she was one of the few women at the time to produce her own television series. She opted to end the series on her terms, avoiding a conventional wedding finale to reflect a progressive message for her female audience.

Early Life and Background
What is known about Marlo Thomas's early life and family?
Marlo Thomas was born in Detroit and raised in Beverly Hills, California. She is the eldest child of Rose Marie Cassaniti and famed comedian Danny Thomas. Her father was of Lebanese descent, and her mother was Sicilian American. Thomas has a brother, producer Tony Thomas, and a sister, Terre. She attended Marymount High School and then the University of Southern California, earning a degree in education. Her childhood nickname "Marlo" originated from a mispronunciation of her name "Margo" by her family.

Activism and Philanthropy
What are some of Marlo Thomas's contributions to activism and philanthropy?
Marlo Thomas has been a dedicated activist, especially known for her work with St. Jude Children's Research Hospital, founded by her father Danny Thomas. She is the National Outreach Director and launched the Thanks & Giving campaign in 2004 to support the hospital. Thomas was also instrumental in founding the Ms. Foundation for Women, the first women's fund dedicated to supporting liberal women's voices nationwide.

Personal Life
What is notable about Marlo Thomas's personal life and marriage?
Marlo Thomas met talk show host Phil Donahue in 1977 on his show, and they experienced immediate chemistry. The couple married on May 21, 1980. Thomas became the stepmother to Donahue's five children from his previous marriage. Thomas has emphasized her choice to be a friend to her stepchildren rather than a traditional mother figure, fostering strong, lasting relationships. Phil Donahue passed away in 2024.

Later Career
In what ways did Marlo Thomas expand her career after "That Girl"?
After "That Girl," Marlo Thomas broadened her career by studying at the Actors Studio with Lee Strasberg and Sandra Seacat, and performing on Broadway. She made various television and film appearances, including roles on "Friends," "Law & Order: Special Victims Unit," and in Broadway plays like "Social Security" and "The Shadow Box." She is also known for her children's book and album "Free to Be... You and Me," and has published several best-selling books.
