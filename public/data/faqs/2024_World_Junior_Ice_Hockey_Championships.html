FAQs: 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships

Background
Why was Gothenburg chosen as the host city for 2024, and were there any changes in the hosting plan?
Gothenburg was initially announced as the host city for the 2022 championships on March 14, 2019. However, due to the COVID-19 pandemic, the event was moved from Gothenburg to Edmonton and Red Deer, Alberta, for 2022. Consequently, Gothenburg's hosting was rescheduled to 2024.

Overview
What is the 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships, and where was it held?
The 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships is the 48th edition of the Ice Hockey World Junior Championship's top division. This tournament took place from December 26, 2023, to January 5, 2024, in Gothenburg, Sweden. It was the first time Gothenburg had hosted the event and the seventh time Sweden had been the host nation.
Related topics: IIHF World Junior Championship, Gothenburg, Sweden
Final Results
Who won the 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships, and what were the final standings?
The United States won their sixth title in the 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships, defeating the host country Sweden 6–2 in the gold medal game. Sweden finished as the runner-up, Czechia secured third place, and Finland came in fourth.

What were the key details of the 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships?
The tournament featured 10 teams and was held in two venues in Gothenburg, Sweden: Scandinavium and Frölundaborg. Over the course of the event, 29 games were played, with a total of 219 goals scored and an attendance of 170,673 spectators.

What are the 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships?
The 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships, also known as the 2024 WJHC, is the 48th edition of the IIHF World Junior Championship's top division. The tournament took place from December 26, 2023, to January 5, 2024, in Gothenburg, Sweden. For the seventh time, Sweden hosted the WJHC, with Gothenburg chosen as the specific location this time. The United States emerged victorious, claiming their sixth championship title by defeating Sweden with a score of 6–2 in the gold medal game.
