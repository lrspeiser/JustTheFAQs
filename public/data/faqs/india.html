FAQs: India

Overview
What is India's geographical location?
India is located in South Asia, surrounded by the Indian Ocean to the south, the Arabian Sea to the southwest, and the Bay of Bengal to the southeast. It shares land borders with Pakistan to the west, China, Nepal, and Bhutan to the north, and Bangladesh and Myanmar to the east. It is in the vicinity of Sri Lanka and the Maldives in the Indian Ocean.
Related topics: South Asia, Indian Ocean, Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal, Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, Myanmar, Sri Lanka, Maldives
Demographics
How populous is India?
India is the most populous country in the world, with an estimated population of 1,428,627,663 in 2023, overtaking China's population. The country is known for its diversity, with hundreds of languages spoken and various ethnic groups residing within its borders.
Related topics: List of countries by population (United Nations), Diversity of India
History
What are some key historical empires in ancient India?
Key historical empires in ancient India include the Maurya and Gupta Empires. The Maurya Empire was known for its political consolidation under rulers like Ashoka, who promoted Buddhism. The Gupta Empire is often referred to as the Golden Age of India, notable for advancements in science, technology, engineering, arts, dialectic, literature, logic, mathematics, astronomy, religion, and philosophy.
Related topics: Maurya Empire, Gupta Empire, Ashoka
Geography
What are the major geographical features of India?
India's major geographical features include the Himalayan mountain range to the north, the Thar Desert to the west, the Indo-Gangetic Plain, the Deccan Plateau, and the Western and Eastern Ghats. These features encompass diverse climates and ecosystems, contributing to India's biodiversity.
Related topics: Himalayas, Thar Desert, Indo-Gangetic Plain, Deccan Plateau, Western Ghats, Eastern Ghats
Economy
How has India's economy evolved in recent years?
India's economy has shown significant growth, positioning itself as one of the world's largest and fastest-growing economies. It is now the third-largest economy by purchasing power parity. The country has experienced a notable shift towards technology and services, with information technology and software services becoming major contributors to India's GDP. However, challenges such as poverty, unemployment, and economic inequality persist.
Related topics: Economy of India, Purchasing power parity, Information technology in India
Biodiversity
Why is India considered a megadiverse country?
India is considered a megadiverse country due to its significant biological diversity, hosting a high percentage of the world's flora and fauna, including numerous endemic species. The country features a wide range of habitats, from the Himalayan regions to the deserts and rainforests, contributing to its rich biodiversity. India's efforts in environmental conservation are reflected in its numerous national parks and protected areas.
Related topics: Biodiversity of India, National parks of India, Protected areas of India
Politics and Government
What type of political system does India have?
India operates as a federal parliamentary democratic republic, with a President as the head of state and a Prime Minister as head of government. Its political system is characterized by a multi-party system with power divided between the central government and various states. The constitution, which came into effect in 1950, is the country's supreme legal document, describing India as a sovereign, socialist, secular, and democratic republic.
Related topics: Constitution of India, Federalism in India, President of India, Prime Minister of India
International Relations
How does India engage in international relations?
India plays an active role in international relations, being a prominent member of several international organizations, including the United Nations and the World Trade Organization. It maintains strategic partnerships with various countries and has a special relationship with Russia. India's foreign policy emphasizes its status as a significant regional power in South Asia, actively engaging in peacekeeping missions and fostering economic and strategic collaborations worldwide.
Related topics: Foreign relations of India, United Nations, World Trade Organization, India–Russia relations
