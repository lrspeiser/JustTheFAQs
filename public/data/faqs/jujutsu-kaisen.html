FAQs: Jujutsu Kaisen

General Information
What is Jujutsu Kaisen?
Jujutsu Kaisen is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Gege Akutami. The series was serialized in Shueisha's Weekly Shōnen Jump from March 2018 to September 2024 and is compiled into 30 tankōbon volumes. It follows high school student Yuji Itadori who joins a secret organization of Jujutsu Sorcerers to eliminate a powerful curse named Ryomen Sukuna, of whom Yuji becomes the host. The series is well-known for its adventure and dark fantasy themes.

Manga and Anime
Who is the author of Jujutsu Kaisen?
The series is written and illustrated by Gege Akutami. Gege Akutami first published a four-chapter series titled Tokyo Metropolitan Curse Technical School, which later served as a prologue to Jujutsu Kaisen.

Media Expansion
Is there an anime adaptation of Jujutsu Kaisen?
Yes, Jujutsu Kaisen has been adapted into an anime television series. The first season aired from October 2020 to March 2021, and the second season aired from July to December 2023. An adaptation of the 'Culling Game' arc has been announced.

Story Elements
What is the basic plot of Jujutsu Kaisen?
Jujutsu Kaisen follows Yuji Itadori, a high school student with extraordinary physical abilities, who becomes involved with the occult world. After swallowing a cursed object, he becomes the host for a powerful curse, Ryomen Sukuna. Yuji joins an organization of Jujutsu Sorcerers to defeat Sukuna and other curses while learning to control his new powers.

Sales and Popularity
How well has Jujutsu Kaisen performed in sales?
By September 2024, the Jujutsu Kaisen manga had over 100 million copies in circulation, including digital versions and Jujutsu Kaisen 0, making it one of the best-selling manga series of all time.

Production Details
What are some influences on Jujutsu Kaisen’s creation?
Gege Akutami has cited Neon Genesis Evangelion and horror films as influences on Jujutsu Kaisen, aiming to blend mythological aspects with horror. Additionally, Akutami is inspired by manga creators like Yoshihiro Togashi (YuYu Hakusho, Hunter × Hunter) and Tite Kubo (Bleach).
