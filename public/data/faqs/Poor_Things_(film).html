FAQs: Poor Things (film)

Accolades
What awards and nominations has 'Poor Things' received?
'Poor Things' won the Golden Lion at the 80th Venice International Film Festival. It received 11 nominations at the 96th Academy Awards, winning 4, including Best Actress for Emma Stone. It also won the Golden Globe Award for Best Motion Picture – Musical or Comedy. The film was recognized as one of the top ten films of 2023 by several organizations.
Related topics: 80th Venice International Film Festival, 96th Academy Awards, Golden Globe Award for Best Motion Picture – Musical or Comedy
Reception
How was 'Poor Things' received by critics and audiences?
'Poor Things' received positive reviews, with a 93% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes and a score of 88/100 on Metacritic. Critics praised its imaginative storytelling and Emma Stone's performance, although some critiques centered on the film's portrayal of gender and sexuality. It was acknowledged for its unique visual style and thematic depth.
Related topics: Rotten Tomatoes, Metacritic
Overview
What is the film 'Poor Things' about?
Poor Things is a 2023 film directed by Yorgos Lanthimos, based on the 1992 novel by Alasdair Gray. The film revolves around Bella Baxter, a young woman in Victorian London brought back to life by a brain transplant. It explores themes of rebirth, sexual liberation, and social norms. The movie stars Emma Stone, Mark Ruffalo, and Willem Dafoe.
Related topics: Yorgos Lanthimos, Emma Stone, Victorian London
Filming Details
What were the filming conditions like for 'Poor Things'?
'Poor Things' was filmed in Hungary, with production starting in August 2021 and concluding in December of the same year. It was notably the first film to use Kodak's 35mm Ektachrome color reversal motion picture film stock since 2018. Willem Dafoe had a demanding makeup routine, spending several hours daily in the makeup chair.
Related topics: Hungary, Kodak
Plot Summary
How does Bella Baxter's character develop in 'Poor Things'?
Bella Baxter begins as nearly mute with an infant's mind due to her unique brain transplant. Her character evolves as she gains language and begins to understand the world, seeking pleasure and autonomy. Her journey includes leaving with a debauched lawyer, engaging in sexual exploration, encountering philosophy, and embracing socialism, all while later rediscovering her past life as Victoria, leading to further conflicts and resolutions.
Related topics: Socialism, Victorian London, Brain transplant
Production
How was the development process for 'Poor Things'?
Development for 'Poor Things' began as early as 2009 when director Yorgos Lanthimos discussed acquiring the rights with Alasdair Gray. After the success of his film 'The Favourite,' Lanthimos actively pursued the project with Emma Stone starring and co-producing. The film's production involved extensive collaboration and unique challenges, including casting during the COVID-19 pandemic.
Related topics: Yorgos Lanthimos, Alasdair Gray, The Favourite
