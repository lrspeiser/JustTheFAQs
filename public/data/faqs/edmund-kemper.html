FAQs: Edmund Kemper

General Information
Who is Edmund Kemper?
Edmund Emil Kemper III, born on December 18, 1948, in Burbank, California, is a notorious American serial killer. He was convicted of murdering eight individuals, including seven women and one girl, between 1972 and 1973. Kemper's first murders were of his grandparents when he was just 15 years old. He is infamously known as the 'Co-ed Killer' because most of his victims were female college students hitchhiking in Santa Cruz County, California. His murders typically involved necrophilia, decapitation, and dismemberment.
Related topics: Serial killer, Necrophilia, Decapitation, Dismemberment
Early Life and Psychology
What was Kemper's early life like?
Edmund Kemper exhibited troubling behavior from a young age, including cruelty to animals and disturbing fantasies. He experienced a highly dysfunctional family life, particularly with his mother, Clarnell. His father described his relationship with Clarnell as more stressful than wartime experiences. Kemper was notably larger than his peers and suffered from a lack of affection and severe abuse from his mother, which many believe contributed to his later violent behaviors.
Related topics: Burbank, California, Santa Cruz County, California
First Murders and Imprisonment
How did Kemper's first murders unfold?
At age 15, on August 27, 1964, Kemper shot his grandmother multiple times and later killed his grandfather. He claimed the act was part of a desire to understand what it felt like to kill. Diagnosed initially with paranoid schizophrenia, he was placed in Atascadero State Hospital, showing intelligence and manipulative capabilities, learning to befool psychiatric tests during his time there.
Related topics: Paranoid schizophrenia, Atascadero State Hospital
Later Murders
What were the details of Kemper's later murders?
Between May 1972 and April 1973, Kemper killed eight victims, including his mother and her friend. He often preyed on female students who he picked up while driving around Santa Cruz. His methods included shooting, strangling, and then taking the bodies to perform post-mortem acts. Kemper's resentment towards his mother was a significant factor in his subsequent murders.
Related topics: Santa Cruz, California, Serial killers in the United States
Capture and Trial
How was Kemper captured and what happened during his trial?
After murdering his mother and her friend in April 1973, Kemper fled to Colorado. Surprisingly, he voluntarily called the police to confess. During his trial, he pleaded not guilty by reason of insanity, but was found sane and guilty of eight counts of first-degree murder. He received concurrent life sentences due to a moratorium on capital punishment in California at the time.
Related topics: Capital punishment in California, First-degree murder
Imprisonment and Mental Health
What has been Kemper's life in prison like?
Kemper has been incarcerated at the California Medical Facility, where he has been a model prisoner, showing leadership skills and working on various projects, including recording audiobooks for the blind. He admitted to learning and manipulating psychiatric tests. Despite being diagnosed with several personality disorders, Kemper remains forthcoming about his crimes and has participated in interviews to aid studies on criminal psychology.
Related topics: California Medical Facility, Narcissistic personality disorder, Schizotypal personality disorder
Influence and Pop Culture
How has Kemper influenced popular culture?
Kemper's crimes have inspired various works in film and literature. He was one of several serial killers who inspired the character of Buffalo Bill in 'The Silence of the Lambs'. Kemper has been featured in documentaries and dramatizations, including the Netflix series 'Mindhunter', where he is portrayed by actor Cameron Britton. His story continues to be referenced in music and literature, reflecting his impact on pop culture.
Related topics: The Silence of the Lambs (novel), Mindhunter (TV series)
