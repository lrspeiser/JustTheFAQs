FAQs: Queen Elizabeth II

Death and Funeral
How was Queen Elizabeth II's passing and funeral managed?
Queen Elizabeth II passed away on September 8, 2022, at Balmoral Castle, aged 96. Her state funeral was held on September 19 at Westminster Abbey, followed by a private burial at St. George's Chapel, Windsor Castle. Her death marked a watershed moment with global tributes and mourning.
Related topics: Death and state funeral of Elizabeth II, Operation London Bridge
Involvement in World Wars and Defense
What role did Queen Elizabeth II play during World War II?
During World War II, Elizabeth served in the Auxiliary Territorial Service where she trained as a driver and mechanic, becoming the first female member of the Royal Family to serve as a full-time active member of the armed forces.
Related topics: Auxiliary Territorial Service, Second World War
Historic Visits and Diplomacy
Which historic visits did Queen Elizabeth II make during her reign?
Queen Elizabeth II made historic state visits including to China in 1986, Russia in 1994, and the Republic of Ireland in 2011. She also met with five popes and fourteen US presidents, symbolizing her role as a global diplomatic figure.
Related topics: List of state visits made by Elizabeth II, State visit by Elizabeth II to the Republic of Ireland
Early Life and Education
What was notable about Queen Elizabeth II's early life?
Born on April 21, 1926, in Mayfair, London, Elizabeth was the first child of Albert, Duke of York, and Elizabeth, Duchess of York. She was educated privately at home and developed a love for horses and dogs. Her early life was influenced by her grandfather, King George V, and she was affectionately called 'Lilibet' by her family.
Related topics: George VI, Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother
Overall Reign and Influence
How long was Queen Elizabeth II's reign, and what made it significant?
Queen Elizabeth II reigned for 70 years and 214 days, from February 6, 1952, until her death on September 8, 2022, making her the longest-serving British monarch and the second-longest of any sovereign state. Her reign was marked by immense social change and a transition from the British Empire into the Commonwealth of Nations.
Related topics: Commonwealth realm, List of monarchs in Britain by length of reign
Marriage and Family
Who was Queen Elizabeth II married to, and how many children did she have?
Queen Elizabeth II married Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh, on November 20, 1947. Their marriage lasted 73 years until Philip's death in 2021. Elizabeth and Philip had four children: Charles (later King Charles III), Anne, Andrew, and Edward.
Related topics: Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh, Charles III
Public Image and Legacy
How did Queen Elizabeth II maintain her public image over the years?
Queen Elizabeth II was known for her dedication, humility, and sense of duty. She maintained a non-political stance, rarely expressed personal opinions, and became a symbol of stability and continuity. Her approachable image was enhanced by modern measures like the 1969 'Royal Family' documentary and her first royal walkabout in 1970.
Related topics: Personality and image of Elizabeth II, Royal Family (film)
The Commonwealth and Political Changes
How did Queen Elizabeth II impact the Commonwealth during her reign?
Under Queen Elizabeth II's reign, the British Empire evolved into the Commonwealth of Nations. She was Head of the Commonwealth, leading through events such as the decolonization of Africa, the UK's accession and withdrawal from the European Union, and fostering unity among the member states.
Related topics: Commonwealth realm, Decolonisation of Africa
Succession to the Throne
How did Elizabeth II become queen?
Elizabeth became queen upon the death of her father, King George VI, on February 6, 1952. At that time, she was in Kenya and learned of her accession to the throne during her royal tour with Prince Philip.
