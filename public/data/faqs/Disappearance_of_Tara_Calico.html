FAQs: Disappearance of Tara Calico

Mysterious Photograph
What is the significance of the Polaroid photograph in Tara Calico's case?
The Polaroid photograph became a crucial piece of the mystery surrounding Tara Calico's disappearance. Discovered in June 1989 in a parking lot in Port St. Joe, Florida, it shows an unidentified young woman and boy, both gagged and seemingly bound. The woman in the photo was believed by some, including Tara's mother, to resemble Tara. The photo was analyzed by multiple agencies, including Scotland Yard, which believed it was Tara, while Los Alamos National Laboratory disagreed. The FBI's analysis was inconclusive.

The Day of Disappearance
What happened on the day Tara Calico disappeared?
On September 20, 1988, Tara Calico left her home for a daily bike ride on New Mexico State Road 47. She did not return by noon, after which her mother, Patty Doel, went searching for her. During the search, pieces of Tara's Sony Walkman and a cassette tape were discovered along the road, leading to the theory that she may have dropped them to mark her trail. Witnesses saw a light-colored pickup truck following her, but no one saw the actual abduction.

Later Developments
What are the most recent developments in the Tara Calico case?
Recently, law enforcement has renewed efforts to solve Tara Calico's case with a six-person task force established in 2013. In 2019, the FBI announced a reward for information leading to Tara's whereabouts or those responsible for her disappearance. As of June 2023, sufficient evidence was claimed to have been found to submit the investigation to the district attorney for potential charges, although no arrests have been made, and the identities of persons of interest remain sealed by the court.

Photographs and Theories
Have there been any other photographs or theories about Tara Calico's disappearance?
Yes, aside from the initial Polaroid, other photos have surfaced over the years that are believed to picture Tara. In 2009, images of a boy with features similar to the boy in the original Polaroid were mailed anonymously to a police chief in Port St. Joe, but these did not lead to any breakthroughs. Moreover, further photographs found at different times have been considered, but many are unverified or dismissed as hoaxes by family members, maintaining the aura of mystery and speculation surrounding the case.

Background of Tara Calico
Who is Tara Calico and what is known about her disappearance?
Tara Leigh Calico is an American woman who disappeared on September 20, 1988, near her home in Belen, New Mexico. She was 19 years old at the time of her disappearance and is suspected to have been kidnapped. Her disappearance remains one of the most mysterious and high-profile missing person cases, with some believing she was abducted after she went on a bike ride. Despite extensive investigations and media coverage, she has never been found.
