FAQs: Happy New Year (song)

Overview
What is the background of the song "Happy New Year" by ABBA?
"Happy New Year" is a song by the Swedish group ABBA from their 1980 album Super Trouper, with lead vocals by Agnetha Fältskog. Although it was initially released with a limited distribution as a single in December that year, it has gained popularity over time. The song's working title was "Daddy Don't Get Drunk on Christmas Day."
Related topics: ABBA, Super Trouper (album)
Versions and Releases
Are there different versions of "Happy New Year"?
Yes, there is a Spanish-language version called "Felicidad" which was released in Spanish-speaking territories in 1980. It charted in the top 5 in Argentina, and was included on the South American versions of the Super Trouper album. The song was also re-released in English in 1999, 2008, and on various occasions, charting in multiple countries.
Related topics: Felicidad, Argentina, Super Trouper (album), ABBA Oro: Grandes Éxitos
Cultural Impact
Why is "Happy New Year" considered significant?
"Happy New Year" is a recurring hit that finds renewed popularity during the New Year period. It is played globally during this time, especially in countries like Vietnam.
Related topics: Vietnam
Alternate Versions
Was there a Spanish version of 'Happy New Year' released?
Yes, the Spanish-language version of the song, titled 'Felicidad', was released in 1980 in Spanish-speaking territories. It charted in the top 5 in Argentina and was included on the South American versions of the 'Super Trouper' album. It was later included in the expanded re-release of 'ABBA Oro: Grandes Éxitos'.
Related topics: Argentina, Oro: Grandes Éxitos
Certifications
What certifications has "Happy New Year" received?
The song has been certified Gold in Denmark with over 45,000 sales. These figures include both sales and streaming counts based on the certification alone.
Related topics: IFPI Danmark
Special Releases
Were there any special editions of 'Happy New Year' released?
In December 2011, a limited edition silver glitter vinyl single was released, featuring 'Happy New Year' and 'The Way Old Friends Do'. Only 500 copies were made available, and they sold out quickly.
General Information
What is the song 'Happy New Year' by ABBA about?
'Happy New Year' is a song by the Swedish group ABBA, from their 1980 album 'Super Trouper'. It features lead vocals by Agnetha Fältskog and was initially released as a single in December 1980. The song's working title was 'Daddy Don't Get Drunk on Christmas Day'.
Related topics: ABBA, Super Trouper (album), Agnetha Fältskog
Song Details
Who were the songwriters and producers for 'Happy New Year'?
The song 'Happy New Year' was written by Benny Andersson and Björn Ulvaeus, and produced by Andersson and Ulvaeus.
Related topics: Benny Andersson, Björn Ulvaeus
Chart Performance
How did "Happy New Year" perform on the charts over the years?
The English version of "Happy New Year" charted at number 27 in Sweden, number 15 in the Netherlands, and number 75 in Germany when re-released in 1999. It reached number 4 in Sweden and number 6 in Norway in 2008. It has consistently charted on various occasions, particularly around the New Year.
Related topics: Sverigetopplistan, Dutch Single Top 100, GfK Entertainment charts
Certifications
Has 'Happy New Year' received any certifications?
Yes, 'Happy New Year' has been certified Gold in Denmark for selling 45,000 units.
Related topics: IFPI Danmark
A-Teens Version
Did any other artists cover "Happy New Year"?
Yes, the song was covered by the A-Teens and released as a single in 1999 to commemorate the New Millennium. Their version peaked at number 4 on the Swedish charts and was certified Gold shortly after its release.
Related topics: A-Teens, Sverigetopplistan
Release and Reception
How was 'Happy New Year' received in terms of chart performance?
The song was re-released in 1999 for the new millennium and charted in various countries: number 27 in Sweden, 15 in the Netherlands, and 75 in Germany. Later, in 2008, it was released again, reaching number 4 in Sweden and number 6 in Norway, showing its enduring popularity.
A-Teens Cover
Did the A-Teens cover 'Happy New Year'?
Yes, the A-Teens covered 'Happy New Year', releasing it as a single in 1999. They modified the last line of the third verse to "in the end of ninety-nine" to suit the millennium celebration context. Their version reached number 4 on the Swedish charts.
Related topics: A-Teens
