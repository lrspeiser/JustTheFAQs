FAQs: PDC World Darts Championship

Overview
What is the PDC World Darts Championship?
The PDC World Darts Championship is an annual professional darts tournament organized by the Professional Darts Corporation (PDC). It is regarded as one of the sport's most prestigious events. The tournament is typically held from December to January and has been hosted at Alexandra Palace in London since 2008. The event crowns the world champion of darts, with winners receiving the Sid Waddell Trophy.
Related topics: Professional Darts Corporation
History
How did the PDC World Darts Championship originate?
The PDC World Darts Championship originated from a schism in the sport in 1994, when the World Darts Council (WDC) broke away from the British Darts Organisation (BDO). The championship was initially known as the WDC World Darts Championship and was part of efforts by players to have a more professional environment. The WDC later became the PDC following a settlement with the BDO, with players free to choose between the two organizations until the BDO folded in 2020.

Records and Statistics
Who is the most successful player in PDC World Darts Championship history?
Phil Taylor is the most successful player in the history of the PDC World Darts Championship, having won the title 14 times. His dominance in the sport is unmatched, and he also holds the record for the most finals, with 19 appearances.

Records and Statistics
Which countries have produced champions in the PDC World Darts Championship?
England, the Netherlands, Scotland, Canada, and Wales have all produced champions in the PDC World Darts Championship. England leads with 21 titles, the Netherlands and Scotland have 4 each, Canada has 2, and Wales has won once.

Venue
Where is the PDC World Darts Championship held?
The PDC World Darts Championship has been held at Alexandra Palace in London since 2008. Previously, it was hosted at the Circus Tavern in Purfleet, Essex, from 1994 to 2007. Alexandra Palace provides a grand setting with a capacity of 3,200 attendees.
Related topics: Alexandra Palace, Circus Tavern
Media
Who broadcasts the PDC World Darts Championship?
Sky Sports has been the exclusive UK broadcaster of the PDC World Darts Championship since its inception. The event is also covered by various international channels, such as RTL7 in the Netherlands and DAZN in the USA and other countries.
Related topics: Sky Sports
Event Records
What is a notable achievement in the PDC World Darts Championship?
One notable achievement in the PDC World Darts Championship is the highest one-match average of 114.05, set by Michael van Gerwen in a semi-final match in 2017. This stands as a record in the tournament's history for scoring consistency in a single match.

Event Highlights
Has there been a nine-dart finish in the PDC World Darts Championship?
Yes, there have been multiple nine-dart finishes at the PDC World Darts Championship. The first was thrown by Raymond van Barneveld in 2009. Since then, there have been 16 nine-dart finishes, including in world finals by Adrian Lewis in 2011 and Michael Smith in 2023.

Tournament Format
What is the format of the PDC World Darts Championship?
The tournament is played in a set format, where players must win a specified number of sets to advance. Each set consists of a number of legs (usually best of five), and players must win a certain number of legs to secure a set. This format adds an extra level of strategy and endurance to the competition.
