FAQs: Russian invasion of Ukraine

Introduction
When did the Russian invasion of Ukraine begin?
The Russian invasion of Ukraine began on 24 February 2022 as a major escalation of the Russo-Ukrainian War, which had started in 2014. It is considered the largest and deadliest conflict in Europe since World War II.
Related topics: Russo-Ukrainian War, World War II
Prelude
What led to the buildup of tensions before the Russian invasion?
In late 2021, Russia moved troops near Ukraine's borders and issued demands to NATO, including that Ukraine should never join the alliance. Russia denied plans to attack Ukraine until the day before the invasion. President Putin and a close circle of war hawks reportedly made the decision to invade.
Related topics: Prelude to the Russian invasion of Ukraine, NATO
Initial Invasion
How did Russia conduct its initial invasion?
Russia conducted a large-scale attack on multiple fronts: from Belarus towards Kyiv, from the northeast, and through southern and southeastern Ukraine. The invasion included missile strikes and rapid mechanized advances.
Related topics: Battle of Kyiv (2022), Battle of Kharkiv (2022)
Significant Battles
What were some significant battles in the invasion's early stages?
In the early stages of the invasion, significant battles included the Battle of Kyiv, Battle of Sumy, and the Siege of Mariupol. These battles were marked by intense fighting and resistance from Ukrainian forces.
Related topics: Battle of Kyiv (2022), Siege of Mariupol
International Reaction
How did the international community react to the invasion?
The invasion was widely condemned. The United Nations General Assembly passed a resolution demanding a full Russian withdrawal. Many countries imposed sanctions on Russia and provided military and humanitarian aid to Ukraine.
Related topics: Reactions to the Russian invasion of Ukraine, United Nations General Assembly
Humanitarian Impact
What has been the humanitarian impact of the invasion?
By April 2023, more than 8.2 million Ukrainians had fled the country, creating Europe's largest refugee crisis since World War II. Millions were internally displaced within Ukraine.
Related topics: Ukrainian refugee crisis, List of largest refugee crises
Russian Annexations
Which Ukrainian regions were annexed by Russia?
On 30 September 2022, Russia announced the annexation of Ukraine's Donetsk, Luhansk, Kherson, and Zaporizhzhia oblasts. These actions were widely condemned and labeled illegal by Ukraine, the United States, the European Union, and the United Nations.
Related topics: Russian annexation of Donetsk, Kherson, Luhansk and Zaporizhzhia oblasts
