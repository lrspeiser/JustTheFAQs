FAQs: Scott Weiland

General Overview
Who was Scott Weiland?
Scott Weiland, born Scott Richard Kline, was an American singer and songwriter, best known as the lead vocalist of the rock band Stone Temple Pilots and the supergroup Velvet Revolver. He was recognized for his flamboyant stage presence and versatile vocal style.

Early Life
What was Scott Weiland's early life like?
Scott Weiland was born in San Jose, California, and experienced a challenging childhood that included being legally adopted by his stepfather and taking his surname. He moved to Ohio at a young age and suffered a traumatic event when he was raped at 12, a memory he repressed until later in life. After his family moved back to California, he attended Edison High School and Orange Coast College.

Career with Stone Temple Pilots
How did Weiland become part of Stone Temple Pilots?
Weiland joined Stone Temple Pilots initially called Mighty Joe Young, with bandmates including the DeLeo brothers. The band became famous for its albums like 'Core' and 'Purple', and hits such as "Plush", "Vasoline", and "Interstate Love Song". Weiland's extensive issues with substance abuse led to periods of conflict and ultimately his firing in 2013.
Related topics: Stone Temple Pilots, Core (Stone Temple Pilots album), Purple (Stone Temple Pilots album)
Velvet Revolver
What was Scott Weiland's association with Velvet Revolver?
Scott Weiland was the lead singer of Velvet Revolver, a supergroup formed with former members of Guns N' Roses. The band achieved commercial success with albums like 'Contraband', which included hits like "Slither" and "Fall to Pieces". Weiland won a Grammy for Best Hard Rock Performance with the song "Slither".
Related topics: Velvet Revolver, Contraband (Velvet Revolver album)
Substance Abuse and Health
How did substance abuse affect Scott Weiland's life and career?
Scott Weiland struggled with addiction throughout his life, leading to publicized arrests and periods of incarceration. He was diagnosed with bipolar disorder in 2001 and had a history with crack cocaine, heroin, and other substances. Weiland's substance abuse issues significantly impacted his career, including contributing to his dismissals from bands.
Related topics: Bipolar disorder, Crack cocaine
Death and Legacy
How did Scott Weiland die and what was his legacy?
Scott Weiland died of an accidental overdose in 2015 on his tour bus in Minnesota. He left behind a legacy as a prominent figure in '90s rock with his work in Stone Temple Pilots and Velvet Revolver, selling over 50 million records worldwide. His unique vocal style and stage presence remain influential and remembered in the rock music community.
