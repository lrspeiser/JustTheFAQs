FAQs: Super Bowl XXVIII

Overview
What is the significance of Super Bowl XXVIII?
Super Bowl XXVIII was a pivotal American football game played on January 30, 1994, between the Dallas Cowboys, champions of the National Football Conference (NFC), and the Buffalo Bills, champions of the American Football Conference (AFC). The game determined the National Football League (NFL) champion for the 1993 season. The Cowboys defeated the Bills 30-13, securing their fourth Super Bowl victory and tying the Pittsburgh Steelers and San Francisco 49ers for the most Super Bowl wins at the time. This Super Bowl marked the last time two teams faced each other in consecutive finals, with the Bills losing their fourth straight Super Bowl.

Host Selection
How was the host city for Super Bowl XXVIII selected?
Atlanta was chosen as the host city for Super Bowl XXVIII during an NFL owners' meeting on May 23, 1990, in Irving, Texas. The city was selected in part to reward Falcons owner Rankin Smith for keeping the team in Atlanta and orchestrating the construction of the Georgia Dome. Four cities submitted bids, but Atlanta's was preferred due to support from Tampa Bay representatives when Tampa was eliminated in the voting process. Conditions were attached due to ongoing construction of the Georgia Dome, but these were met, allowing Atlanta to host the event.

Buffalo Bills
What was notable about the Buffalo Bills' fourth consecutive Super Bowl appearance in 1993?
The Buffalo Bills' journey to their fourth consecutive Super Bowl appearance was marked by a 12-4 record and securing the best regular-season record in the AFC. Despite their strong offense led by quarterback Jim Kelly and running back Thurman Thomas, who led the AFC in rushing, the Bills struggled defensively, ranking low in the league. Their appearance in Super Bowl XXVIII underscored their inability to secure a championship, as they became the first team to lose four consecutive Super Bowls.

Game Summary
How did the Cowboys defeat the Bills in Super Bowl XXVIII?
The Cowboys triumphed over the Bills with a strategic second half, overcoming a 13-6 halftime deficit to score 24 unanswered points. The turning point came early in the third quarter when Cowboys safety James Washington returned a fumble for a touchdown. Running back Emmitt Smith was the standout performer, rushing for 132 yards and scoring two touchdowns, earning him the Super Bowl MVP award. The Bills struggled with turnovers, which proved costly in the game's outcome.

Entertainment
Who performed during the halftime show of Super Bowl XXVIII?
The halftime show of Super Bowl XXVIII was titled 'Rockin' Country Sunday' and featured performances by country music stars Clint Black, Tanya Tucker, Travis Tritt, and Wynonna Judd. The finale included a special appearance by Naomi Judd to perform 'Love Can Build a Bridge' with Wynonna and other artists as participants joined in, marking a vibrant country music celebration during the event.

Broadcasting
How was Super Bowl XXVIII broadcasted and who were the main commentators?
Super Bowl XXVIII was broadcasted in the United States by NBC with play-by-play announcer Dick Enberg and color commentator Bob Trumpy. Sideline reports were provided by O. J. Simpson and Will McDonough. Jim Lampley hosted the events with analysts Mike Ditka and Joe Gibbs. Notably, this was the last appearance of O. J. Simpson on 'NFL on NBC' before his significant legal issues later in 1994.
