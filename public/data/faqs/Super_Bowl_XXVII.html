FAQs: Super Bowl XXVII

Overview
What was significant about Super Bowl XXVII?
Super Bowl XXVII was a pivotal American football championship game played to decide the NFL champion for the 1992 season. The game was held on January 31, 1993, at the Rose Bowl in Pasadena, California. The Dallas Cowboys decisively defeated the Buffalo Bills 52–17. This victory marked the Cowboys' third Super Bowl win and their first in 15 years. The game is tied for the fourth-highest scoring Super Bowl, with a total of 69 points. Buffalo's loss made them the first team to lose three consecutive Super Bowls.
Related topics: Super Bowl XXVIII, Super Bowl XXXVII
Teams and Players
Who were the key players in Super Bowl XXVII?
Key players for the Cowboys included quarterback Troy Aikman, who was named the game MVP for his performance. Aikman completed 22 of 30 passes for 273 yards and four touchdowns, with a passer rating of 140.6. For the Bills, backup quarterback Frank Reich replaced injured starting quarterback Jim Kelly and threw a memorably long touchdown pass. Buffalo's star running back, Thurman Thomas, was held to just 29 yards, a critical setback in their game plan.
Related topics: Troy Aikman, Jim Kelly, Frank Reich, Thurman Thomas
Venue
Where was Super Bowl XXVII held and why was the venue significant?
Super Bowl XXVII was held at the Rose Bowl in Pasadena, California. This venue was significant as it was the last NFL championship game to date to be played in a non-NFL stadium. It was the seventh Super Bowl held in the Greater Los Angeles Area, which would not host another until Super Bowl LVI in 2022.

Cultural and Historical Context
What controversy surrounded the location of Super Bowl XXVII?
Originally scheduled to be held at the Sun Devil Stadium in Tempe, Arizona, Super Bowl XXVII was moved to the Rose Bowl in California due to a controversy over Arizona's refusal to recognize Martin Luther King Jr. Day as a holiday. This decision was influenced by widespread boycotts and a vote to relocate if the MLK holiday was not established.
Related topics: Martin Luther King Jr. Day, Sun Devil Stadium, Rose Bowl (stadium)
Game Highlights
What were some key moments from the game?
A key moment in Super Bowl XXVII was when Dallas Cowboys' Jimmie Jones recovered a fumble and scored a touchdown, one of many turnovers that led to the Cowboys' significant lead. The Cowboys scored 35 points off of nine Buffalo turnovers. Another memorable moment was a play where Cowboys' Leon Lett fumbled just before scoring, knocked away by Bills wide receiver Don Beebe, highlighting Lett's eagerness and Beebe's determination.
Related topics: Leon Lett, Don Beebe
Event Location
Why was the 1993 Super Bowl held at the Rose Bowl?
Super Bowl XXVII was hosted at the Rose Bowl due to a significant controversy and subsequent decision by the NFL. Originally planned to be held at Sun Devil Stadium in Tempe, Arizona, the game was moved because of the state of Arizona's failure to recognize Martin Luther King Jr. Day as a holiday. The NFL had warned Arizona they would move the Super Bowl if the holiday issue wasn't resolved. After reopening the bidding, it was ultimately decided to host the Super Bowl at the Rose Bowl.

Broadcasting and Entertainment
How was the Super Bowl XXVII halftime show different?
Super Bowl XXVII featured a groundbreaking halftime show headlined by Michael Jackson. His performance is credited with shifting the Super Bowl halftime shows towards big-name musical acts. The production was hugely successful and marked an increase in viewership during the halftime show for the first time in Super Bowl history, with 133.4 million people tuning in. Jackson performed hits including "Jam", "Billie Jean", and "Black or White".

Players and Coaches
Who were the key players and coaches in Super Bowl XXVII?
The Dallas Cowboys were coached by Jimmy Johnson, and their key player, quarterback Troy Aikman, was named the Super Bowl MVP for his performance. For the Buffalo Bills, Marv Levy was the head coach. The Cowboys' team included stars like Emmitt Smith and Michael Irvin, while the Bills were powered by players such as Jim Kelly and Andre Reed.
Related topics: Troy Aikman, Emmitt Smith, Michael Irvin, Jim Kelly, Andre Reed
Teams
Which teams played in Super Bowl XXVII and what were their records going in?
Super Bowl XXVII featured the AFC champion Buffalo Bills and the NFC champion Dallas Cowboys. The Cowboys, with a 13–3 record, were making their sixth Super Bowl appearance. The Bills entered the game with an 11–5 record and were advancing to their third consecutive Super Bowl.

Game Highlights
What were the standout moments from the game itself?
One of the memorable highlights from Super Bowl XXVII was the Cowboys' defense creating a Super Bowl-record nine turnovers from the Bills, resulting in multiple touchdowns. A notable play featured Cowboys’ defensive end Leon Lett recovering a fumble and running towards the end zone, only to be stripped of the ball by Bills' Don Beebe as he slowed to celebrate before crossing the goal line. This mistake resulted in a touchback, providing a small moral victory for the determined Bills' player.

Introduction
What was significant about Super Bowl XXVII?
Super Bowl XXVII, played on January 31, 1993, was notable for the Dallas Cowboys' dominant victory over the Buffalo Bills with a score of 52–17. This game was tied as the fourth-highest scoring Super Bowl with 69 combined points. It was significant as the Cowboys won their third Super Bowl in team history and the Bills became the first team to lose three consecutive Super Bowls. The game was played at the Rose Bowl in Pasadena, California. The Bills made history by being one of the few teams to participate in three consecutive Super Bowls, although they lost all three.

Halftime Show
Who performed at the Super Bowl XXVII halftime show and why was it noteworthy?
Michael Jackson headlined the Super Bowl XXVII halftime show, making it memorable because he enhanced the entertainment value of the Super Bowl halftime, attracting record viewership. His performance featured hits like "Jam," "Billie Jean," and "Black or White," and involved a call for charity, marking a shift in the Super Bowl's approach to halftime shows to boost ratings.

Broadcasting
Which network broadcasted Super Bowl XXVII and who were the announcers?
NBC broadcasted Super Bowl XXVII, with Dick Enberg serving as the play-by-play announcer and Bob Trumpy as the color commentator. The coverage included numerous features and interviews, marking NBC's significant broadcast event for the year.
Controversies and Challenges
What was the controversy surrounding Arizona and the Martin Luther King Jr. Day?
The controversy began when Arizona refused to recognize Martin Luther King Jr. Day as a state holiday. Initially established by Governor Bruce Babbitt through an executive order, it was rescinded by his successor, Evan Mecham. This decision led to significant nationwide backlash and pressure from civil rights activists, as well as an entertainment and convention boycott of Arizona led by figures like Coretta Scott King and Stevie Wonder. The controversy had broader implications on Arizona's sporting events, including the Super Bowl.
Related topics: Evan Mecham, Coretta Scott King, Stevie Wonder
