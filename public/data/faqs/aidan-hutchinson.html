FAQs: Aidan Hutchinson

Professional Career
What were the highlights of Aidan Hutchinson's rookie season in the NFL?
In his rookie season with the Detroit Lions, Aidan Hutchinson set a team rookie record with three sacks in a single game against the Washington Commanders. He finished the 2022 season with 9.5 sacks, 52 total tackles, and three interceptions, earning a spot on the Pro Football Writers of America All-Rookie Team. His achievements contributed to winning the Pepsi NFL Rookie of the Year award.

College Career
How did Aidan Hutchinson perform in college?
During his time at the University of Michigan, Aidan Hutchinson was a standout player. As a freshman in 2018, he played in all 13 games, making 15 tackles. His sophomore year in 2019 saw him start all 13 games and achieve 68 tackles and 4.5 sacks. In 2020, an injury limited him to three games. As a senior in 2021, Hutchinson set a Michigan record with 14 sacks, also earning several awards like the Ted Hendricks Award and the Lombardi Award. He was a finalist for the Heisman Trophy, notable for being the only defensive player amongst finalists that year.

Injuries and Comebacks
What injury did Aidan Hutchinson sustain in 2024?
In 2024, Aidan Hutchinson suffered a significant leg injury against the Dallas Cowboys, fracturing both his tibia and fibula during a game. This injury required surgery and posed a recovery period of four to six months. Prior to the injury, he was leading the league with 7.5 sacks early in the season.

General Overview
Who is Aidan Hutchinson?
Aidan Hutchinson is an American professional football player born on August 9, 2000, in Plymouth, Michigan. He plays as a defensive end for the Detroit Lions in the National Football League (NFL). Hutchinson gained recognition as a unanimous All-American for the Michigan Wolverines and was a runner-up for the Heisman Trophy in 2021. He was selected second overall in the 2022 NFL Draft by the Detroit Lions.

Early Life
What was Aidan Hutchinson's high school career like?
Aidan Hutchinson attended Divine Child High School in Dearborn, Michigan, where he played various positions, including defensive end, tight end, offensive line, and long snapper. He was noted for his performance in the 2018 U.S. Army All-American Game, where he recorded two sacks, and committed to playing college football at the University of Michigan.
