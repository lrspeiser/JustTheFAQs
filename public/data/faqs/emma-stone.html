FAQs: Emma Stone

Early Life
Where was Emma Stone born and what was her early life like?
Emma Stone was born on November 6, 1988, in Scottsdale, Arizona. She grew up living at the Camelback Inn resort and has a younger brother named Spencer. Stone is of Swedish, German, English, Scottish, and Irish ancestry.
Related topics: Scottsdale, Arizona, Camelback Inn
Career Beginnings
How did Emma Stone start her acting career?
Emma Stone began acting in a theater production of 'The Wind in the Willows' at age 11. She moved to Los Angeles with her family in 2004 and made her television debut on the reality show 'In Search of the New Partridge Family.'
Related topics: The Wind in the Willows, The New Partridge Family
Breakthrough and Success
What were Emma Stone's breakout roles that brought her mainstream recognition?
Emma Stone's breakout roles included her appearances in 'Superbad' (2007), 'Zombieland' (2009), and 'Easy A' (2010). 'Easy A' was her first time in a leading role, earning her a Golden Globe nomination and winning an MTV Movie Award for Best Comedic Performance.
Related topics: Superbad, Zombieland, Easy A
Accolades and Achievements
What are some of Emma Stone's notable awards and recognitions?
Emma Stone has received numerous accolades, including two Academy Awards, two British Academy Film Awards, and two Golden Globe Awards. She was also the world's highest-paid actress in 2017 and was named one of Time's 100 most influential people in the world.
Related topics: Academy Awards, British Academy Film Awards, Golden Globe Awards, List of highest-paid film actors, Time 100
Personal Life
Who is Emma Stone married to and do they have any children?
Emma Stone is married to Dave McCary, a segment director for 'Saturday Night Live.' They were married in September 2020 and have a daughter named Louise Jean, born in March 2021.
Related topics: Dave McCary
Philanthropy
What philanthropic efforts is Emma Stone involved in?
Emma Stone has participated in various philanthropic activities. She has been involved in campaigns to promote breast cancer awareness, supported the World Wide Fund for Nature's Earth Hour, and co-launched the Time's Up initiative against sexual harassment.
Related topics: Time's Up (organization)
