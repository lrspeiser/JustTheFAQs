FAQs: Ferrari (2023 Film)

Production and Development
How did the film 'Ferrari' come to be made?
Michael Mann had been exploring the idea for 'Ferrari' since around 2000, with various actors like Christian Bale and Hugh Jackman attached over the years. Initially, the project stalled until Adam Driver was cast as Enzo Ferrari in 2022. The film faced numerous budget and production challenges before STX Entertainment and other international partners helped bring it to fruition.
Related topics: Michael Mann, Christian Bale, Hugh Jackman, STX Entertainment
Plot Details
What are the key plot points of 'Ferrari'?
The film explores Enzo Ferrari's preparation for the Mille Miglia race in 1957 amidst personal challenges, such as the loss of his son Dino and managing relationships with his estranged wife Laura and mistress Lina Lardi. Tensions arise over granting his illegitimate child, Piero, the Ferrari name, while financial troubles loom over the company.
Related topics: Enzo Ferrari, Mille Miglia, Dino Ferrari
Critical Responses
What were the critical opinions on Adam Driver's performance in 'Ferrari'?
Critical opinions on Adam Driver's performance in 'Ferrari' were mixed. While some critics praised his portrayal of Enzo Ferrari, others felt his performance lacked emotional depth. Damon Wise from Deadline Hollywood described Driver's performance as "strangely unemotional," while other reviews acknowledged his effort in capturing Ferrari's relentlessness and ambition.
Related topics: Adam Driver, Damon Wise, Deadline Hollywood
Overview
What is the 2023 film 'Ferrari' about?
'Ferrari' is a 2023 American biographical sports drama film directed by Michael Mann and based on the 1991 biography 'Enzo Ferrari: The Man, the Cars, the Races, the Machine' by Brock Yates. The film depicts the personal and professional struggles of Enzo Ferrari, the founder of the car manufacturer Ferrari, during the summer of 1957 as Scuderia Ferrari prepares to compete in the 1957 Mille Miglia race.
Related topics: Ferrari, Enzo Ferrari, Mille Miglia, Biographical film
Plot Summary
What is the plot of 'Ferrari'?
'Ferrari' depicts the personal and professional challenges faced by Enzo Ferrari, the founder of Ferrari, during the summer of 1957. The film covers his preparation for the Mille Miglia race amidst financial troubles, familial grief over the death of his son Dino, and marital challenges with Laura Ferrari. It culminates in a tragic race accident that impacts his legacy and personal life.
Related topics: Enzo Ferrari, Mille Miglia, Dino Ferrari

Who stars in 'Ferrari' and who do they portray?

Release and Reception
When was 'Ferrari' released, and how was it received?
'Ferrari' premiered at the 80th Venice International Film Festival on August 31, 2023, and was theatrically released in the United States on December 25, 2023. The film received generally positive reviews and was named one of the top 10 films of 2023 by the National Board of Review. Despite its critical acclaim, it had a moderate box office performance, grossing $43.3 million worldwide against a $95 million budget.
Related topics: 80th Venice International Film Festival, National Board of Review
Production
How was the film 'Ferrari' developed?
Director Michael Mann began developing the 'Ferrari' film around 2000. He collaborated with writer Troy Kennedy Martin, who passed away in 2009. The project went through various casting and distribution changes over the years, with actors like Christian Bale and Hugh Jackman previously attached. The film finally went into production with Adam Driver in the lead role, and filming began in 2022.
Related topics: Christian Bale, Hugh Jackman, Troy Kennedy Martin, Michael Mann

Filming
Where was 'Ferrari' filmed, and what challenges did the production face?
Filming took place in various Italian locations, including Modena and Brescia, starting in August 2022. A significant production challenge involved recreating the historic Mille Miglia race and details like the worn cat's eye that caused a fatal crash were rendered using 3D-printed props.
Related topics: Modena, Brescia, Mille Miglia, 3D printing
