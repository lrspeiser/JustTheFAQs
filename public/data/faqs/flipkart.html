FAQs: Flipkart

General Information
What is Flipkart?
Flipkart Private Limited is an Indian e-commerce company, headquartered in Bangalore, India, and legally domiciled in Singapore. Initially, it focused on online book sales before expanding into other product categories like consumer electronics, fashion, home essentials, groceries, and lifestyle products. It is one of India's leading e-commerce platforms, competing with Amazon and Snapdeal in the Indian market.

History
When was Flipkart founded and who were its founders?
Flipkart was founded in October 2007 by Sachin Bansal and Binny Bansal, both alumni of IIT Delhi and former employees of Amazon. The company started its operations from a two-bedroom apartment in Bangalore.

History
How did Flipkart expand its services beyond books?
Flipkart expanded its services beyond book sales by acquiring companies and diversifying its product offerings. One of its early acquisitions was WeRead in 2010, which boosted its position in the digital retail of books. It then moved into other segments like consumer electronics and fashion, following aggressive marketing and customer service campaigns.

Business Developments
What are some significant acquisitions made by Flipkart?
Flipkart has made several significant acquisitions, including Myntra for online fashion retail and Jabong.com, bolstering its dominance in the apparel segment. It acquired eBay.in, PhonePe, Cleartrip, and other companies to expand into various segments like travel and digital payments.

What are the financial highlights for Flipkart in recent years?
As of 2024, Flipkart reported a revenue of ₹70,541 crore (US$8.3 billion) but had a net loss of ₹−4,248 crore (US$−500 million). The company has seen substantial investments, including Google investing $350 million in 2024.

Ownership
What role does Walmart play in Flipkart's ownership?
Walmart acquired a 77% controlling stake in Flipkart for US$16 billion in 2018, and has since increased its ownership to approximately 85%. This acquisition was part of Walmart's strategic move to compete in the global e-commerce market.

History
What was Flipkart's 'Big Billion Days' event and how did it impact the company?
'Big Billion Days' is an annual sales event launched by Flipkart to boost its sales and market share, modeled similarly to Black Friday sales. During this event in 2014, the company achieved a gross merchandise volume of US$300 million, witnessing significant sales in fashion and mobile phones.

Technological Innovations
How is Flipkart innovating in the technology and AI space?
Flipkart is leveraging artificial intelligence and integrating video content to enhance online shopping experiences. It launched an AI-powered shopping assistant called 'Flippi' and an initiative called 'Vibes' that integrates video content with shopping to mimic a 'window-shopping' experience.

Criticism
Has Flipkart faced criticism or controversies?
Yes, Flipkart has faced criticism, particularly concerning its competitive practices and business model. Issues have included complaints from vendors regarding unfair policies, and controversies like the backlash from participating in the Airtel Zero program, which users argued was against net neutrality principles.

Consumer Affairs
Has Flipkart faced any challenges with product deliveries?
Flipkart has encountered problems with product deliveries, where customers reported receiving incorrect items, such as bricks or soap bars instead of the ordered electronic devices. These incidents have been part of larger scams targeting the company and its customers.
