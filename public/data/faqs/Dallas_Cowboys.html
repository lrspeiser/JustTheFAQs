FAQs: Dallas Cowboys

Notable Rivalries
Who are the Dallas Cowboys' biggest rivals?
The Dallas Cowboys have fierce rivalries with several teams, most notably in the NFC East division. Their biggest rivals include the Philadelphia Eagles, New York Giants, and Washington Commanders. The Cowboys also have significant rivalries with the San Francisco 49ers and Pittsburgh Steelers, highlighted by numerous playoff contests.
Related topics: Cowboys–Eagles rivalry, Cowboys–Giants rivalry, Commanders–Cowboys rivalry
Stadiums
What is the current home stadium of the Dallas Cowboys?
The Dallas Cowboys currently play their home games at AT&T Stadium in Arlington, Texas. Opened in 2009, this state-of-the-art stadium is known for its retractable roof and giant high-definition video screens, which were once the largest in the world.
Related topics: AT&T Stadium
Playoff and Championship History
How successful have the Dallas Cowboys been in the playoffs?
The Dallas Cowboys have been highly successful in the NFL playoffs, with 36 playoff appearances and five Super Bowl victories (1971, 1977, 1992, 1993, 1995). They have reached the Super Bowl eight times, tied with the Denver Broncos and San Francisco 49ers for the second-most appearances, behind the New England Patriots.

Personnel and Team Management
Who owns the Dallas Cowboys?
Jerry Jones owns the Dallas Cowboys. He purchased the franchise in 1989 and has been the team's owner, president, and general manager since then. Under his leadership, the Cowboys won three Super Bowls in the 1990s.
Related topics: Jerry Jones
Team Origins
How did the Dallas Cowboys originate?
The Dallas Cowboys were established in 1960 as an expansion team. Before their inception, there was no NFL team south of Washington, D.C. Oilman Clint Murchison Jr. was pivotal in bringing an NFL team to Dallas, overcoming opposition from other franchise owners, particularly George Preston Marshall of the Washington Redskins, through strategic negotiations.
Related topics: Washington Commanders, Clint Murchison Jr., Expansion team
Cultural Significance
Why are the Dallas Cowboys known as "America's Team"?
The nickname "America's Team" is derived from the Cowboys' broad fan base and national following, characterized by a strong presence at road games and numerous consecutive sell-out games. This label was popularized by NFL Films in the late 1970s, and it has stuck due to their success and branding.
Related topics: America's Team
Team Culture and Identity
What is unique about the Dallas Cowboys' uniforms and logos?
The Dallas Cowboys' uniforms are iconic in the NFL, featuring a blue star logo representing Texas as "The Lone Star State." They traditionally wear white jerseys at home, a notable departure from the typical practice of wearing colored jerseys at home. Their helmet features a metallic silver-blue tint with the iconic star, making it one of the most recognized logos in sports.
History and Achievements
What are some notable achievements of the Dallas Cowboys?
The Dallas Cowboys have a rich history marked by multiple achievements. They have won five Super Bowl championships, tying them with the San Francisco 49ers for the second most by an NFL team. They have also made it to the Super Bowl eight times and recorded 20 straight winning seasons from 1966 to 1985.
Introduction
Who are the Dallas Cowboys?
The Dallas Cowboys are a professional American football team based in the Dallas–Fort Worth metroplex. They compete in the National Football League (NFL) as a member club of the league's National Football Conference (NFC) East division. Known as "America's Team," they are one of the most recognized and valuable sports franchises in the world.
