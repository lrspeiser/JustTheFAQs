FAQs: Parvati Shallow

General Information
Who is Parvati Shallow?
Parvati Shallow is an American television personality born on September 21, 1982, in Vero Beach, Florida. She is best known for her appearances on the reality TV show 'Survivor,' participating in four seasons, and winning 'Survivor: Micronesia.' Beyond 'Survivor,' she is a yoga teacher, internet talk show host, and a charity organizer.

Survivor Journey
What is Parvati Shallow's legacy in 'Survivor'?
Parvati Shallow is celebrated as one of the greatest contestants in 'Survivor' history. She is particularly remembered for her strategic gameplay, including her trademark use of flirtation to advance in the game, earning her the nickname 'Black Widow.' She won 'Survivor: Micronesia' and was the runner-up in 'Survivor: Heroes vs. Villains.' Parvati has been inducted into the 'Survivor' Hall of Fame and was named the show's best winner by host Jeff Probst.

Personal Life
What kind of upbringing did Parvati Shallow have?
Parvati Shallow grew up in a 'hippie' commune that practiced Hinduism, which is reflected in her name, Parvati, a Hindu goddess. Her family moved from Vero Beach, Florida, to Marietta, Georgia, when she was 11 years old. Shallow attended Sprayberry High School and the University of Georgia, where she obtained a Bachelor of Arts degree in Journalism.

Television and Other Ventures
How has Parvati Shallow expanded her career beyond 'Survivor'?
Apart from her success on 'Survivor,' Parvati Shallow has ventured into various roles including hosting the internet series 'Around the World For Free' and making an appearance in the movie 'Into the Blue 2: The Reef.' She is also known for her role as a charity organizer and for her online life coaching course 'How Villains are Made.'

Personal Life
What is known about Parvati Shallow's personal relationships?
Parvati Shallow was married to fellow 'Survivor' contestant John Fincher from 2017 to 2021, with whom she has a daughter. As of December 2023, Parvati has publicly come out as queer and announced her relationship with nonbinary comedian and writer Mae Martin.
