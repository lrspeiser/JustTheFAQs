FAQs: Dallas Cowboys Ring of Honor

History and Significance
Who was the first inductee into the Dallas Cowboys Ring of Honor?
Bob Lilly was the first inductee into the Ring of Honor. His induction took place on November 23, 1975, during a special event designated as Bob Lilly Day in Dallas. Lilly's name and jersey number (74) were unveiled during a halftime ceremony at Texas Stadium. He was chosen for his outstanding contributions after 14 years with the team, having retired earlier that year.
Related topics: Bob Lilly, Dallas, Texas
Induction Process
Who is responsible for choosing the inductees for the Ring of Honor?
Initially, inductees were chosen by Tex Schramm, former president and general manager. After him, Jerry Jones, the owner of the Dallas Cowboys, took over the responsibility, continuing the tradition of honoring significant contributors.
Related topics: Tex Schramm, Jerry Jones
Overview
What is the Dallas Cowboys Ring of Honor?
The Dallas Cowboys Ring of Honor is a commemorative installation formerly located at Texas Stadium and now at AT&T Stadium in Arlington, Texas. It honors former players, coaches, and club officials who have made significant contributions to the Dallas Cowboys football team. It was described by Jerry Jones in 1993 as representing the men who built the franchise and additionally reference 'America's Team.'

Induction Processes and Notable Members
How has the selection for the Ring of Honor evolved over time?
Initially, Dallas Cowboys' former president and general manager, Tex Schramm, selected inductees for the RoH. After his tenure, the responsibility passed to the team's owner, Jerry Jones. A high value was placed on the character of the inductees, though some selections, like Michael Irvin's, stirred controversy due to his personal issues.
Related topics: Tex Schramm, Jerry Jones, Michael Irvin
History and Significance
Who created the Dallas Cowboys Ring of Honor and when was it established?
The Ring of Honor was established by Tex Schramm on November 23, 1975, a day designated as Bob Lilly Day in Dallas. Bob Lilly was the first inductee, having retired from the NFL just a few months earlier, marking the start of this honored tradition.

Who are 'The Triplets' that were inducted into the Ring of Honor in 2005?
'The Triplets,' inducted during a Monday Night Football ceremony in 2005, refers to Troy Aikman, Emmitt Smith, and Michael Irvin. These players were pivotal in the Cowboys' success during the 1990s, leading the team to three Super Bowl victories.

Overview
What is the Dallas Cowboys Ring of Honor?
The Dallas Cowboys Ring of Honor (RoH) is a ceremonial ring that recognizes former players, coaches, and team officials who have made significant contributions to the Dallas Cowboys football organization. Initially located at Texas Stadium in Irving, Texas, the Ring is now at AT&T Stadium in Arlington, Texas. It embodies the legacy and excellence of those who helped build the Cowboys into "America's Team."
Related topics: Texas Stadium, Irving, Texas, Arlington, Texas, Dallas Cowboys
Notable Inductees
Who were the first inductees into the Ring of Honor, and what notable players have been added?
Bob Lilly was the first inductee in 1975. Other notable inductees include 'The Triplets' - Troy Aikman, Emmitt Smith, and Michael Irvin - who were inducted simultaneously in 2005, and coach Tom Landry in 1993. The ring continues to include key figures from the Cowboys' successful history.
Related topics: Bob Lilly, Troy Aikman, Emmitt Smith, Michael Irvin, Tom Landry
Controversies and Challenges
Have there been any controversies regarding inductees into the Ring of Honor?
Yes, the inclusion of Michael Irvin was controversial due to his legal issues despite his career success. However, the value placed on character by Tex Schramm set high standards for inductees.
Related topics: Michael Irvin, Tex Schramm

Historical Context
What is the significance of the Dallas Cowboys Ring of Honor's location change?
Initially located at Texas Stadium, the Dallas Cowboys Ring of Honor was moved to AT&T Stadium when the new stadium was established. This relocation underpins the ongoing legacy and enduring recognition of excellence within the Cowboys organization.
