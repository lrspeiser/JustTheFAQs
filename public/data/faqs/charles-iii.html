FAQs: Charles III

Accession to the throne
When did Charles III become King of the United Kingdom?
Charles III became King of the United Kingdom on 8 September 2022 following the death of his mother, Queen Elizabeth II. At 73, he became the oldest person to ascend to the British throne, surpassing William IV, who became king at the age of 64 in 1830.

Early Life
Where was Charles III born and who are his parents?
Charles III was born as Prince Charles of Edinburgh on 14 November 1948 at Buckingham Palace, London, England. He is the eldest son of Queen Elizabeth II and Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh. He was christened by the Archbishop of Canterbury in the Music Room of Buckingham Palace.

Education and Military Career
What was Charles III's education and military service background?
Charles attended prestigious schools like Hill House School, Cheam School, and Gordonstoun, following in the footsteps of his father, Prince Philip. He later attended Trinity College, Cambridge, where he earned a BA degree in history. Charles also spent time at the University College of Wales studying Welsh. He served in both the Royal Air Force and the Royal Navy from 1971 to 1976.

Personal Life
What are some notable aspects of Charles III's personal life and relationships?
Charles married Lady Diana Spencer in 1981 and they had two sons, William and Harry. Charles and Diana divorced in 1996 after a highly publicized marriage beset by infidelity. Diana died in 1997 in a car crash. Charles later married Camilla Parker Bowles in 2005. He is known for his love of gardening, organic farming, and championing various charitable causes.

Reign
What significant actions and events have marked Charles III's reign?
Charles III's reign has seen his coronation in 2023 and a notable shift in financial policies regarding the monarchy, such as requesting profits from Britain's offshore windfarms to be used for public good. His reign has also focused on fostering a diverse representation and working to promote Commonwealth unity.

Public Life and Environmental Advocacy
How has Charles III been involved in environmental and charitable work?
Charles III is a long-time environmental advocate, promoting organic farming and sustainability through initiatives like his Sustainable Markets Initiative launched at the World Economic Forum. He also founded the Prince's Trust in 1976 and has been involved in numerous charitable activities, supporting over 800 organizations.

Public Perception and Media
What has been the public and media perception of Charles III?
Public perception of Charles has varied over time, often influenced by his relationships, particularly with Diana and Camilla. Initially seen as less charismatic than his late first wife, Diana, Charles has been more positively received in light of his environmental advocacy and dedication to various charitable causes. After his accession to the throne, his approval ratings increased.

What are some key aspects of Charles III's health challenges?
Charles III has undergone various health treatments over the years, including keyhole surgery on his knee and surgery for a non-cancerous growth on his nose. In 2024, he announced undergoing treatment after a cancer diagnosis, which led to a temporary pause in public engagements, although he expressed positivity regarding his recovery.

Titles and Arms
What titles and honors has Charles III held throughout his life?
Charles III has held many titles, including Prince of Wales, Duke of Cornwall, and Duke of Rothesay. Upon his accession to the throne, he inherited titles such as King of the United Kingdom and Defender of the Faith. He has also received numerous honours and awards from across the world, including military ranks in the British Armed Forces.
