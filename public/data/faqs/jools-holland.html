FAQs: Jools Holland

Early life and education
What was Jools Holland's early musical talent?
Jools Holland exhibited an exceptional musical talent from a young age. By the age of eight, he could play the piano fluently by ear. During his early teens, he performed regularly in pubs across southeast London and the East End Docks.

Career
How did Jools Holland start his music career?
Jools Holland began his music career as a session musician. His first studio session was with Wayne County & the Electric Chairs in 1976. He was also a founding member of the British pop band Squeeze, which was formed in 1974.
Related topics: Session musician, Wayne County & the Electric Chairs, Squeeze (band)
Career
What is 'Later... with Jools Holland'?
'Later... with Jools Holland' is a music program presented by Jools Holland since 1992. It features a variety of musical performances and has become a staple of British music television. The show is known for its eclectic mix of artists and live performances.

Television and Radio
What other television shows has Jools Holland been involved in?
Beyond his famous show 'Later... with Jools Holland,' Jools Holland co-presented 'The Tube' from 1982 to 1987 and hosted 'Jools' Annual Hootenanny.' He also appeared on shows such as 'The Name That Tune' and 'Celebrity Gogglebox.'

Discography
What is notable about Jools Holland's discography?
Jools Holland's discography features solo efforts, and collaborations. He achieved his first UK number one album in 2024 with 'Swing Fever,' a collaboration with Rod Stewart. His works span various musical styles, including boogie-woogie, jazz, and blues.
