FAQs: Zulu (1964 film)

Historical Accuracy
How historically accurate is the film 'Zulu'?
While 'Zulu' captures the general events of the Battle of Rorke's Drift accurately, it takes liberties with certain character portrayals and events for dramatic purposes. Some British soldiers' characters are fictionalized, and the film presents a mythologized version of the regiment. A factual mistake is portraying the regiment as predominantly Welsh, although it was mostly composed of English soldiers at the time. The film's portrayal of other historical figures and interactions is also dramatized for intensity and impact.

Historical Accuracy
How accurate is 'Zulu' in terms of historical events?
While the film accurately depicts the defense of Rorke's Drift, certain historical details were altered for dramatic effect. For example, some characters' portrayals and regimental affiliations were fictionalized. The film consulted Zulu oral traditions but also introduced fictional events.
Related topics: Rorke's Drift, British Army, Zulu Kingdom
General Information
What is the film 'Zulu' about?
'Zulu' is a 1964 British epic adventure war film that depicts the historical 1879 Battle of Rorke's Drift, which occurred during the Anglo-Zulu War. The story follows a small British Army force defending an outpost against a larger Zulu army. Renowned for its remarkable sets, soundtrack, and action sequences, the film is highly praised for bringing the events of this historic conflict to life.
Related topics: Battle of Rorke's Drift, Anglo-Zulu War, British Army, Zulu Kingdom
Production Insights
Where was 'Zulu' filmed and what can you tell us about its production?
Most of the film was shot on location in South Africa, notably in the Drakensberg Mountains. The film set, depicting the mission station at Rorke's Drift, was recreated to match the historical landscape more dramatically. The production overcame challenges such as casting a significant number of real Zulu extras and dealing with apartheid laws. Directed by Cy Endfield and produced by Stanley Baker, 'Zulu' was shot using the Super Technirama 70 cinematographic process.
Related topics: Battle of Rorke's Drift, Drakensberg, Super Technirama 70

Presentation and Legacy
What is notable about 'Zulu's' presentation format?
'Zulu' was filmed in Super Technirama 70, intended for 70mm presentation. However, due to UK film quota regulations, it was often shown in 35mm format to qualify as a British film. The 70mm print was used mainly for a press show.
Related topics: Super Technirama 70, British films
Production and Cast
Who directed and starred in the film 'Zulu'?
The film was directed by Cy Endfield and features Stanley Baker, Michael Caine (in his first major role), Jack Hawkins, Ulla Jacobsson, and James Booth. Richard Burton provided the narration.
Related topics: Cy Endfield, Stanley Baker, Michael Caine, Richard Burton
Cast and Characters
Who were the main actors in 'Zulu'?
The film stars Stanley Baker and introduced Michael Caine in his first major role. The supporting cast includes Jack Hawkins, Ulla Jacobsson, James Booth, Nigel Green, and Richard Burton (as the narrator). Michael Caine's role as Lieutenant Gonville Bromhead was significant in propelling his career. The film features Chief Mangosuthu Buthelezi, a future South African political leader, portraying his own great-grandfather, Zulu King Cetshwayo kaMpande.
Related topics: Stanley Baker, Michael Caine, Jack Hawkins, Richard Burton
Awards and Honors
Did 'Zulu' receive any awards or recognitions?
Ernest Archer was nominated for a BAFTA Award for Best Colour Art Direction for his work on 'Zulu'. The film has been lauded over the years, being ranked the 37th greatest British movie of all time by Total Film in 2004 and eighth in the British television programme The 100 Greatest War Films. Empire magazine also ranked it among the 500 greatest films.
Related topics: BAFTA Award
Critical Reception
How was 'Zulu' received by critics and audiences?
Upon its release, 'Zulu' received critical acclaim for its thrilling battle scenes and performances, particularly Michael Caine's breakout role. It was praised for its technical qualities and compelling storytelling, becoming one of the biggest box-office hits in the British market. Modern critiques are polarized about whether the film is anti-imperialist or racially insensitive. Despite these debates, 'Zulu' remains a staple of British cinema and is revered for its depiction of heroism.
Related topics: Michael Caine, British cinema
Production Details
Where was 'Zulu' filmed?
The film was primarily shot on location in South Africa, with key scenes filmed at the Drakensberg Mountains. Some scenes were completed at Twickenham Film Studios in Middlesex, England. The production employed real Zulu extras.
Related topics: Drakensberg Mountains, Twickenham Film Studios

How was 'Zulu' received by critics and audiences?
'Zulu' was critically acclaimed upon release and remains a favorite, particularly in Britain. It was praised for its cinematography, battle sequences, and performances, notably that of Michael Caine. The film is also ranked as one of the best British films.

Plot Summary
What is the plot of 'Zulu'?
The plot follows the British soldiers at Rorke's Drift who, after the defeat at Isandlwana, fortify their position against an imminent Zulu attack. The film portrays the strategic decisions and the intense combat that ensues as the outnumbered defenders hold off the advancing Zulu impis.
