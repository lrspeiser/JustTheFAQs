FAQs: The Holdovers

Production
How did 'The Holdovers' come to be made?
'The Holdovers' is the second collaboration between director Alexander Payne and actor Paul Giamatti after 'Sideways.' The concept originated from Payne after seeing the 1935 film 'Merlusse.' Screenwriter David Hemingson wrote the script, reflecting some semi-autobiographical elements.
Related topics: Alexander Payne, Sideways, Merlusse
Reception
How was "The Holdovers" received by critics and audiences?
"The Holdovers" was met with critical acclaim. On Rotten Tomatoes, it has a 97% approval rating based on 360 reviews, with the consensus describing it as a "beautifully bittersweet" film and a satisfying return to form for director Alexander Payne. It scored 82 out of 100 on Metacritic, indicating universal acclaim, and audiences polled by CinemaScore gave it an average grade of "A" on an A+ to F scale.
Related topics: Rotten Tomatoes, Metacritic, CinemaScore

Overview
What is the premise of the film "The Holdovers"?
"The Holdovers" is a 2023 American Christmas comedy-drama film directed by Alexander Payne. Set in 1970, it follows the story of a strict classics teacher, Paul Hunham, at a New England boarding school. He is tasked with supervising students who have nowhere to go during the school's Christmas break. The narrative unfolds as Hunham, a stern disciplinarian, forms an unlikely bond with one of the students left behind, Angus Tully, and the school's cafeteria manager, Mary Lamb.
Related topics: Christmas films, Comedy drama, Alexander Payne
Production
Where was 'The Holdovers' filmed?
Filming for 'The Holdovers' took place in Massachusetts from January to March 2022. Specific locations included several real-life Massachusetts schools and other landmarks in the area to create the fictional Barton Academy.

Accolades
What awards did "The Holdovers" win or get nominated for?
"The Holdovers" received critical acclaim and various accolades. It was nominated for five categories at the 96th Academy Awards, including Best Picture, with Da'Vine Joy Randolph winning for her performance as Best Supporting Actress. Additionally, the film was listed among the top 10 films of 2023 by the National Board of Review and the American Film Institute.

Cast and Characters
Who are the main characters in "The Holdovers"?
The main characters of "The Holdovers" include Paul Hunham, a classics teacher played by Paul Giamatti. His character is known for being strict and having a lazy eye, a feature achieved using special contact lenses. Angus Tully, a student held over the Christmas break, is played by Dominic Sessa. Da'Vine Joy Randolph plays Mary Lamb, the school's cafeteria manager and a grieving mother whose son died in the Vietnam War.
Related topics: Paul Giamatti, Da'Vine Joy Randolph, Vietnam War
Plot
What is the main plot of 'The Holdovers'?
Set in December 1970, 'The Holdovers' revolves around Paul Hunham, a disliked classics teacher at a New England boarding school, who must oversee a group of students who remain on campus during Christmas. The key narrative arc involves the developing relationships between Hunham, the students, especially Angus Tully, and the school's cafeteria manager, Mary Lamb.
Related topics: New England, Boarding school
Production Details
What are some key details about the production and filming of "The Holdovers"?
The film is the second collaboration between Alexander Payne and Paul Giamatti, following their work on "Sideways" (2004). Filming took place in Massachusetts from January to March 2022. To capture a 1970s aesthetic, a mix of digital and film formats was tested before settling on digital with specific lens setups. Director Payne mentioned the ease of creating a 1970s look in New England because change comes slowly there.
