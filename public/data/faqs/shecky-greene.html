FAQs: Shecky Greene

General Information
Who was Shecky Greene?
Shecky Greene, originally born as Fred Sheldon Greenfield on April 8, 1926, in Chicago, Illinois, was an American comedian and actor notable for his high-energy nightclub performances. He was known primarily for his act in Las Vegas, becoming a headliner in the 1950s and 1960s, and for his appearances on television and in films such as "Tony Rome," "History of the World, Part I," and "Splash." Greene's career spanned from 1954 until 2023, with some breaks due to health issues.

Early Life and Education
What was Shecky Greene's early life like?
Shecky Greene was born to Jewish parents and grew up on Chicago's North Side. He had an interest in performance from a young age, participating in his high school drama club and emulating his older brother, who spoke in accents. Greene attended Sullivan High School and later briefly enrolled at Wilbur Wright College.

Career
How did Shecky Greene start his career in comedy?
Shecky Greene began his stand-up comedy career in mob-run nightclubs in Chicago. His big break came when he performed at the Prevue Lounge in New Orleans. This success led him to perform in Miami, Chicago, and eventually Las Vegas, where he became a leading act for many years. Greene's free-form approach to comedy was unique and couldn't be confined to short television spots.
Related topics: New Orleans, Chicago, Illinois, Las Vegas, Nevada
Career
What television and film roles is Shecky Greene known for?
Shecky Greene made numerous appearances on television shows including "The Ed Sullivan Show," "Combat!," and "The Tonight Show." He also served as a guest host and had parts in films such as "Tony Rome," "History of the World, Part I," and "Splash." His improvisational style was well-regarded, although he was known to dislike the rushed format of television.
Related topics: The Ed Sullivan Show, Combat!, The Tonight Show, Johnny Carson
Personal Life
What were some personal challenges Shecky Greene faced?
Throughout his life, Shecky Greene struggled with depression, bipolar disorder, stage fright, gambling, panic attacks, substance abuse, and alcoholism. These challenges affected his career and personal life. He was open about his bipolar disorder, and his humor often reflected his personal experiences.
Related topics: Bipolar disorder, Stage fright, Panic attack, Substance abuse
Personal Life
How was Shecky Greene involved in humanitarian efforts?
Shecky Greene led humanitarian efforts to create St. Jude's Ranch, a shelter for indigent and neglected children located in Boulder City, Nevada. This demonstrated his commitment to giving back to his community beyond his entertainment career.

Awards and Recognition
What awards did Shecky Greene receive?
Shecky Greene was recognized with several awards, including the Las Vegas Entertainment Award for Best Lounge Entertainer, the Jimmy Durante Award for Best Comedian, and an accolade from the Las Vegas Academy of Variety and Cabaret Artists as Male Comedy Star. These awards highlight his impact and achievement in the entertainment industry.

Later Years and Death
How did Shecky Greene's career and life end?
Shecky Greene's career saw a resurgence in 2009 when he returned to performing after overcoming a six-year period of panic attacks and stage fright. However, he eventually retired and passed away at the age of 97 in his Las Vegas home on December 31, 2023.
