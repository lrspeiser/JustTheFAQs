Procedure
How does the U.S. presidential election process work?
U.S. presidential elections are governed by Article Two of the Constitution and the Twenty-second Amendment. Candidates seek nominations through primary elections to be selected by their party's national convention, where they also choose their running mate. The general election is indirect; voters elect members of the Electoral College, which then elects the president and vice president. The election has faced challenges including misinformation and mistrust from previous elections.
