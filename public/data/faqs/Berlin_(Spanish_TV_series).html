What is the premise of 'Berlin'?

The plan involves framing the security provider, but complications arise when Berlin falls in love with the victim’s wife. The series takes place predominantly in Paris.

Production Details
Who created the TV series 'Berlin' and what is its connection to 'Money Heist'?
The series 'Berlin' was created by Álex Pina and Esther Martínez Lobato. It is a spin-off and prequel of the popular series 'Money Heist', exploring the backstory of the character Berlin. The series gives insight into Berlin's life prior to the events of 'Money Heist', offering a different tone with more comedic elements.
Related topics: Álex Pina, Esther Martínez Lobato, Money Heist
General Information
What is the premise of the Spanish TV series 'Berlin'?
'Berlin' is a Spanish television series that serves as a prequel to 'Money Heist'. It focuses on the life of the character Andrés de Fonollosa, aka 'Berlin', before the events of seasons 1 and 2 of 'Money Heist'. The series showcases Berlin leading a criminal gang and executing a heist that involves stealing €44 million in jewels, while falling in love with the wife of someone he's planning to frame. The series is primarily set in Paris.

Production
How was 'Berlin' produced?
'Berlin' was announced by Netflix as a spin-off before the release of 'Money Heist's final volume. Filming began in October 2022 in Paris, France, and continued in Madrid, Spain. The series creators, Álex Pina and Esther Martínez Lobato, aimed for a lighter, more comedic tone compared to 'Money Heist'. The series was shot in a 2:1 widescreen ratio to enhance the scenic experience.

Introduction
What is 'Berlin', the Spanish TV series?
'Berlin', also known as 'Money Heist: Berlin' or in Spanish 'La casa de papel: Berlín', is a Spanish heist crime drama television series created by Álex Pina and Esther Martínez Lobato for Netflix. It serves as a prequel to 'Money Heist', focusing on the character Berlin, portrayed by Pedro Alonso, and his life before the events of the original show's first two seasons.
