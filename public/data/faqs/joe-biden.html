FAQs: Joe Biden

Presidency
What were some key actions Biden took immediately after becoming president?
Joe Biden signed several executive orders in his first days as president, focusing on different areas. These included rejoining the Paris Climate Agreement, ending the national emergency at the Mexico border, re-entering the World Health Organization, introducing face mask requirements on federal property, measures against hunger in the U.S., and revoking permits for the Keystone XL pipeline.

Vice Presidency
How did Biden's role as vice president under Obama influence his political career?
During his vice presidency, Joe Biden was deeply involved in foreign policy decisions and acted as a close advisor to President Barack Obama. He played a crucial role in initiatives like the withdrawal of U.S. troops from Iraq, was a key negotiator with Congress on budget deals, and led efforts on the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act. His position strengthened his political credentials and visibility, paving the way for his presidential run.

Early Life and Education
What events shaped Joe Biden's early life and educational background?
Joe Biden was born in Scranton, Pennsylvania, and later moved to Delaware. He earned a Bachelor of Arts from the University of Delaware in 1965 and a Juris Doctor from Syracuse University in 1968. He suffered from a stutter, which he worked hard to overcome. During his university years, Biden was known for his leadership roles despite being an unexceptional student.

U.S. Senate Career
What were some of Biden's significant legislative achievements as a U.S. Senator?
As a U.S. Senator, Joe Biden played a major role in several key pieces of legislation, including the Violent Crime Control and Law Enforcement Act and the Violence Against Women Act. He was also significant in foreign relations, having taken stances on issues like the Yugoslav Wars and opposition to the Iraq War's initial conduct by the Bush administration.

Personal Life
How did personal tragedy impact Joe Biden's life?
Joe Biden experienced significant personal tragedy, including the loss of his first wife and daughter in a car accident and later, his son Beau Biden's death from cancer. These losses deeply affected him personally and shaped his political career, contributing to his compassionate public persona and influencing his policy priorities, such as healthcare and cancer research.
Related topics: Family of Joe Biden, Beau Biden
Foreign Policy
What were some key foreign policy stances of Biden during his Senate and Vice Presidential tenure?
Joe Biden held a liberal internationalist view, supporting NATO interventions in the Balkans and advocating for diplomacy coupled with military strength. He was involved in formulating U.S. policy on the Iraq War, although he later criticized its execution. Biden also played a role in Obama's decision-making, such as advising on the troop withdrawal from Afghanistan.
Related topics: Foreign policy of the Barack Obama administration, Wars in Afghanistan and Iraq, NATO bombing of Yugoslavia
2020 Presidential Campaign
What challenges did Biden face during his 2020 presidential campaign?
Joe Biden faced several challenges during his 2020 presidential campaign, including dealing with allegations of inappropriate physical contact and a more serious sexual assault allegation, managing the COVID-19 pandemic's impact on campaign activities, and countering attacks from incumbent President Donald Trump, who sought to link Biden and his son to controversies in Ukraine. Despite these, he successfully secured the Democratic nomination and won the presidency.
