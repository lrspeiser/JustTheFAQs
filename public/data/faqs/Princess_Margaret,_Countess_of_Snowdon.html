FAQs: Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon

Later Years and Legacy
How did Princess Margaret's health fare in her later years and what was her legacy?
In her later years, Princess Margaret experienced serious health issues, including strokes and lung problems related to her lifelong smoking habit. Her legacy includes influencing public acceptance of divorce in the royal family and contributing to the public's evolving view of the monarchy.

Relationships and Romances
What was the nature of Princess Margaret's relationship with Peter Townsend?
Princess Margaret had a widely publicized and controversial romance with Peter Townsend, a married RAF officer. Serious consideration was given to their potential marriage, but it was ultimately abandoned due to concerns about Townsend's divorce, religious and constitutional matters, and royal family opposition.

Public Life and Duties
What were some of Princess Margaret's public duties and charity work?
Princess Margaret was involved in various public duties and was president or patron of numerous organizations. Her interests included welfare charities, music, and ballet, and she was notably president of the National Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Children and the Royal Ballet.
Related topics: National Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Children, Birmingham Royal Ballet
Marriage and Family
Who did Princess Margaret marry and did she have children?
Princess Margaret married Antony Armstrong-Jones, later known as the Earl of Snowdon, on 6 May 1960. They had two children: David Armstrong-Jones, 2nd Earl of Snowdon, and Lady Sarah Chatto.

Early Life
Where was Princess Margaret born and did she have any siblings?
Princess Margaret was born at Glamis Castle in Scotland on 21 August 1930. She was the younger daughter of King George VI and Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother, and the only sibling of Queen Elizabeth II.
