FAQs: AEW Continental Championship

Introduction
What is the AEW Continental Championship?
The AEW Continental Championship is a men's professional wrestling championship created and promoted by All Elite Wrestling (AEW). The championship was established on November 18, 2023, and it is contested under Continental Rules, which prohibit outside interference and have a 20-minute time limit for matches. Kazuchika Okada is the current champion, holding the title since March 20, 2024.
Related topics: All Elite Wrestling, Professional wrestling
History
How did the AEW Continental Championship originate?
The AEW Continental Championship was introduced through a tournament called the Continental Classic. Announced by AEW president Tony Khan and wrestler Bryan Danielson, the tournament began on November 22, 2023, and concluded at the 'Worlds End' pay-per-view event on December 30, 2023. Eddie Kingston emerged as the inaugural champion by winning this tournament.
Related topics: Continental Classic, Tony Khan, Bryan Danielson, AEW Worlds End
Title Matches and Rules
What are the rules associated with the AEW Continental Championship matches?
Matches for the AEW Continental Championship are contested under 'Continental Rules,' which include no interference from anyone at ringside and strict prohibition of outside interference, along with a 20-minute time limit. These rules were first introduced during the Continental Classic tournament.

Reigns
Who has held the AEW Continental Championship?
As of January 3, 2025, the AEW Continental Championship has been held by two wrestlers. Eddie Kingston was the inaugural champion, serving the shortest reign at 81 days. Kazuchika Okada is the current champion with the longest reign, which is ongoing at 289+ days. Kingston was recognized as the Continental Crown Champion for holding additional titles during his reign.
Related topics: Eddie Kingston, Kazuchika Okada
Tournaments
What is the AEW Continental Classic?
The AEW Continental Classic is an annual round-robin tournament held by AEW, starting in November and culminating in December at the AEW Worlds End event. The tournament determines the AEW Continental Champion, with the incumbent champion automatically entered to defend their title during the competition.
Related topics: Continental Classic, AEW Worlds End
Notable Events
What significant events have involved the AEW Continental Championship?
A notable event was when Kazuchika Okada defeated Eddie Kingston on March 20, 2024, on AEW Dynamite to separate the AEW Continental Championship from the 'American Triple Crown.' This match marked the first time the Continental Championship was defended independently of the other titles Kingston held at that time.
