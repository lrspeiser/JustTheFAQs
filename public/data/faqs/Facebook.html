FAQs: Facebook

Influence in Politics
What role has Facebook played in political contexts?
Facebook has notably influenced political discourse and events, often being used for organizing protests and movements like the Arab Spring. The platform's algorithms for targeting voters have raised concerns about data use in political campaigns, exemplified by the 2016 U.S. elections and Cambridge Analytica's data misuse. Facebook has been scrutinized for how its platform has been used to spread misinformation and has taken steps to counteract this influence.
Related topics: Social media in politics, Russian interference in the 2016 United States elections
Economic Impact
What is Facebook's economic impact and environmental commitment?
Facebook contributes significantly to the economy by creating a development platform for various applications and social media interfaces, resulting in job creation and business growth. For example, its platform added approximately 182,000 jobs to the U.S. economy in 2011. Additionally, Facebook has committed to reducing its carbon footprint, achieving 100% renewable energy support and net zero emissions for its global operations.
Related topics: Social networking service, Surveillance Capitalism
User Privacy
How does Facebook approach user privacy and data sharing?
Facebook's privacy policy has evolved over the years, focusing on how user data is collected, stored, and shared. It allows users to control access to their posts and profiles through privacy settings. The platform's revenue relies on targeted advertising, which utilizes user data for ad placement without revealing personal identifiers to advertisers. Facebook also shares data with developers and third parties under certain conditions, while law enforcement agencies can access data when necessary.
Related topics: Privacy concerns with Facebook, General Data Protection Regulation
Features
What kind of content can users interact with on Facebook?
Facebook allows users to interact with a variety of content including text, photos, and multimedia posts. They can send friend requests, join groups, and 'friend' others, allowing for personal sharing and interaction. Users can also adjust privacy settings to control who sees their posts. Additional features include Facebook Marketplace for buying, selling, or swapping items, and events advertisements for both offline and online activities.
Related topics: List of Facebook features, Facebook Marketplace
Creation and Development
How did Facebook originate and what were its initial restrictions?
Facebook originated from a website called 'Facemash' created by Mark Zuckerberg in 2003 to let Harvard students compare the looks of fellow students. It quickly evolved into 'TheFacebook' in 2004, initially restricted to Harvard students, before expanding to other universities and eventually to everyone over 13 years old globally in 2006. Zuckerberg met Harvard student Eduardo Saverin during the early days, resulting in both investing $1,000 into the project.
Related topics: History of Facebook, Mark Zuckerberg, Eduardo Saverin
General Information
What is Facebook and who owns it?
Facebook is a social media and social networking service owned by Meta Platforms, an American technology conglomerate. It was created in 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg and his Harvard College roommates Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes. Designed initially for college students, it has evolved into a global platform open to anyone over 13 years old, with specific age limits for certain countries. As of December 2022, Facebook reported nearly 3 billion monthly active users.
Related topics: Meta Platforms, Mark Zuckerberg, Harvard College
Global Reach
How widespread is Facebook's usage globally?
Facebook is available in 112 languages and serves users worldwide except where it is blocked by governmental policies. The platform hosts nearly 3 billion users monthly, making it one of the most engaging and far-reaching social networking services, with its highest number of users located in India, the United States, Indonesia, Brazil, Mexico, and the Philippines. It was also the most downloaded mobile app of the 2010s.

Criticism and Controversies
What controversies has Facebook been involved in?
Facebook has faced numerous controversies, including the Cambridge Analytica data scandal where data from millions of users was misused to influence political campaigns. The platform has also been criticized for privacy violations, spreading misinformation, and enabling fake accounts, leading to scrutiny over its data handling policies. These issues have sparked global outcry and brought substantial legal and regulatory challenges.

Technology
What technology underpins Facebook's operation and development?
Facebook was initially developed using PHP, a step taken due to its simplicity and ease of use. To handle scalability and performance issues as Facebook expanded, engineers developed the HipHop Virtual Machine (HHVM) for optimized PHP performance. The site began moving from HTTP to HTTPS in 2011 for enhanced security. Facebook uses a monolithic application architecture and its servers handle millions of requests using technologies like AJAX, Ptail, and Thrift.
Related topics: PHP, HTTPS, HHVM
