FAQs: Pro Kabaddi League

General Overview
What is the Pro Kabaddi League?
The Pro Kabaddi League (PKL) is an Indian professional league for the sport of Kabaddi. It was inaugurated in 2014 and is the most popular Kabaddi league globally. It is the second most watched sports league in India after the Indian Premier League (IPL).
Related topics: Indian Premier League, Kabaddi
Performance and Achievements
Which team has been the most successful in the Pro Kabaddi League?
The most successful team in the history of the Pro Kabaddi League is the Patna Pirates, having won a total of 3 titles. They are followed by the Jaipur Pink Panthers and Haryana Steelers with 2 titles each.
Related topics: Patna Pirates, Jaipur Pink Panthers, Haryana Steelers
Players and Records
Who has scored the most raid and tackle points in the Pro Kabaddi League?
Pardeep Narwal holds the record for most successful raiding points, while Fazel Atrachali from Iran is known for the most tackle points in PKL history. Pardeep is regarded as a standout player in the league's history.
Related topics: Pardeep Narwal, Fazel Atrachali
League Format
How is the Pro Kabaddi League structured?
The league follows a double round-robin tournament format with playoffs. During the league phase, teams compete against each other twice, and the top teams advance to the playoffs, which include eliminators and finals.

Viewership and Popularity
How popular is the Pro Kabaddi League in India?
The Pro Kabaddi League is extremely popular in India, becoming the second most-watched sports league after the IPL. The inaugural season was watched by 435 million viewers, significantly contributing to its early success.
Related topics: Indian Premier League
Historical Context
What inspired the creation of the Pro Kabaddi League?
The Pro Kabaddi League was inspired by the popularity of Kabaddi in community settings and gained momentum from the 2006 Asian Games Kabaddi tournament. It aimed to capitalize on Kabaddi's local appeal and bring it to a larger audience through professional league modeling.
