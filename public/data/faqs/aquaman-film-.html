FAQs: Aquaman (film)

General Information
What is the film 'Aquaman' about?
Aquaman is a 2018 American superhero film based on the DC Comics character of the same name. Directed by James Wan, the film follows Arthur Curry/Aquaman, played by Jason Momoa, as he sets out to lead the underwater kingdom of Atlantis and stop his half-brother, King Orm, from uniting the seven underwater kingdoms to wage war on the surface world. It is the sixth installment in the DC Extended Universe and was distributed by Warner Bros. Pictures.
Related topics: DC Comics, DC Extended Universe
Plot
What is the main storyline of Aquaman (2018)?
The film begins in 1985 Maine, where lighthouse keeper Thomas Curry rescues Atlanna, queen of Atlantis, from a storm. They fall in love and have a son, Arthur, who can communicate with sea creatures. Atlanna is forced to return to Atlantis and later executed. In the present, Arthur confronts pirates hijacking a Russian submarine and soon embarks on a journey to stop his half-brother Orm from destroying the surface world. Arthur discovers his mother's fate and embraces his destiny as Aquaman to protect both worlds from destruction, ultimately reclaiming his rightful place as king.
Related topics: Atlanna (DC Extended Universe), Ocean Master
Cast
Who are the main actors in Aquaman?
The main cast includes Jason Momoa as Arthur Curry/Aquaman, Amber Heard as Mera, Willem Dafoe as Nuidis Vulko, Patrick Wilson as Orm Marius/Ocean Master, Nicole Kidman as Atlanna, Dolph Lundgren as King Nereus, and Yahya Abdul-Mateen II as David Kane/Black Manta. The film also features Temuera Morrison as Tom Curry and Randall Park as Dr. Stephen Shin.
Related topics: Jason Momoa, Amber Heard, Willem Dafoe, Patrick Wilson, Nicole Kidman
Production
What were some challenges faced during the production of Aquaman?
One of the significant challenges faced during production was filming the underwater sequences. Director James Wan noted the complexity of shooting the 'underwater world,' which required innovative filming techniques such as actors being shot dry-for-wet on special rigs, and later digitally doubled. Several visual effects (VFX) vendors participated in creating the digital environments and CGI animals, pushing the boundaries of hair simulation and digital animation for fluid underwater effects.
Related topics: James Wan, Computer-generated imagery
Reception and Impact
How was Aquaman received by critics and audiences?
Aquaman received mixed reviews from critics but was praised for its visual spectacle and adventurous tone. Despite some criticism for its dialogue and plot, the film was a commercial success, grossing $1.152 billion worldwide. It became the highest-grossing film in the DC Extended Universe at its release time and was lauded for its ability to capture a broad demographic, including female audiences, through effective marketing strategies.
Related topics: Box office
Accolades
What awards did Aquaman receive nominations for?
Aquaman received several nominations, including the Alliance of Women Film Journalists' Outstanding Achievement by a Woman in The Film Industry for Nicole Kidman. It was also nominated for Best Visual Effects at the Gold Derby Awards and received nominations at the Visual Effects Society Awards for Outstanding Created Environment and Virtual Cinematography.
Related topics: Alliance of Women Film Journalists, Visual Effects Society Awards
Future
Is there a sequel to Aquaman, and what is it called?
Yes, there is a sequel to Aquaman. It is titled 'Aquaman and the Lost Kingdom' and was released on December 22, 2023, with James Wan returning as director. The film continues to explore the underwater worlds introduced in the first film, with Jason Momoa reprising his role.
Related topics: Aquaman and the Lost Kingdom
Soundtrack
Who composed the music for Aquaman, and what are some notable tracks?
The music for Aquaman was composed by Rupert Gregson-Williams. Notable tracks from the soundtrack include 'Everything I Need' by Skylar Grey and 'Ocean to Ocean' by Pitbull featuring Rhea, which samples Toto's song 'Africa.' The soundtrack was released by WaterTower Music on December 14, 2018.
Related topics: Rupert Gregson-Williams, Skylar Grey
