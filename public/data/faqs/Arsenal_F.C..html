FAQs: Arsenal F.C.

Stadium and Facilities
Where does Arsenal F.C. play their home games?
Arsenal plays their home games at the Emirates Stadium, which has a capacity of 60,704. This state-of-the-art venue in Holloway, North London, replaced their previous home, Highbury, in 2006. The stadium is known for its modern facilities and is named after the airline company Emirates, which is also the club's sponsor.
Related topics: Emirates Stadium, Highbury
Crest
How has Arsenal's crest evolved over the years?
Arsenal's first crest, adopted in 1888, featured three cannons. Over the years, several redesigns were introduced, including the 'monogram' badge in 1930 and an 'Art Deco' badge later on. The cannon symbol has been a consistent element, and the current crest adopted in 2002 is a copyright-protected design with modern lines and sans-serif typeface.
Mascot
Who is the mascot for Arsenal F.C.?
Arsenal's club mascot is Gunnersaurus Rex, a smiling, 7-foot-tall green dinosaur. Created based on a drawing by an 11-year-old competition winner in 1993, Gunnersaurus is a popular figure at matches and community events, renowned for his playful characteristics and love for the club.
Ownership and Management
Who owns Arsenal F.C. and who is the current manager?
Arsenal F.C. is owned by Stan Kroenke through Kroenke Sports & Entertainment. The current manager is Mikel Arteta, a former club captain who was appointed head coach in December 2019.
Related topics: Stan Kroenke, Mikel Arteta

Where does Arsenal F.C. play their home matches and what is their stadium capacity?
Arsenal F.C. plays their home matches at the Emirates Stadium, which has a seating capacity of 60,704. The club moved to the Emirates Stadium in 2006 after leaving the Arsenal Stadium in Highbury.
Related topics: Emirates Stadium, Arsenal Stadium
Achievements
What major trophies has Arsenal F.C. won?
Arsenal has won 13 league titles, a record 14 FA Cups, two League Cups, 17 FA Community Shields, a Football League Centenary Trophy, one European Cup Winners' Cup, and one Inter-Cities Fairs Cup. They are the third-most successful club in English football in terms of trophies won.
Related topics: FA Cup, List of English football champions
Rivalries
Who are Arsenal's main rivals?
Arsenal's longest and deepest rivalry is with their nearest major neighbor, Tottenham Hotspur, and matches between these teams are known as the North London derby. There is also a rivalry with Chelsea and a historical on-pitch rivalry with Manchester United that intensified during the late 1980s and early 2000s.
Related topics: North London derby, Tottenham Hotspur F.C., Chelsea F.C., Manchester United F.C.
Colours and Crest
What are Arsenal's traditional home and away colours?
Arsenal's traditional home colours are bright red shirts with white sleeves and white shorts. The club's traditional away colours are yellow shirts with blue shorts. The choice of red signifies a historical donation from Nottingham Forest, and the design was updated in 1933 by Herbert Chapman.
Related topics: Nottingham Forest F.C., Herbert Chapman
Supporters and Rivalries
Who are Arsenal's main rivals and how intense is their competition?
Arsenal's primary rivalry is with Tottenham Hotspur, with whom they contest the North London derby. Further rivalries include Chelsea and Manchester United, particularly fierce in times when both clubs competed for league titles. These derbies are some of the most anticipated fixtures in the English football calendar.
Related topics: North London derby, Arsenal F.C.–Chelsea F.C. rivalry, Arsenal F.C.–Manchester United F.C. rivalry
Historical Highlights
When was Arsenal F.C. founded and under what name?
Arsenal F.C. was founded on December 11, 1886, as Dial Square by Scotsman David Danskin and fellow munitions workers in Woolwich. It was later renamed to Royal Arsenal and then to Woolwich Arsenal before becoming known just as Arsenal.
Related topics: Royal Arsenal, Woolwich
General Information
What is the full name of Arsenal F.C. and where are they based?
The full name of the club is The Arsenal Football Club, and it is based in Holloway, North London, England. Arsenal competes in the Premier League, the top tier of English football, and is one of the third-most successful clubs in English football.
Related topics: Premier League, Association football, Holloway, London
Club Colours and Crest
What are the traditional colors of Arsenal, and how has their crest evolved?
Traditionally, Arsenal's home colors have been bright red shirts with white sleeves paired with white shorts. These colors were introduced by manager Herbert Chapman in the 1930s. Over the years, the club's crest has evolved from depicting three cannons to a single one, with the club's name and motto 'Victoria Concordia Crescit,' meaning 'Victory Through Harmony.'
Related topics: Secondary colors
History and Achievements
How did Arsenal begin and what are some of its major achievements?
Founded in 1886 by munitions workers at the Royal Arsenal in Woolwich, the club was initially named Dial Square before being renamed Royal Arsenal. They became the first club from southern England to join the Football League in 1893. Arsenal has achieved major successes including 13 League Championships, 14 FA Cups, two League Cups, and several community shields, making it one of the most decorated clubs in England. It also became the first London club to reach the UEFA Champions League final in 2006.

What is the name of Arsenal's club mascot?

Management and Ownership
Who owns Arsenal and who is their current manager?
Arsenal is predominantly owned by American sports tycoon Stan Kroenke through his KSE UK Inc. Currently, the team's manager is Mikel Arteta, a former club captain, who took over in December 2019. Arteta is known for his strategic management style, having previously worked under renowned manager Pep Guardiola.
Related topics: Mikel Arteta, Stan Kroenke
Club Background
What is Arsenal Football Club and what are they known for?
Arsenal Football Club, commonly known as Arsenal, is a professional football club based in Holloway, North London, England. They compete in the Premier League, the highest level of English football. Known for their rich history, Arsenal has won 13 league titles, including an unbeaten title, a record 14 FA Cups, and several other domestic and European trophies. Arsenal is the third-most successful club in English football, known for their distinctive red and white kits.
