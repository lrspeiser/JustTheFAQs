FAQs: Main Page

 Overview - What is Wikipedia?
Wikipedia is a free content encyclopedia that anyone can edit. It currently has 6,934,106 articles in English.
Featured Article - What is the 'From today's featured article' section?
This section features a highlighted article from Wikipedia's collection. For example, the 'Albona-class minelayer' article discusses Italian mine-warfare ships used by the Italian Regia Marina and the Royal Yugoslav Navy.
Did You Know... - What type of content is included in the 'Did You Know' section?
The 'Did You Know' section includes interesting facts that are often sourced from new or recently expanded articles on Wikipedia. It shares little-known information, such as facts about Saint Amalberga of Temse, criticisms of actresses in civilized drama, and historical events like the Jewish participation in the Polish Army during WWII.
In the News - What events are currently featured in the 'In the News' section?
Recent and ongoing global events are featured, such as the 2025 New Orleans truck attack, the death of former U.S. president Jimmy Carter, the crash of Jeju Air Flight 2216, and the impeachment of South Korea's prime minister.

Featured List - What is highlighted in the 'From today's featured list' section?
The 'From today's featured list' section highlights a particular list from Wikipedia's collection. A current example is the 'Hugo Award for Best Professional Editor', which covers awards given to editors of science-fiction or fantasy stories. Gardner Dozois is noted as a frequent winner.

This section showcases a selected photograph. Currently, it features KiMo Theater, a historic landmark in Albuquerque, New Mexico, known for its Pueblo Deco architecture and associated legends.
Other Areas - What are the other areas of Wikipedia highlighted on the main page?
The main page features areas like the Community portal, Village pump, Site news, Teahouse, Help desk, Reference desk, and Content portals which provide resources and forums for both new and experienced Wikipedia editors.
Wikipedia's Sister Projects - What sister projects does Wikipedia have?
Wikipedia is hosted by the Wikimedia Foundation and has several sister projects, including Commons, MediaWiki, Meta-Wiki, Wikibooks, Wikidata, Wikinews, Wikiquote, Wikisource, Wikispecies, Wikiversity, Wikivoyage, and Wiktionary.
