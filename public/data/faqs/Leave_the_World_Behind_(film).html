FAQs: Leave the World Behind (film)

Production Insights
Who was involved in the production of the film and what was unique about it?
Sam Esmail directed and wrote the screenplay for 'Leave the World Behind'. The film was produced by Julia Roberts, Marisa Yeres Gill, Lisa Gillan, Sam Esmail, and Chad Hamilton. It also featured executive production by Barack and Michelle Obama through their Higher Ground Productions company, a unique aspect considering Barack Obama included the novel on his reading list. The film was predominantly shot on Long Island, New York.
Related topics: Sam Esmail, Higher Ground Productions, Barack Obama, Michelle Obama
Cast
Who are the main actors in 'Leave the World Behind'?
The film stars Julia Roberts as Amanda Sandford, Mahershala Ali as G.H. Scott, Ethan Hawke as Clay Sandford, Myha'la as Ruth Scott, and Kevin Bacon as Danny. Other notable cast members include Farrah Mackenzie as Rose Sandford, and Charlie Evans as Archie Sandford.
Related topics: Julia Roberts, Mahershala Ali, Ethan Hawke, Myha%27la, Kevin Bacon
Symbolism and Themes
What are the key themes and symbolic elements in 'Leave the World Behind'?
The film addresses themes of societal collapse and racial dynamics in America. It symbolically uses the ship 'White Lion', linking issues of misinformation and chaos to historical themes of slavery and societal inequity. Through its narrative, the film continues exploring racial and socio-economic disparities, reflecting on systemic racism and survival instincts in apocalyptic scenarios.
Related topics: Societal collapse, Racism in the United States, Poverty in the United States, White Lion (privateer)
Plot Details
What are the key events in the plot of 'Leave the World Behind'?
The plot revolves around Amanda Sandford and her family who escape to a remote rental house, only to encounter a statewide blackout and mysterious events like oil tankers crashing. The owners, G.H. Scott and his daughter Ruth, join them seeking refuge. The breakdown in communication deepens with a televised emergency alert, and cryptic messages hint at broader chaos potentially linked to an attack on America. The situation escalates with tensions between the characters as America spirals into societal collapse.

General Overview
What is the film 'Leave the World Behind' about?
'Leave the World Behind' is a 2023 American apocalyptic psychological thriller film directed by Sam Esmail. It is based on the 2020 novel by Rumaan Alam. The film follows two families who struggle to comprehend a sudden breakdown in communication and technology suggesting a potentially catastrophic event. Set on Long Island, the story explores themes of societal collapse and human relationships amidst chaos.
Related topics: Apocalyptic and post-apocalyptic fiction, Psychological thriller, Long Island
Release and Reception
How was 'Leave the World Behind' received by audiences and critics?
The film premiered at the AFI Fest on October 25, 2023, and was released in limited theaters and on Netflix. It received generally favorable reviews, holding a 73% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes. Critics praised the performances and its apocalyptic storytelling, though some found its message simplistic. The film quickly became popular on Netflix, reaching eighth in its list of most-watched films.
Related topics: AFI Fest, Rotten Tomatoes, Netflix
Soundtrack
What is notable about the soundtrack of 'Leave the World Behind'?
The soundtrack was composed by Mac Quayle and features an original score inspired by French composer Olivier Messiaen's Mode 3. Director Sam Esmail intentionally chose less commonly used songs to avoid preconceived associations, with a notable scene using the song 'Too Close' by Next to intensify an emotional shift.
Related topics: Mac Quayle, Olivier Messiaen, Mode of limited transposition, Too Close (Next song)
