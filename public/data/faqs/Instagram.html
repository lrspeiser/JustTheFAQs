FAQs: Instagram

Growth and Acquisition
How did Instagram become a part of Meta Platforms?
Instagram was acquired by Facebook (now Meta Platforms) for about $1 billion in cash and stock in April 2012. The deal was meant to keep Instagram independently managed, and both the UK's Office of Fair Trading and the US Federal Trade Commission approved the acquisition. At the time, Instagram had a rapidly growing user base, reaching 1 billion users by June 2018.
Related topics: Meta Platforms, Facebook, Kevin Systrom
History
How did Instagram start and who created it?
Instagram began as Burbn, a mobile check-in app developed by Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger in San Francisco. It initially received seed funding of $500,000 from Baseline Ventures and Andreessen Horowitz. Realizing it was too similar to Foursquare, the creators refocused it on photo-sharing and renamed it Instagram, a blend of instant camera and telegram.
Related topics: Kevin Systrom, Mike Krieger, San Francisco
Features and Tools
What are some key features of Instagram?
Instagram allows users to upload photos and short videos, apply filters, and follow other users. Key features include Stories, a feed of photo and video content that expires after 24 hours, Direct Messaging for private communication, and Explore for discovering trending content. Users can also create Reels and IGTV videos, engage with posts through likes and comments, and use hashtags to discover content.
Related topics: Instagram Reels, IGTV, Hashtag
Safety and Privacy
What measures does Instagram take to ensure user safety?
Instagram has various features to enhance user safety such as comment filters, the ability to turn off comments, restrict interaction from other users, and manage account privacy settings. There are also efforts to combat harassment through automated detection and content filtering. Instagram works on removing illegal content and misinformation, employs algorithms, and allows direct messaging only from approved followers.
Related topics: Cyberbullying, Hate speech, Misinformation
General Overview
What is Instagram?
Instagram is an American photo and video-sharing social networking service owned by Meta Platforms. It allows users to upload media that can be edited with filters, organized by hashtags, and associated with locations via geotagging. Posts can be shared publicly or with followers, and users can interact with others by liking photos and following accounts to add their content to a personal feed. The platform supports 32 languages and is available on iOS, Android, Windows, and the web.
Related topics: Photo sharing, Video sharing, Social networking service
Impact on Mental Health
How does Instagram affect mental health?
Research has indicated potential negative impacts of Instagram on mental health, especially among teens, due to social comparison and body image issues. Meta's internal studies, leaked by The Wall Street Journal, revealed concerns about Instagram's influence on young people's self-perception. Instagram has been criticized for these impacts but also noted for building community and offering self-expression channels.
Related topics: Body image, Social comparison, Digital dependence
