FAQs: Diana, Princess of Wales

General Information
Who was Diana, Princess of Wales?
Diana, Princess of Wales, was a member of the British royal family, born on 1 July 1961 and passing away on 31 August 1997. She was the first wife of Charles III when he was Prince of Wales, and the mother of Princes William and Harry. Diana became an international icon for her activism, glamour, and enduring popularity.

Early Life and Family
What was Diana's background and family life?
Born into the British nobility, Diana was the daughter of John Spencer, Viscount Althorp, and Frances Spencer, Viscountess Althorp. She had a close connection to the royal family from a young age, as her family lived on the Sandringham estate, and her grandmothers served as ladies-in-waiting to Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother. Diana had three siblings and a brother who died in infancy.

Charity Work and Legacy
What was Diana known for in terms of charity work?
Diana was famous for her charitable efforts and was involved with numerous causes, including AIDS awareness and landmine removal. She famously destigmatized AIDS patients by making physical contact with them at a time when the disease was poorly understood. Her humanitarian efforts continued to have an impact even after her death.

Fashion and Style
How did Diana influence fashion?
Diana, Princess of Wales, was a major fashion icon, known for her sophisticated style and ability to make trends. Her outfits were often in the spotlight and she used fashion to influence and endorse her charitable work. Her sense of style continues to impact fashion today.

Death and Commemoration
What led to Diana's death and how was she commemorated?
Diana died in a car crash in the Pont de l'Alma tunnel in Paris on 31 August 1997. Her death prompted an unprecedented public mourning. The televised funeral, attended by royalty and celebrities, was watched by an estimated 2.5 billion people worldwide. Her legacy continues through various memorials and her charitable foundation.

Trivia and Lesser-Known Facts
What are some lesser-known facts about Diana, Princess of Wales?
Among lesser-known facts, Diana was known to some as 'Duch,' a childhood nickname. She was also the first royal bride to have a paying job before her engagement to Charles. Diana frequently defied traditional royal protocols, such as choosing to omit 'obey' from her wedding vows.
