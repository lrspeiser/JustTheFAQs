FAQs: Twitter

Introduction
What is Twitter currently known as?
Since July 2023, Twitter has been officially rebranded as "X". It remains one of the largest social media platforms, allowing users to share short messages, images, and videos.
Related topics: Social networking service, Social media, Most-visited websites
Ownership and Management
Who owns Twitter and when did it change ownership?
Twitter is owned by X Corp, which replaced the previous owner Twitter, Inc. in March 2023. This change followed the acquisition by Elon Musk in October 2022 for $44 billion.
Related topics: X Corp., Twitter, Inc., Acquisition of Twitter by Elon Musk
History and Evolution
Who were the original founders of Twitter?
The original founders of Twitter were Jack Dorsey, Noah Glass, Biz Stone, and Evan Williams. The platform was launched in July 2006 after being created in March of that year.
Related topics: Jack Dorsey, Noah Glass, Biz Stone, Evan Williams (Internet entrepreneur)
Functionality and Features
What are some notable features of Twitter?
Twitter, now X, allows users to share "tweets", or short posts, which can include text, images, and videos. The platform supports various features like direct messaging, video and audio calls, and the Community Notes feature, where users vote on content context.
Related topics: Tweet (social media), Direct message, Community Notes
Business and Financials
What was a major rebranding decision made by Elon Musk in 2023?
In July 2023, Elon Musk announced the rebranding of Twitter to "X" and retired the bird logo. This rebranding completed by May 2024 represents Musk's goal of transforming Twitter into a more comprehensive platform akin to an "everything app".
Related topics: Twitter under Elon Musk
User Engagement and Interaction
How has the character limit for tweets changed over the years?
Originally, tweets were limited to 140 characters, which increased to 280 characters in 2017. In 2023, the limit was removed for subscribed accounts, allowing more flexibility in post length.
Related topics: Tweet (social media)
Censorship and Content Moderation
What are some controversies related to content moderation on Twitter?
Twitter has faced criticism for its handling of disinformation and hate speech. Under Elon Musk's ownership, there have been concerns about the rise of false information and hate speech due to changes in content moderation policies.
Related topics: Disinformation, Hate speech
User Demographics and Usage
What is the demographic composition of Twitter users?
Twitter's user base has evolved over the years. Initially, it was predominantly used by older adults. Recently, the platform has a balanced mix of genders and is popular among younger generations, with a significant portion of engagement coming from a small percentage of highly active users.
Related topics: Twitter usage, Black Twitter
Censorship and Regional Blockages
In which countries is Twitter banned or has been intermittently blocked?
Twitter is completely banned in countries like Russia, Iran, China, and North Korea, with intermittent blocks in several others including Egypt, Turkey, and Venezuela.
Related topics: Censorship of Twitter
Historical Milestones
What are some major events in Twitter's history related to its user growth?
Notable milestones in Twitter's user growth include reaching over 100 million users by 2012 and 200 million by 2013. More recently, under Musk's ownership, the platform claimed to have reached 600 million users by 2024, despite reported declines in usage.
Related topics: Timeline of Twitter
