FAQs: Saltburn (film)

Overview
What is Saltburn about?
*Saltburn* is a 2023 black comedy thriller film written, directed, and co-produced by Emerald Fennell. The story follows a scholarship student, Oliver Quick, who struggles with social class expectations at the University of Oxford. He becomes infatuated with Felix Catton, an aristocratic peer who invites him to his family's eccentric estate, Saltburn, for the summer. The plot explores themes of obsession, deceit, and social dynamics within the context of wealth and privilege. The film culminates in a series of tragic events leading to Oliver's dark manipulation of those around him.

Release Information
When was *Saltburn* released and where can it be streamed?
*Saltburn* premiered at the 50th Telluride Film Festival on August 31, 2023. It was released in theaters in the United Kingdom and the United States on November 17, 2023, with a wide release planned for November 22, 2023. Following its theatrical run, the film became available for streaming on Amazon Prime Video on December 22, 2023. It quickly became one of the most-streamed films on the platform, experiencing a spike in viewership due to its marketing and word-of-mouth promotion on social media platforms, particularly TikTok.
Related topics: 50th Telluride Film Festival, Amazon Prime Video
Critical Reception
How was *Saltburn* received by critics?
*Saltburn* received generally positive reviews from critics. On Rotten Tomatoes, the film holds a 72% approval rating based on 295 reviews, with an average rating of 6.8/10. The consensus describes it as "a candy-coated and incisive film that is a debauched jolt to the senses". Metacritic gives it a score of 61 out of 100, indicating 'generally favorable reviews'. Audiences have shown a mixed but overall positive response, with a CinemaScore grade of 'B−' and a 75% positive feedback from PostTrak surveys.
Related topics: Rotten Tomatoes, Metacritic
Themes and Influences
What themes does *Saltburn* explore and what are its influences?
*Saltburn* delves into themes of excess and obsessive love, with writer Emerald Fennell drawing from her personal experiences of attraction to those from privileged backgrounds. The film's influences include classic works like *A Clockwork Orange*, *Cruel Intentions*, and Daphne du Maurier’s *Rebecca*. Critics have noted parallels to *The Talented Mr. Ripley* in terms of social class themes and character similarities between Oliver Quick and Tom Ripley. Fennell aims to critique and explore the British class system through her satirical lens, likening it to "Barry Lyndon meets indie sleaze".
Related topics: A Clockwork Orange (film), Cruel Intentions, Daphne du Maurier, The Talented Mr. Ripley, Barry Lyndon
Filming Locations
Where was *Saltburn* filmed?
Filming for *Saltburn* began on July 16, 2022, and primarily took place at Drayton House in Northamptonshire, which had never been used for a film before. The production aimed to create an immersive environment that aligned closely with the film's plot. Additional filming occurred at various locations within the University of Oxford, including Magdalen College and St Hugh's College. The unique setting and isolation were chosen to mirror the film's narrative of enclosed summer madness.
Related topics: University of Oxford, Drayton House
