FAQs: 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships

Overview
What are the 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships?
The 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships, also known as the 2024 WJHC, is the 48th edition of the IIHF World Junior Championship's top division. The tournament took place from December 26, 2023, to January 5, 2024, in Gothenburg, Sweden. For the seventh time, Sweden hosted the WJHC, with Gothenburg chosen as the specific location this time. The United States emerged victorious, claiming their sixth championship title by defeating Sweden with a score of 6–2 in the gold medal game.
Related topics: IIHF World Junior Championship, Sweden, Gothenburg
Background
Why was Gothenburg chosen as the host city for the 2024 Championships?
Gothenburg was initially announced as the host city for the 2022 Championships. However, due to adjustments made necessary by the COVID-19 pandemic, the event was relocated to Edmonton and Red Deer, Alberta, for that year. Consequently, Gothenburg's hosting duties shifted to 2024.

Tournament Summary
Which country won the 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships?
The United States won the 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships, securing their sixth title with a 6–2 victory over Sweden in the final match.

Venues
What were the venues for the 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships?
The 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships were hosted in Gothenburg, Sweden. The matches were held at two main venues: the Scandinavium, with a capacity of 12,044, and Frölundaborg, which can accommodate 6,044 spectators, with seating for 2,430.
Related topics: Scandinavium, Frölundaborg
Tournament Statistics
Who was the scoring leader in the 2024 WJHC and how many points did they score?
Jiří Kulich from the Czech Republic was the scoring leader in the 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships, with a total of 12 points.

Who was named the MVP of the 2024 Championships?
Jonathan Lekkerimäki of Sweden was named the Most Valuable Player (MVP) of the 2024 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships.
