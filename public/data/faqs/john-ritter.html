Death
How did John Ritter die and what was the outcome of the legal actions that followed?
John Ritter died on September 11, 2003, from an aortic dissection. While rehearsing for '8 Simple Rules,' he experienced symptoms that were initially treated as a heart attack. He was misdiagnosed, and his condition worsened, leading to his death. Following his passing, Ritter's widow, Amy Yasbeck, and his family filed lawsuits against the doctors and hospital involved. Some lawsuits were settled out of court for $14 million, but the physicians present on the day of his death were found not negligent in a trial.

How did John Ritter's show '8 Simple Rules' continue after his death?
After John Ritter's death, the show '8 Simple Rules' was renamed '8 Simple Rules' and continued for one and a half more seasons before cancellation in 2005.
