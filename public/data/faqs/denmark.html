FAQs: Denmark

General Information
What is the current population estimate of Denmark?
Denmark's population is estimated to be approximately 5.98 million people as of September 2024, making it the 112th most populous country in the world.

Symbols of Denmark
What are the national symbols of Denmark?
Denmark's national symbols include the flag known as Dannebrog, featuring a white Scandinavian cross on a red background, and the national anthem "Der er et yndigt land" (There is a lovely country).

Geography and Environment
What is Denmark's geographical location and terrain like?
Denmark is located in Northern Europe and consists of the Jutland Peninsula and an archipelago of 406 islands. It's known for its flat terrain, sandy coasts, and temperate climate, with the highest point being Møllehøj at 170.86 meters above sea level.

History
When did Denmark transition to a constitutional monarchy?
Denmark became a constitutional monarchy on 5 June 1849, following the peaceful adoption of a new constitution which established a two-chamber parliament.
Related topics: Constitution of Denmark
Culture
What is unique about Denmark's cultural contributions to literature and performing arts?
Denmark is renowned for its literary figures like Hans Christian Andersen and Søren Kierkegaard. In performing arts, Danish cinema gained international acclaim with movements like Dogme 95, co-founded by Lars von Trier and Thomas Vinterberg.
Related topics: Culture of Denmark, Hans Christian Andersen, Dogme 95
Climate and Ecology
What is the climate like in Denmark?
Denmark has a temperate climate with cool to cold winters and mild summers. The average temperature in January is 1.5°C while in August it is 17.2°C. The country experiences about 765 mm of precipitation annually, with autumn being the wettest.
Related topics: Climate of Denmark
Economy
What are Denmark's main economic sectors and its status in global markets?
Denmark's economy is a developed mixed market economy, with significant sectors in wind turbines, pharmaceuticals, and food processing. It has a high-income economy and is one of the top exporters of wind turbines globally.
Related topics: Economy of Denmark
Government and Politics
What is the political structure of Denmark?
Denmark operates under a constitutional monarchy with a unicameral parliament, the Folketing. The monarch retains ceremonial duties, while executive power is exercised by the Prime Minister and the cabinet. Denmark emphasizes consensus decision-making in its political processes.
Related topics: Politics of Denmark
International Relations
How does Denmark participate in international communities and organizations?
Denmark is a member of the European Union but retains its own currency. It is also a founding member of NATO and participates actively in various United Nations programs, maintaining strong political and cultural ties, especially within Scandinavia.
Related topics: Foreign relations of Denmark, Denmark and the European Union
Social Policies
What unique social model does Denmark follow?
Denmark follows the Nordic model which combines free market capitalism with a comprehensive welfare state and strong worker protections, leading to a high standard of living and a high level of income equality.
