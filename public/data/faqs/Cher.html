FAQs: Cher

Cultural Impact
Why is Cher considered a cultural icon?
Cher's vast career spanning music, film, and television, along with her distinctive style and ability to reinvent herself, have established her as a cultural icon. Known for setting fashion trends, challenging social norms, and her open support for LGBTQ rights, she has served as a symbol of independence and reinvention, earning a dedicated fanbase across generations.
Related topics: LGBTQ rights, Goddess of Pop
Later Years
What are some recent projects Cher has been involved in?
In recent years, Cher has been involved in several projects, including her role in 'Mamma Mia! Here We Go Again' (2018), where she sang ABBA's "Fernando". She also released an album of ABBA covers, 'Dancing Queen' (2018), which was critically acclaimed. Cher continues to perform live and has been recognized for her role in charity work and activism, particularly in LGBTQ and human rights issues.
Career Beginnings
How did Cher start her career in music?
At 16, Cher left school and moved to Los Angeles, where she took acting classes. She met Sonny Bono in 1962, and he introduced her to Phil Spector, who used her as a backup singer. She released her first single, "Ringo, I Love You," under the pseudonym Bonnie Jo Mason. In 1965, she gained commercial success with the single "All I Really Want to Do," and soon after, Sonny & Cher became a popular duo.
Career Highlights
What are some key highlights of Cher's career?
Cher gained fame in 1965 with the folk rock duo Sonny & Cher and also achieved solo success with songs like "All I Really Want to Do" and "Bang Bang (My Baby Shot Me Down)." In the 1970s, she topped the US charts and became the female solo artist with the most number-one singles. Cher returned to music in the late 1980s and had a massive hit with "Believe" in 1998, pioneering the use of Auto-Tune. Her career includes ventures into acting, where she won an Academy Award for Best Actress for the film "Moonstruck" in 1987.
Related topics: Sonny & Cher, Auto-Tune, Believe (Cher song), Moonstruck
General Overview
Who is Cher?
Cher, born Cheryl Sarkisian on May 20, 1946, in El Centro, California, is an American singer, actress, and television personality. Known as the "Goddess of Pop," she has a career spanning seven decades, characterized by her continuous reinvention in the entertainment industry. Cher rose to fame as part of the folk-rock duo Sonny & Cher with hits like "I Got You Babe" and has had a successful solo career with hits like "Believe." She is also known for her distinctive contralto voice and for her work as a television star and an Oscar-winning actress.
Acting and Television
When did Cher gain recognition in acting?
Cher gained recognition as an actress in the early 1980s. She garnered critical acclaim for her role in 'Silkwood' (1983), for which she received a Golden Globe Award and an Academy Award nomination. She then starred in hits like 'Mask' (1985), 'The Witches of Eastwick' (1987), and won an Academy Award for Best Actress for 'Moonstruck' (1987). Her television debut on 'The Sonny & Cher Comedy Hour' also contributed significantly to her fame.

Philanthropy and Activism
What philanthropic efforts and causes has Cher supported?
Cher actively supports numerous causes, including poverty alleviation, health research, veterans' rights, and LGBTQ issues. She is involved with organizations like Keep a Child Alive and Habitat for Humanity, and has supported vulnerable children, veterans, and the elderly. Her advocacy extends to animal rights and international issues, like providing aid during the Armenian crisis and promoting awareness about the Armenian Genocide.

Fashion and Icon Status
How has Cher influenced fashion?
Cher has been a fashion icon for decades, often working with designer Bob Mackie to create memorable, groundbreaking outfits that challenged norms, such as her see-through gowns with elaborate beadwork. Cher's bold sense of style has influenced and inspired many artists and designers over the years. Her appearances at events like the Met Gala have set fashion trends, making her a perennial figure in the world of celebrity style.
Personal Life
What can you tell me about Cher's early life and family background?
Cher was born to John Sarkisian, an Armenian-American truck driver, and Georgia Holt, a model and actress. Her paternal grandparents survived the Armenian Genocide. Cher has a half-sister, Georganne. Her parents divorced when she was ten months old, and her mother remarried several times. They often moved, and at one point, Cher was temporarily placed in an orphanage.
Musical Comeback
What is significant about Cher's song 'Believe'?
Cher's 1998 hit "Believe" is notable for its pioneering use of Auto-Tune, creating the "Cher effect," a vocal distortion that became widely popular in music. 'Believe' became a massive hit, topping charts in 23 countries, and made Cher the oldest female artist to have a number-one song on the Billboard Hot 100. The song won her a Grammy Award, and its success revitalized her career in the late 1990s.
General Information
Who is Cher?
Cher, born Cheryl Sarkisian on May 20, 1946, in El Centro, California, is an iconic American singer, actress, and television personality. Known as the 'Goddess of Pop', she stands out for her androgynous contralto voice, bold fashion choices, and the ability to continuously reinvent herself over a career spanning seven decades.
Early Life
What were Cher's early life and family background like?
Cher, whose full name is Cheryl Sarkisian, was born in El Centro, California. Her father was an Armenian-American truck driver, and her mother, Georgia Holt, was an actress and model of Irish, English, German, and Cherokee heritage. Her parents divorced when she was an infant, and she was raised by her mother, who moved the family around frequently due to multiple marriages and financial instability. Despite a difficult childhood, Cher was inspired by film stars like Audrey Hepburn and pursued a career in entertainment.
Music
How did Cher's music career begin and evolve?
Cher's music career began in the 1960s when she was discovered by Sonny Bono. She initially performed as a backing singer for Phil Spector before embarking on a solo career. Her solo work and collaborations with Sonny, such as "I Got You Babe", launched her into stardom. She adapted to various music genres over the years, including disco in the late 1970s and rock in the 1980s. The late 1990s saw her embrace dance-pop with the album 'Believe', which included the hugely successful title track using Auto-Tune.

Recent Projects and Achievements
What are some recent achievements of Cher?
Cher was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2024, making her the first musician-actor to win an Academy Award for acting also to be inducted. She released her first Christmas album in 2023 and continues to influence the music industry with ongoing projects and collaborations, such as a recent venture into her gelato brand, Cherlato.
