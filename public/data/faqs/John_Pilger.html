FAQs: John Pilger

Biography
Who was John Pilger and what is he known for?
John Pilger was an Australian journalist, writer, scholar, and documentary filmmaker celebrated for his critical views on foreign policies of Western countries and his work on Indigenous Australian issues. Starting as a journalist in Australia, he moved to Britain in 1962 and became known for his reports on the Cambodian genocide, among other global issues.
Controversies and Criticism
What controversy surrounded John Pilger's report on a Thai slavery story?
John Pilger reported that he bought a Thai slave girl and returned her to her village, pledging to aid her education. However, this story was later questioned by investigations that suggested the girl and mother were paid to fabricate the story, raising serious doubts about the report's authenticity.

Career Highlights
What are some of John Pilger's notable documentaries?
John Pilger made over 50 documentaries, with highlights including 'Year Zero: The Silent Death of Cambodia', which covered the aftermath of the Khmer Rouge regime, and 'The War on Democracy' that challenged U.S. foreign policy in Latin America. His documentaries often focused on uncovering issues of injustice and human rights violations.
Related topics: Year Zero: The Silent Death of Cambodia, The War on Democracy
Legacy
What is the legacy of John Pilger in journalism?
John Pilger is remembered as one of the most influential investigative journalists, known for his commitment to exposing injustice and state-sponsored violence. His works have inspired discussions on the responsibilities of journalists in holding power to account.

Political Views and Activism
What were John Pilger's views on the U.S. and its foreign policy?
John Pilger was a staunch critic of U.S. foreign policies, which he interpreted as imperialistic and colonialist. He consistently argued that American interventions in countries like Vietnam and Iraq were driven by interests in resources like oil rather than humanitarian concerns.
Related topics: Foreign policy of the United States
Media Critique
How did John Pilger view mainstream media and journalism?
John Pilger was critical of mainstream media, accusing it of echoing government propaganda rather than challenging it. He believed that most journalists were more concerned with supporting the established power structures rather than delivering truth to the public. He argued for more independent journalism that exposes truths rather than aligns with corporate interests.
