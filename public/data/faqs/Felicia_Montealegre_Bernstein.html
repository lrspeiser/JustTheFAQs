Dramatic works with orchestra
In what way did Felicia Montealegre contribute to dramatic works with orchestra?
Felicia Montealegre's contributions to dramatic works with orchestra included her role as the narrator in Lukas Foss's 'Parable of Death' and her memorable performance as Joan in Arthur Honegger's 'Joan of Arc at the Stake,' conducted by her husband Leonard Bernstein with the New York Philharmonic. Additionally, Bernstein wrote the narration for his Symphony No. 3: Kaddish with her in mind, and she narrated its American premiere.

Early life and education
What was Felicia Montealegre's early life like before her career in acting?
Felicia María Cohn Montealegre was born on February 6, 1922, in San José, Costa Rica. She moved to Chile at the age of one, where she was educated at the French School of Nuns. Her multicultural background included a Costa Rican mother and a U.S. father, which enriched her world view. Felicia converted to Judaism before marrying Leonard Bernstein, reflecting her diverse cultural experiences and intellectual inclinations.

Social activism
How did Felicia Montealegre participate in social activism during her lifetime?
Felicia Montealegre was actively involved in social activism, particularly as the first chair of the Women's Division of the New York Civil Liberties Union. She was heavily involved in educational programs and fundraising events, promoting civil liberties awareness. Montealegre was also a supporter of the anti-war organization Another Mother for Peace and was arrested during an anti-war protest in Washington, D.C. In 1970, she hosted a controversial fundraising event for the families of the Panther 21, which drew criticism and media attention.
Related topics: New York Civil Liberties Union, Another Mother for Peace, Black Panther Party
Personal life
Who was Felicia Montealegre married to and did they have children?
Felicia Montealegre was married to renowned composer-conductor Leonard Bernstein. They married on September 9, 1951, and had three children: Jamie, Alexander, and Nina. The Bernsteins were known for their vibrant social life and often hosted notable artists and intellectuals in their home.

Career
What were some significant roles played by Felicia Montealegre in her acting career?
Felicia Montealegre was a versatile actress with an extensive career in both theater and television. On Broadway, she made her debut as the ingénue in Ben Hecht's 'Swan Song' in 1946. Her notable Shakespearean roles included Jessica in 'The Merchant of Venice' at New York City Center and Katharine in 'Henry V' at the Cambridge Drama Festival. Her television career was equally distinguished, starring in leading roles on shows like 'Kraft Television Theatre' and 'Studio One.' She appeared in eleven 'Studio One' teleplays, including 'Of Human Bondage' where she starred opposite Charlton Heston.
