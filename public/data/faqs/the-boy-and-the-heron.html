FAQs: The Boy and the Heron

General Information
What is 'The Boy and the Heron'?
'The Boy and the Heron' is a 2023 Japanese animated fantasy film written and directed by Hayao Miyazaki. Produced by Studio Ghibli, it is not an adaptation but is influenced by Genzabur≈ç Yoshino's 1937 novel of the same name. The film follows a boy named Mahito Maki who moves to the countryside after his mother's death and enters a fantastical world with a talking grey heron.

Production
Why was 'The Boy and the Heron' created after Miyazaki's retirement announcement?
Hayao Miyazaki announced his retirement in 2013 but reversed this decision after working on the short film 'Boro the Caterpillar.' In 2016, he began storyboarding a new feature-length project inspired by his childhood and personal relationships. The film is considered his most expensive and personal project, and was produced over seven years.
Related topics: Hayao Miyazaki, Studio Ghibli, Boro the Caterpillar
Plot
What is the plot of 'The Boy and the Heron'?
Set during the Pacific War in Tokyo, the film follows Mahito Maki, a boy who loses his mother in a hospital fire and moves to the countryside with his father and new stepmother. Mahito encounters a talking heron that leads him into a fantastical world, where he discovers family secrets and battles fantastical creatures.
Related topics: Pacific War, Tokyo, Grey heron
Voice Cast
Who are the voice actors in 'The Boy and the Heron'?
The Japanese voice cast includes Soma Santoki as Mahito Maki, Masaki Suda as the Grey Heron, Aimyon as Lady Himi, and others. The English dub features Luca Padovan, Robert Pattinson as the Grey Heron, Karen Fukuhara, and Gemma Chan, among others.
Related topics: Masaki Suda, Robert Pattinson, Karen Fukuhara
Themes
What are the themes explored in 'The Boy and the Heron'?
The film explores themes of coming of age, coping with loss, and the conflict between personal desires and the greater good. It contains autobiographical elements reflecting Miyazaki's own childhood and includes themes of destruction and reconstruction, inspired by his personal relationships and historical events.
Related topics: Hayao Miyazaki, Pacific War, Coming-of-age tale
Music
Who composed the music for 'The Boy and the Heron'?
The film's score was composed by Joe Hisaishi, a longtime collaborator of Hayao Miyazaki. The soundtrack consists of 37 songs and was released in Japan in August 2023. The theme song, "Spinning Globe," was written and performed by Kenshi Yonezu.
Related topics: Joe Hisaishi, Kenshi Yonezu
Release and Reception
How was 'The Boy and the Heron' received commercially and critically?
The film grossed US$294.2 million worldwide, becoming the fifth highest-grossing Japanese film of all time. It received critical acclaim and won several accolades, including Best Animated Feature Film at the Academy Awards and the Golden Globes.

Impact
What impact did 'The Boy and the Heron' have after its release?
Following its release, the novel 'How Do You Live?' saw a surge in demand, temporarily going out of stock due to its connection with the film. The film contributed to a resurgence of interest in Japanese popular culture in the West.
