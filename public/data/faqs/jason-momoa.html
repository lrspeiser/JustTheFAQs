FAQs: Jason Momoa

Introduction
Who is Jason Momoa?
Jason Momoa, whose full name is Joseph Jason Namakaeha Momoa, is an American actor born on August 1, 1979, in Honolulu, Hawaii. He is best known for his roles in 'Baywatch: Hawaii,' 'Stargate Atlantis,' 'Game of Thrones,' and as Aquaman in the DC Extended Universe. Jason has also appeared in films like 'Dune' and 'Fast X.' In addition to acting, he is a filmmaker and has directed works such as 'Road to Paloma.'

Early Life
What is Jason Momoa's heritage and where did he grow up?
Jason Momoa was born to a Native Hawaiian father, Joseph Momoa, and a mother with German, Irish, and Pawnee heritage, Coni Momoa, who was a photographer. He was raised by his mother after his parents divorced shortly after his birth, moving from Honolulu, Hawaii, to Norwalk, Iowa, where he was brought up.

Career
What major film roles has Jason Momoa played?
Jason Momoa is well-known for playing Arthur Curry/Aquaman in the DC Extended Universe, starring in films like 'Justice League,' 'Aquaman,' and 'Aquaman and the Lost Kingdom.' He portrayed Duncan Idaho in 'Dune' (2021), a lead role in 'Braven,' and a villain in 'Fast X' (2023). Momoa has also featured in the film 'Sweet Girl' and the historic drama 'Frontier' as Declan Harp.
