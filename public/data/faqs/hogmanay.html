FAQs: Hogmanay

Introduction to Hogmanay
What is Hogmanay?
Hogmanay is the Scots word for the last day of the old year and refers to the Scottish celebration of the New Year. It typically includes various customs such as gift-giving and visiting friends and neighbors. The celebration can extend into 1 January and sometimes 2 January, which is a bank holiday in Scotland.
Related topics: New Year's Eve, New Year
Etymology
Where does the word Hogmanay come from?
The etymology of "Hogmanay" is not clear, with various theories suggesting it may derive from French, Norse, or Goidelic roots. The earliest recorded mentions of similar terms date back to the 1440s and the 17th century in Scotland, reflecting a rich linguistic history.
Related topics: Etymology, Scots language
Customs
What are some traditional customs associated with Hogmanay?
One of the key customs is 'first-footing,' where the first person to enter a home after midnight brings gifts like coal, shortbread, or whisky, symbolizing good luck. Celebrations often involve partying late into the night, social gatherings, and sometimes singing 'Auld Lang Syne.'
Related topics: First-foot, Auld Lang Syne
Local Customs
What unique local customs are associated with Hogmanay in Scotland?
In Stonehaven, Aberdeenshire, there's the Fireballs Ceremony, where locals swing flaming balls in a celebratory procession. In Burghead, the Clavie burning is a similar fire festival. Other areas have unique traditions such as carrying a decorated herring in fishing communities or baking special cakes in St Andrews.
Related topics: Stonehaven, Burning of the Clavie
"Auld Lang Syne"
Why is 'Auld Lang Syne' sung at Hogmanay?
'Auld Lang Syne,' a poem by Robert Burns set to music, is traditionally sung at the end of Hogmanay as a farewell to the old year. It has become a symbolic worldwide custom to sing it at New Year's celebrations, often while linking arms with others in a circle.
Related topics: Auld Lang Syne, Robert Burns
Major Celebrations
Where are the largest Hogmanay celebrations held in Scotland?
The largest Hogmanay celebrations are held in Scotland's major cities like Glasgow, Edinburgh, and Aberdeen. Edinburgh's Hogmanay festival is renowned as one of the world's largest New Year's events, attracting huge crowds for its street parties, concerts, and fireworks displays.
Related topics: Edinburgh, Glasgow, Aberdeen
