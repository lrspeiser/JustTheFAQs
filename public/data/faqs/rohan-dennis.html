Championships and Records
Has Rohan Dennis ever held a world record?
Yes, Rohan Dennis held the world hour record from February to May 2015. He set the record by cycling 52.491 kilometers, surpassing the previous record by 639 meters before it was broken later the same year by Alex Dowsett.

Introduction and Personal Information
Who is Rohan Dennis?
Rohan Dennis is an Australian former cyclist, born on May 28, 1990, in Adelaide, South Australia. He competed in both track and road cycling disciplines and is notable for his achievements, including setting a world hour record in 2015. Dennis retired from professional cycling, having competed for various teams throughout his career.
Career Achievements
What are some of Rohan Dennis' major career achievements?
Rohan Dennis is renowned for his significant achievements in cycling, such as winning two consecutive World Time Trial Championships in 2018 and 2019. He also claimed the Tour Down Under in 2015 and set a world hour record by cycling 52.491 km. Additionally, Dennis has claimed victories in time trials at both national and international levels and has secured stage wins in all three Grand Tours.

Personal Life
What is known about Rohan Dennis' personal life?
Rohan Dennis was married to fellow professional cyclist Melissa Hoskins, with whom he had two children. Tragically, on December 31, 2023, his wife was fatally injured in an incident involving a vehicle Dennis was driving. He pleaded guilty to charges related to this incident in 2024.
