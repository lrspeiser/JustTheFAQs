FAQs: Mike McDaniel

Biography and Personal Background
Who is Mike McDaniel?
Michael Lee McDaniel, born on March 6, 1983, is the head coach of the Miami Dolphins in the NFL. He is of biracial descent, with a white mother and a black father. Before coaching the Dolphins, he worked as an assistant coach for various NFL teams and was part of the Shanahan coaching tree.
Related topics: Miami Dolphins, Mike Shanahan, National Football League
Early Life and Playing Career
What was Mike McDaniel's early life like?
Mike McDaniel was born in Aurora, Colorado, where he graduated from Smoky Hill High School in 2001. He played college football as a wide receiver at Yale University, earning a degree in history.
Related topics: Aurora, Colorado, Yale Bulldogs football
Coaching Career
How did Mike McDaniel begin his coaching career?
McDaniel began his NFL coaching career as a coaching intern with the Denver Broncos in 2005 under head coach Mike Shanahan. He then moved on to various assistant roles with the Houston Texans, Washington Redskins, and others before becoming an offensive coordinator with the San Francisco 49ers.
Related topics: Denver Broncos, Mike Shanahan, Houston Texans
Coaching Career
What is notable about Mike McDaniel's time with the San Francisco 49ers?
Mike McDaniel spent five seasons with the San Francisco 49ers, chiefly as their run game coordinator, before being promoted to offensive coordinator. He helped lead the team to appear in Super Bowl LIV in 2020.
Related topics: San Francisco 49ers, Super Bowl LIV
Miami Dolphins
What are some of Mike McDaniel's achievements as head coach of the Miami Dolphins?
McDaniel led the Dolphins to a 9-8 record in 2022, securing their first playoff berth since 2016. In 2023, he coached the Dolphins to a historic 70-20 victory over the Denver Broncos, recording the most yards in a game in NFL history since 1951.
Related topics: Miami Dolphins, Denver Broncos
Miami Dolphins
How did Mike McDaniel make history in his debut season opener with the Dolphins?
In 2022, McDaniel became the first Dolphins head coach to win his first game since Nick Saban in 2005 and the first in franchise history to win a season opener as a rookie head coach.
Related topics: Miami Dolphins, Nick Saban
