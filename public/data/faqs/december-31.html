FAQs: December 31

Understanding December 31
Why is December 31 significant in the Gregorian calendar?
December 31 is the 365th day of the year in the Gregorian calendar, and the 366th day in leap years. It is widely recognized as New Year's Eve, the final day of the year, leading into the start of the new year on January 1. It also marks various cultural and religious festivities worldwide, such as Saint Sylvester's Day, New Year's Eve celebrations, and more.
Related topics: Leap year, New Year's Eve, January 1
Historical Events on December 31
What are some notable historical events that occurred on December 31?
Significant events on December 31 include the 406 crossing of the Rhine by Vandals, Alans, and Suebians, the 1600 chartering of the British East India Company, the 1906 signing of the Persian Constitution, the 1907 first ball drop in Times Square, and the 1999 handover of the Panama Canal from the US to Panama. It also marks the end of the Soviet Union's institutions in 1991, leading to the dissolution of what was once a major global superpower.
Related topics: Vandals, East India Company, Times Square Ball, Dissolution of the Soviet Union
Celebrations and Observances
How is December 31 celebrated around the world?
Globally, December 31 is predominantly celebrated as New Year's Eve, where people partake in festivities, fireworks, and countdowns to welcome the new year. In the United States, First Night events offer non-alcoholic, family-friendly activities. In Japan, the day is called Ōmisoka, marked by a special meal and temple visits at midnight. Scotland celebrates Hogmanay with loud celebrations and traditions like first-footing. These traditions often involve music, dance, and fireworks.
Related topics: New Year's Eve, Hogmanay, Ōmisoka
Recent December 31 Events
What significant events have occurred on recent December 31sts?
In recent years, December 31 has witnessed several significant events, such as the commissioning of the USS Essex in 1942, the end of hostilities in World War II proclaimed by President Harry S. Truman in 1946, the dissolution of the Soviet Union in 1991, and the final 'Calvin and Hobbes' comic strip published in 1995. Important global incidents on this day include the WHO being informed of COVID-19 in 2019, which would later unfold into a global pandemic.

Famous Births
Who are some notable individuals born on December 31?
December 31 boasts the births of many prominent figures across various fields. Some include French painter Henri Matisse, a leading figure in modern art known for his use of color and fluid draftsmanship; General George Meade, a key Union Army general in the American Civil War; and contemporary celebrities like Gabby Douglas, an American Olympic gymnast, and Alex Ferguson, the Scottish football manager famed for his tenure at Manchester United.
Related topics: Henri Matisse, Gabby Douglas, Alex Ferguson
Holidays and Observances
What holidays and observances are recognized on December 31?
December 31 is celebrated as New Year's Eve internationally. Specific observances include Saint Sylvester's Day among Catholics, International Solidarity Day of Azerbaijanis, as well as cultural festivals such as Ōmisoka in Japan. The day also marks the seventh day of the Twelve Days of Christmas in Western Christianity and the sixth day of Kwanzaa in the United States.
Related topics: New Year's Eve, Saint Sylvester's Day, Kwanzaa
Cultural Significance
Why is New Year's Eve, celebrated on December 31, important culturally?
New Year's Eve is not just a time for celebration but also a moment of reflection and resolution for many cultures. It signifies the end of the existing year and the anticipation of the new one. Traditionally, it involves gatherings, sharing meals, fireworks displays, and the practice of making New Year's resolutions, which are commitments to personal improvement. The evening is marked by various cultural traditions, including music and festive decorations worldwide.
Related topics: New Year's Eve, New Year's resolution
