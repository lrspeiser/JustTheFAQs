FAQs: Marvel's What If... TV Series

Music
Who composed the music for Marvel's What If...?
The music for the series was composed by Laura Karpman, who crafted the score to echo iconic themes from the MCU while introducing unique musical elements to highlight the series' inventive premises. She was later joined by her wife Nora Kroll-Rosenbaum for the second season.
Related topics: Laura Karpman
Animation and Visual Style
What animation style does 'What If...?' use and what was its inspiration?
'What If...?' uses a cel-shaded animation style with character likenesses based on the actors from the films. The style was inspired by classic American illustrators like J. C. Leyendecker and Norman Rockwell, aiming for a heroic, hyper-realized look that still feels iconic. Directed by Bryan Andrews with visual development by Ryan Meinerding, the style reflects a blend of cinematics from the films and stylized animation.
Related topics: Cel shading, J. C. Leyendecker, Norman Rockwell, Ryan Meinerding
Development
Who created Marvel's What If...?
The series was created by A. C. Bradley and is based on the Marvel Comics series of the same name. It developed as part of Marvel Studios' efforts to expand their storytelling into animated formats on Disney+.
Related topics: Marvel Comics, A. C. Bradley (screenwriter)
Animation
What is the animation style of Marvel's What If...?
The series uses a cel-shaded animation style to reflect the feel of the MCU films, blending realism with stylized elements. This choice offers a unique visual texture that captures the essence of on-screen actors while embracing animation's creative potential.
Related topics: Cel shading, Marvel Studios
Music and Sound
Who composed the music for 'What If...?' and what is distinctive about it?
Laura Karpman composed the music for 'What If...?', joined by Nora Kroll-Rosenbaum for the second season. Karpman describes the series as a composer's playground, allowing her to reference existing MCU scores while deviating from them. The main theme features shattering glass sound effects, with a melody played on a French horn, creating a unique auditory texture that complements the show's alternate realities.
Related topics: Laura Karpman, Alan Silvestri, French horn
Episodes
How many episodes are there in Marvel's What If...?
The series consists of three seasons with a total of 26 episodes. Each season explores different 'what if' scenarios, with the first two seasons containing nine episodes each and the third containing eight episodes.

General
What is Marvel's What If...?
Marvel's What If...? is an animated anthology series by Marvel Studios that premiered on Disney+. The show explores alternate timelines in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU), posing hypothetical questions about what might have happened if key moments in the movies had occurred differently.
Related topics: Marvel Cinematic Universe, Disney+, Anthology television series
Release and Reception
How has 'What If...?' been received critically and by audiences?
The first season of 'What If...?' holds an 89% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes, praised for its creative storylines and voice acting. The second season improved to a 90% rating, cementing its status as a highlight of the MCU's creative offerings. However, the series has faced criticism for the writing of individual episodes and their length. Despite mixed reviews, it has been celebrated for its compelling exploration of the multiverse concepts.

Who narrates Marvel's What If...?
Jeffrey Wright narrates the series as the Watcher, an all-powerful entity who observes the multiverse. His character is intended to provide a sense of humanity and presence throughout the episodes, much like Rod Serling in The Twilight Zone.

Release
When was Marvel's What If...? released?
The first season premiered on August 11, 2021, as part of Phase Four of the MCU. The second season debuted in December 2023, with a third season following in December 2024, both part of Phase Five. Episodes were released regularly to engage audiences over time.
Production Details
Who are the key creators behind 'What If...?'?
A. C. Bradley served as the head writer for the first two seasons, with Bryan Andrews as the lead director. For the third season, Matthew Chauncey took over as head writer. The series features a cel-shaded animation style, developed by Ryan Meinerding and Andrews, and is produced by Marvel Studios Animation. Executive producers include Kevin Feige, Victoria Alonso, Louis D'Esposito, and Brad Winderbaum.
Related topics: A. C. Bradley (screenwriter), Bryan Andrews (filmmaker), Marvel Studios Animation, Kevin Feige

Premise and Origin
What is the premise of 'What If...?' and how did it originate?
'What If...?' is an American animated anthology television series created by A. C. Bradley for Disney+. Based on the Marvel Comics series of the same name, it explores alternate timelines in the multiverse where key moments from the Marvel Cinematic Universe films occurred differently. The series was developed by Marvel Studios by the end of 2018 and officially announced in April 2019. It offers a unique look at the MCU by presenting pivotal moments with unexpected changes, illustrated in a cel-shaded animation style.
