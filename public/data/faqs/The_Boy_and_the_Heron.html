FAQs: The Boy and the Heron

Plot
What is the plot of 'The Boy and the Heron'?
Set during the Pacific War in Tokyo, the film follows Mahito Maki, a boy who loses his mother in a hospital fire and moves to the countryside with his father and new stepmother. Mahito encounters a talking heron that leads him into a fantastical world, where he discovers family secrets and battles fantastical creatures.
Related topics: Pacific War, Tokyo, Grey heron
Production
Why did Hayao Miyazaki come out of retirement to create this film?
Hayao Miyazaki came out of retirement after working on the short film 'Boro the Caterpillar' and began this new feature-length project. Announcing retirement in 2013, he later reversed this decision, starting to storyboard the film in July 2016. The thematic elements were influenced by his childhood and professional relationships, and he wanted to leave a meaningful work for his grandson.
Related topics: Boro the Caterpillar, Hayao Miyazaki

Music
Who composed the music for the film, and what is notable about it?
The music for 'The Boy and the Heron' was composed by Joe Hisaishi, a long-time collaborator of Hayao Miyazaki. Notably, Kenshi Yonezu contributed the theme song 'Spinning Globe'. The score plays a crucial role in encapsulating the film's emotional depth and was released as a 37-track soundtrack in Japan.

Themes
What themes does 'The Boy and the Heron' explore?
The film delves into autobiographical themes reflecting Miyazaki's own childhood experiences during war, such as evacuation to the countryside and personal loss. Central themes include coming of age, spiritual growth, and creating a world that surpasses the previous generations'. It also uniquely combines elements of the fantastic and the mundane.

What was the box office success of 'The Boy and the Heron'?

Why was 'The Boy and the Heron' created after Miyazaki's retirement announcement?
Hayao Miyazaki announced his retirement in 2013 but reversed this decision after working on the short film 'Boro the Caterpillar.' How was 'The Boy and the Heron' received by audiences and critics?
'The Boy and the Heron' was well-received, achieving critical acclaim for its storytelling and animation. It gained a 97% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes and a score of 91 on Metacritic. The film became the fifth highest-grossing Japanese film of all time, making a significant impact at the box office both domestically and internationally.
