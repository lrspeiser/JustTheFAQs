FAQs: 2023

Key Global Events
What were some of the most significant global events in 2023?
2023 was marked by a series of impactful global events, including the devastating earthquakes in Turkey and Syria, which resulted in nearly 62,000 deaths, and Cyclone Freddy, the longest-lasting tropical cyclone on record, causing over 1,400 deaths in southeast Africa. The Russian invasion of Ukraine continued, alongside the Nagorno-Karabakh conflict which ended with a significant Azeri military offensive. Additionally, the Western U.S. banking crisis led to major bank collapses, notably Silicon Valley Bank and First Republic Bank.

Disasters and Crises
How did natural disasters impact the world in 2023?
Natural disasters significantly impacted the world in 2023. The Turkey-Syria earthquakes were the 5th-deadliest of the century, with massive casualties and destruction. Cyclone Freddy wreaked havoc across southeastern Africa for an unprecedented duration. In Libya, Storm Daniel was the deadliest cyclone since Cyclone Nargis, killing at least 11,000 people. Moreover, severe droughts and wildfires, including those in Greece and Canada, exacerbated climate change impacts.

Technological Advancements
What were the key technological advancements in 2023?
In 2023, significant advancements were made in technology, particularly in AI, with generative AI models gaining widespread application across multiple industries. The field saw improvements in creating realistic text, images, and music, fueled by innovations in machine learning. A breakthrough in synthetic biology was reported with the creation of the first synthetic human embryo from stem cells without sperm or egg.

Environmental Developments
What measures were taken globally in 2023 to address environmental issues?
2023 marked significant progress in environmental policy, with the adoption of the High Seas Treaty aimed at protecting 30% of the world's oceans by 2030. COP28 reached a consensus for countries to transition away from fossil fuels, demonstrating a concerted global effort to tackle climate change, although many challenges remain due to ongoing environmental degradation and climate-related disasters.
