FAQs: Scott Weiland

Early Life
What was Scott Weiland's early life like?
Scott Weiland was born Scott Richard Kline on October 27, 1967, in San Jose, California. Raised in a Catholic household, he was adopted by his stepfather and took the last name Weiland. He was involved in choir during his youth and faced personal challenges, including a traumatic experience of childhood sexual abuse, which he later addressed in his autobiography.

Solo Career and Collaborations
What are some highlights of Scott Weiland's solo career?
Scott Weiland released several solo projects, with '12 Bar Blues' showcasing his versatility in genres like glam rock and trip-hop. He also formed a band called the Wildabouts, releasing 'Blaster' in 2015, shortly before his death.

Death and Legacy
How did Scott Weiland die and what was his legacy?
Scott Weiland died of an accidental overdose in 2015 on his tour bus in Minnesota. He left behind a legacy as a prominent figure in '90s rock with his work in Stone Temple Pilots and Velvet Revolver, selling over 50 million records worldwide. His unique vocal style and stage presence remain influential and remembered in the rock music community.
Career with Stone Temple Pilots
How did Weiland become part of Stone Temple Pilots?
Weiland joined Stone Temple Pilots initially called Mighty Joe Young, with bandmates including the DeLeo brothers. The band became famous for its albums like 'Core' and 'Purple', and hits such as "Plush", "Vasoline", and "Interstate Love Song". Weiland's extensive issues with substance abuse led to periods of conflict and ultimately his firing in 2013.

Career
How did Scott Weiland's career progress with Stone Temple Pilots?
Scott Weiland co-founded the band Stone Temple Pilots in 1985, which became highly successful with albums like 'Core' and 'Purple'. Known for their electric stage presence and hits like 'Plush' and 'Interstate Love Song', the band faced turbulence due to Weiland's substance abuse issues. They experienced multiple breakups and reunions, with Weiland being fired in 2013.

Death and Legacy
How did Scott Weiland pass away and how is he remembered?
Scott Weiland died on December 3, 2015, from an accidental drug overdose. His struggle with substance abuse deeply impacted his life and career. Weiland's legacy is celebrated by fans and peers alike for his contributions to rock music. Artists such as Chris Cornell paid tribute to him, and he remains influential as a frontman of two iconic bands.

General Overview
Who was Scott Weiland?
Scott Weiland, born Scott Richard Kline, was an American singer and songwriter, best known as the lead vocalist of the rock band Stone Temple Pilots and the supergroup Velvet Revolver. He was recognized for his flamboyant stage presence and versatile vocal style.

How did substance abuse affect Scott Weiland's life and career?
Scott Weiland struggled with addiction throughout his life, leading to publicized arrests and periods of incarceration. Weiland's substance abuse issues significantly impacted his career, including contributing to his dismissals from bands.

Early Life
What was Scott Weiland's early life like?
Scott Weiland was born in San Jose, California, and experienced a challenging childhood that included being legally adopted by his stepfather and taking his surname. He moved to Ohio at a young age and suffered a traumatic event when he was raped at 12, a memory he repressed until later in life. After his family moved back to California, he attended Edison High School and Orange Coast College.

Artistry
What was distinctive about Scott Weiland's musical style?
Weiland's musical style was marked by its versatility, moving from grunge and baritone vocals in early works with Stone Temple Pilots, to exploring genres like bossa nova, blues rock, and neo-psychedelia in his solo projects. He was praised for blending influences from varied artists like John Lennon and Jim Morrison.
Related topics: Bossa nova, Blues rock, Jim Morrison
Velvet Revolver
What was Scott Weiland's association with Velvet Revolver?
Scott Weiland was the lead singer of Velvet Revolver, a supergroup formed with former members of Guns N' Roses. The band achieved commercial success with albums like 'Contraband', which included hits like "Slither" and "Fall to Pieces". Weiland won a Grammy for Best Hard Rock Performance with the song "Slither".
