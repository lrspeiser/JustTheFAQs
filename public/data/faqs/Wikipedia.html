FAQs: Wikipedia

Popularity
How popular is Wikipedia globally?
As of April 2024, Wikipedia is ranked fourth by Semrush and seventh by Similarweb among the most visited websites globally. It attracts more than 1.5 billion unique device visits and 13 million edits per month, with over 6.9 million articles on the English Wikipedia alone.
Related topics: Semrush, Similarweb, English Wikipedia
Legal and Copyright
What is Wikipedia's content licensing policy?
Wikipedia's content is licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0, allowing users to share and adapt the material as long as they give appropriate credit and share any derivative works under the same license. It also leverages the GNU Free Documentation License for most text.
Related topics: CC BY-SA, GNU Free Documentation License
Criticism and Controversies
What are some criticisms of Wikipedia?
Wikipedia has faced criticism for systemic bias, including gender and geographical biases, and for the reliability of its content, especially in the early years. The 'Seigenthaler incident' exposed issues with fact-checking, leading to policy changes for biographical articles on living people.
Related topics: Systemic bias, Seigenthaler incident, Reliability of Wikipedia
Openness and Editing
What is Wikipedia's approach to content editing and openness?
Wikipedia follows the procrastination principle for content security, waiting for problems to arise before fixing them. Anyone can edit most articles, although some articles have editing restrictions to prevent vandalism. The "pending changes" system reviews new edits by unregistered users before they are published.
Related topics: Procrastination principle, Vandalism on Wikipedia, Pending changes
Governance and Policies
What are the Five Pillars of Wikipedia?
The Five Pillars of Wikipedia are the core principles guiding its operation: Wikipedia is an encyclopedia; it is written from a neutral point of view; it is free content that anyone can use, edit, and distribute; editors should treat each other with respect and civility; and Wikipedia has no firm rules.
Related topics: Five pillars of Wikipedia, Neutral point of view, Free content
Reach and Impact
How many languages is Wikipedia available in?
Wikipedia is available in 340 languages, making it one of the most multilingual platforms on the internet. The largest language edition is the English Wikipedia, with over 6.9 million articles.
Related topics: List of Wikipedias, English Wikipedia
Community
Who contributes to Wikipedia?
Wikipedia's content is provided by a global community of volunteers, also known as Wikipedians. While anonymous contributions are possible, there's a core group of registered users who make the majority of edits. Studies show a significant gender imbalance in contributors, with most being male.
Related topics: Wikipedia community, Gender bias on Wikipedia
Technology and Design
What technology does Wikipedia use to operate?
Wikipedia relies on the MediaWiki software platform, which is written in PHP and uses a MySQL database. It integrates features like a macro language, variables, and a templating system.
Related topics: MediaWiki
Language Versions
How many language editions does Wikipedia have?
Wikipedia has 340 language editions. The English, Cebuano, German, French, Swedish, and Dutch Wikipedias are among the largest by article count. Wikipedia's language editions collectively comprise more than 64 million articles.
Related topics: List of Wikipedias, English Wikipedia, Cebuano Wikipedia, German Wikipedia, French Wikipedia, Swedish Wikipedia, Dutch Wikipedia
Media and Access
How can Wikipedia be accessed?
Wikipedia can be accessed online via web browsers, and there are mobile versions and apps available for iOS and Android devices. It has various mirrors, archives, and is available in forms like print books and DVDs for offline use.
Related topics: List of Wikipedia mobile applications, Wikipedia:Database download
Community and Culture
How does Wikipedia address editor participation and diversity?
Wikipedia has acknowledged issues with systemic bias and aims to increase diversity among its editor base. Initiatives include edit-a-thons aimed at encouraging female participation and addressing geographical bias. Studies show most contributors are male, with Wikipedia hosting events to promote inclusivity.
Related topics: Gender bias on Wikipedia, Edit-a-thon
Governance
How is Wikipedia governed and managed?
Wikipedia operates with a mix of democratic and hierarchical elements. It is hosted by the Wikimedia Foundation, a non-profit organization, and its community follows certain policies and guidelines developed through consensus.
Related topics: Wikimedia Foundation, Wikipedia:Administration
General Information
What is Wikipedia?
Wikipedia is a free-content online encyclopedia written and maintained by a community of volunteers, known as Wikipedians, using open collaboration and the wiki software MediaWiki. It is the largest and most-read reference work in history and is consistently ranked among the ten most visited websites.
Related topics: Online encyclopedia, Free content, Open collaboration, MediaWiki
History
Who founded Wikipedia and when?
Wikipedia was founded by Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger on January 15, 2001. It began as a project complementary to Nupedia, a free online English-language encyclopedia project.
Related topics: History of Wikipedia, Nupedia
General Information
What is Wikipedia?
Wikipedia is a free-content online encyclopedia written and maintained by a community of volunteers through open collaboration and the wiki software MediaWiki. It is the largest and most-read reference work in history and one of the most visited websites globally, funded primarily by donations.
History
Who founded Wikipedia and when?
Wikipedia was launched by Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger on January 15, 2001, as a single English-language edition. The concept was a complementary project for Nupedia. It has been hosted by the Wikimedia Foundation since 2003.
Related topics: Jimmy Wales, Larry Sanger, Nupedia, Wikimedia Foundation
Legal Aspects
What licensing does Wikipedia content have?
Wikipedia's content is available under the Creative Commons Attribution-Share-Alike 3.0 license, which allows for redistribution, creation of derivative works, and commercial use while authors retain copyright.
Related topics: GNU Free Documentation License, Creative Commons license
Reliability
Is Wikipedia a reliable source?
While Wikipedia has improved in reliability over time, it should still be used with caution. It is not considered a citable source in academic contexts but can be a useful starting point for general information and reference links.
Related topics: Reliability of Wikipedia, Wikipedia:No original research
Criticism and Praise
What are some major criticisms and praises of Wikipedia?
Wikipedia has been praised for democratizing knowledge and its vast coverage. However, it's also criticized for systemic biases, including gender and geographical biases, and for challenges with reliability and control over content edits.
Related topics: Criticism of Wikipedia, Reliability of Wikipedia
