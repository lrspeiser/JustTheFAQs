FAQs: William, Prince of Wales

Education
Where did Prince William go to school and what degree did he earn?
Prince William attended several prestigious institutions starting with Wetherby School and Ludgrove School, ending with Eton College. He graduated from the University of St Andrews in 2005 with a Master of Arts degree in Geography. At Eton, he studied geography, biology, and history of art at A-Level, obtaining an 'A' in geography, a 'C' in biology, and a 'B' in history of art.
Related topics: Wetherby School, Ludgrove School, Eton College, University of St Andrews
Marriage and Children
When did Prince William and Catherine Middleton get married, and how many children do they have?
Prince William and Catherine Middleton married at Westminster Abbey on 29 April 2011. They are parents to three children: Prince George, born 22 July 2013, Princess Charlotte, born 2 May 2015, and Prince Louis, born 23 April 2018.
Related topics: Wedding of Prince William and Catherine Middleton, Prince George of Wales, Princess Charlotte of Wales (born 2015), Prince Louis of Wales
Titles and Duties
What are Prince William's primary royal titles?
Prince William holds the titles of Duke of Cambridge, Duke of Cornwall, and Duke of Rothesay among others. He became Prince of Wales following his father's accession to the throne in September 2022. His duties include representing the monarch at official events and undertaking charitable work.
Related topics: Duke of Cambridge, Duke of Cornwall, Duke of Rothesay, Prince of Wales
Relationship with Catherine Middleton
How did Prince William and Catherine Middleton meet?
Prince William met Catherine Middleton in 2001 while both were studying at the University of St Andrews. They became close friends, reportedly beginning to date in 2003. The couple experienced a brief split in 2004 but reconciled shortly after.
Related topics: Catherine, Princess of Wales, University of St Andrews
Public Image
How is Prince William perceived by the public?
Prince William has consistently been one of the most popular members of the British royal family. His appeal has been described as refined and polite, evolving from a public figure seen as a heartthrob to a family man and a serious figure handling his royal duties.
Related topics: British royal family
Charity Work
What are Prince William's main focuses in his charity work?
Prince William's charity work focuses on mental health, conservation, homelessness, and supporting emergency workers. He has launched initiatives such as the Earthshot Prize to incentivize environmental solutions and has been involved in various organizations promoting these causes.
Related topics: Earthshot Prize, Royal Foundation
Early Life
Who are Prince William's parents and when was he born?
Prince William was born on 21 June 1982 at St Mary's Hospital, London, as the first child of Charles, then Prince of Wales, and Diana, Princess of Wales. His birth occurred during the reign of his paternal grandmother, Queen Elizabeth II.
Related topics: Charles III, Diana, Princess of Wales, Queen Elizabeth II
Military Career
What roles has Prince William held in the military?
Prince William was commissioned as a British Army officer after graduating from the Royal Military Academy Sandhurst. He served in the Blues and Royals regiment and trained with the Royal Navy and Royal Air Force, ultimately becoming a helicopter pilot with the RAF's Search and Rescue Force. He ended his active service in 2013 after conducting 156 search and rescue operations.
Related topics: Royal Military Academy Sandhurst, Blues and Royals, Royal Navy, Royal Air Force, Search and Rescue Force
