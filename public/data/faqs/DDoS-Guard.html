FAQs: DDoS-Guard

General Information
What is DDoS-Guard?
DDoS-Guard is a Russian Internet infrastructure company that provides DDoS protection and web hosting services. It was founded in 2011 by Evgeny Marchenko and has been associated with various controversial clients.
Related topics: Denial-of-service attack, Web hosting service
Company
Where is DDoS-Guard headquartered?
DDoS-Guard is headquartered in Russia, with most of its employees being based there. The company was first registered in 2014 in Sevastopol and has been incorporated in Scotland as Cognitive Cloud LP and in Belize as DDoS-Guard Corp.
Related topics: Russia, Sevastopol, Scotland, Belize
Controversies
Why has DDoS-Guard been investigated by security agencies?
DDoS-Guard has been investigated due to allegations of hosting cybercrime services. Investigative reporter Brian Krebs reported that many of the websites hosted by DDoS-Guard are phishing sites and domains tied to cybercrime services. The company has had clients with criminal activities or connections to controversial groups and movements.
Related topics: Brian Krebs, Phishing, Hamas, Parler, Russia
Infrastructure
Where does DDoS-Guard operate its traffic filtering nodes?
DDoS-Guard operates its traffic filtering nodes in Russia, Germany, the Netherlands, and Japan, where it runs clusters to support its services.
Related topics: Russia, Germany, The Netherlands, Japan
Clients
Who are some of the notable clients of DDoS-Guard?
Some of DDoS-Guard's notable clients have included Hamas, the alt-tech social network Parler, and the imageboard 8kun, which is linked with the QAnon conspiracy theory. The company has also been linked to services like The Daily Stormer, an American neo-Nazi website.
Related topics: Hamas, Parler, 8kun, QAnon, The Daily Stormer
