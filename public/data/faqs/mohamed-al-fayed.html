FAQs: Mohamed Al-Fayed

Introduction and Early Life
Where was Mohamed Al-Fayed born and when?
Mohamed Al-Fayed was born on January 27, 1929, in the Roshdy neighborhood of Alexandria, Egypt. His father was an Egyptian primary school teacher from Asyut.
Related topics: Alexandria, Kingdom of Egypt
Prominent Business Ventures
What are some of Mohamed Al-Fayed's notable business ventures?
Mohamed Al-Fayed's business ventures included owning the Hôtel Ritz Paris, Harrods department store, and Fulham Football Club. He acquired Harrods along with the House of Fraser group in 1985 for £615 million and sold it in 2010 for £1.5 billion to Qatar Holdings.
Related topics: Hôtel Ritz Paris, Harrods, Fulham Football Club
Later Life and Death
When did Mohamed Al-Fayed pass away, and what were the circumstances of his death?
Mohamed Al-Fayed died on August 30, 2023, in London at the age of 94. His cause of death was listed as old age. He was buried at Barrow Green Court alongside his son Dodi after a funeral service during Friday prayers at London Central Mosque.
Related topics: Barrow Green Court, Dodi Fayed
Controversies and Legal Issues
What controversies surrounded Mohamed Al-Fayed regarding the death of Diana, Princess of Wales?
Al-Fayed claimed that the death of Diana, Princess of Wales, and his son Dodi in a car crash was a conspiracy orchestrated by MI6 on behalf of Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh. This claim was dismissed by both French and British investigations, including Operation Paget.
Related topics: Conspiracy theories about the death of Diana, Princess of Wales, MI6, Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh
Sexual Misconduct Allegations
What were the allegations of sexual misconduct against Mohamed Al-Fayed?
Al-Fayed faced multiple accusations of sexual harassment and assault over the years, with more than 20 women accusing him, including allegations of rape. The allegations date from as early as 1979 to 2013, and further claims emerged posthumously in 2024, involving around 200 women.
Related topics: Sexual harassment, Rape
Business Strategies and Impact
How did Al-Fayed impact Fulham F.C. during his ownership?
Al-Fayed bought Fulham F.C. in 1997 for £6.25 million, with the long-term aim of achieving Premier League status, which was achieved by the 2000–01 season. Under his ownership, Fulham also competed in European football, including reaching the 2010 UEFA Europa League final.
Related topics: Fulham F.C.#1997–2001: Al-Fayed takeover
Connection to Scotland
What was Mohamed Al-Fayed's connection to Scotland?
Al-Fayed purchased the Balnagown estate in Easter Ross, Scotland, in 1972 and expanded it significantly. He invested over £20 million in the estate and restored the 14th-century Balnagown Castle. He was an advocate for Scottish independence and even funded a reprint of the Scotichronicon linking Scots to Egyptians.
Related topics: Balnagown Castle, Scottish independence
