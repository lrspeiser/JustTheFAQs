FAQs: Jimmy Johnson (American Football Coach)

Career Overview
What is Jimmy Johnson most famous for in his coaching career?
Jimmy Johnson is renowned for being the first head football coach to win both a college football national championship and a Super Bowl. He achieved the national championship with the University of Miami in 1987 and secure back-to-back Super Bowl titles (XXVII and XXVIII) with the Dallas Cowboys from 1989 to 1993. Additionally, he holds a reputation for rebuilding underperforming programs and for his success as a football analyst post-retirement.
Related topics: College football national championships in NCAA Division I FBS, Super Bowl, Dallas Cowboys
Coaching History
What teams did Jimmy Johnson coach during his career?
Jimmy Johnson began his coaching career as an assistant coach at Louisiana Tech in 1965, later moving to several schools including Picayune Memorial High School, Wichita State, Iowa State, Oklahoma, and Arkansas. His notable head coaching positions included Oklahoma State University (1979-1983), the University of Miami (1984-1988), the Dallas Cowboys (1989-1993), and the Miami Dolphins (1996-1999).
Related topics: Louisiana Tech, Oklahoma State Cowboys football, Miami Hurricanes football, Dallas Cowboys, Miami Dolphins
Notable Achievements
What are some of Jimmy Johnson's major awards and honors?
Jimmy Johnson has received numerous accolades throughout his career, including being a two-time Super Bowl champion, NCAA national champion, AP NFL Coach of the Year in 1990, and induction into both the College Football Hall of Fame (2012) and the Pro Football Hall of Fame (2020). He also had a significant impact on the teams he coached, earning him a revered status in football history.

Television Career
What has Jimmy Johnson done after his coaching career?
After retiring from coaching in 1999, Jimmy Johnson pivoted to a career in sports analysis. He became a prominent analyst for Fox Sports, including roles on 'Fox NFL Sunday' and coverage of the Bowl Championship Series. His media presence has kept him in the public eye, contributing to his continued popularity.
Related topics: Fox Sports, Fox NFL Sunday
Personal Life
What is known about Jimmy Johnson's personal life?
Jimmy Johnson was born on July 16, 1943, in Port Arthur, Texas. He attended Thomas Jefferson High School and later played college football at the University of Arkansas, where he was part of the 1964 national championship team. Johnson has been married twice; first to Linda Kay Cooper and then to Rhonda Rookmaaker. He currently resides in the Florida Keys and has been involved in various business ventures, including restaurant ownership.
Related topics: Port Arthur, Texas, Arkansas Razorbacks football
Survivor Participation
What was Jimmy Johnson's experience on Survivor?
Jimmy Johnson participated as a contestant on the 21st season of Survivor: Nicaragua in 2010. As the oldest contestant on the show, he was part of a tribe that consisted solely of individuals aged 40 and older. Johnson was voted out on Day Eight, finishing in 18th place overall, noting the stress of the competition compared to his coaching experiences.
Related topics: Survivor: Nicaragua
Legacy
What is Jimmy Johnson's legacy in football?
Jimmy Johnson's legacy in football is marked by his innovative coaching style, his success at both the collegiate and professional levels, and his ability to transform teams into champions. His contributions to the game, strategic mind, and persona have left an enduring impact, celebrated through numerous honors, including his induction into the Pro Football Hall of Fame.
