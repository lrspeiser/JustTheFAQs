FAQs: 2024 PDC World Darts Championship

Overview
What is the 2024 PDC World Darts Championship?
The 2024 PDC World Darts Championship, sponsored as the 2023/24 Paddy Power World Darts Championship, was a major professional darts event held at Alexandra Palace in London from December 15, 2023, to January 3, 2024. It was the 31st event organized by the Professional Darts Corporation.

Key Moments
Who won the 2024 PDC World Darts Championship and what were some highlights?
Luke Humphries claimed his first World Championship title, defeating 16-year-old debutant Luke Littler 7–4 in the final. The championship, which was notable for having two unseeded semi-finalists and a record number of 180s, also saw Humphries becoming the world number 1. A significant charitable donation was made as £1,000,000 was raised for Prostate Cancer UK for 180s scored during the tournament.

Player Performances
What was notable about Michael Smith's performance in 2024?
Michael Smith, the defending champion, was seen as unlikely to retain his title after a change in equipment and a challenging season. He was eventually defeated 4–0 by Chris Dobey in the fourth round, marking an early exit for the previous year's winner.

Format and Rules
How were matches structured in this championship?
Matches at the championship followed a 'straight in, double out' rule, requiring players to score 501 points and finish on a double. The tournament used a set format, with matches involving best of five legs per set initially and escalating through each round up to the final, which was a 'first to 7 sets' format.

Historical Context
How did the 2024 championship continue its legacy at Alexandra Palace?
The 2024 tournament marked the 17th consecutive year the World Darts Championship was hosted at Alexandra Palace, reinforcing its significance as a staple venue in the darts calendar and continuing its tradition of showcasing top-tier darts in a festive atmosphere.

Prize and Charity Contributions
What was the total prize money, and how did the event impact charity?
The total prize fund for this championship was £2.5 million, maintained for the sixth consecutive year. Notably, the event enabled a charitable contribution to Prostate Cancer UK, raising £914,000 from 180s, which was rounded up to an impressive £1 million.

Media and Cultural Impact
Was the 2024 PDC World Darts Championship covered by major media?
Yes, major media outlets, including BBC Sport, covered the event extensively. For instance, they ran a unique liveblog during Luke Littler's match against Raymond van Barneveld, highlighting the generational differences and significant moments.

Qualification and Seeding
How was the qualification process for the tournament structured?
The championship featured 96 players. The top 32 were seeded and automatically advanced to the second round. The remaining places were filled by players ranked 33–64 on the PDC Order of Merit and 32 additional qualifiers from various tours and national competitions.
