FAQs: Ray Rice

Overview
Who is Ray Rice?
Raymell Mourice Rice is a former professional American football running back. He played six seasons in the NFL with the Baltimore Ravens from 2008 to 2013. Rice was known for his time at Rutgers during his college career, and he became a key player for the Ravens, earning multiple Pro Bowl selections. However, his career ended following a controversy involving a domestic violence incident.

Early Life
What was Ray Rice's early life like?
Ray Rice was born in New Rochelle, New York, on January 22, 1987. He faced several personal tragedies early in life, including losing his father in a drive-by shooting when he was a year old and his cousin Myshaun Rice-Nichols in a drunk driving accident. Despite this, Rice emerged as a promising athlete, attending New Rochelle High School where he led his football team to success, including holding the state record for yards in a single game.

College Career
How did Ray Rice perform in college football?
Ray attended Rutgers University and became a standout player on the Scarlet Knights football team. He was instrumental in leading Rutgers to several successful seasons, including an 11-win season and their first bowl victory. Rice set multiple school records and was a finalist for the Maxwell Award and ranked seventh in Heisman Trophy voting during his college career.

NFL Career
What were Ray Rice's achievements in the NFL?
Ray Rice was drafted by the Baltimore Ravens in the second round of the 2008 NFL draft. He quickly became a key player for the team, contributing to their Super Bowl XLVII victory and earning three Pro Bowl selections. He ended his NFL career with impressive statistics, including over 6,000 rushing yards and 37 touchdowns.
