FAQs: Burt Reynolds

Early Life
Where was Burt Reynolds born and what is notable about his heritage?
Burt Reynolds was born as Burton Leon Reynolds Jr. on February 11, 1936, in Lansing, Michigan. His ancestry included Dutch, English, Scots-Irish, and Scottish. Despite frequently claiming he was born in Waycross, Georgia, he confirmed his birthplace as Lansing in 2015. He also claimed to have some Cherokee and Italian ancestry.
Related topics: Lansing, Michigan, Dutch people, Scotch-Irish Americans, Scottish people, Cherokee, Italians
Career Highlights
What were some of Burt Reynolds' most iconic film roles?
Burt Reynolds starred in several iconic films throughout his career, including 'Deliverance' (1972), which marked a major turning point in his career. He also achieved great success with 'Smokey and the Bandit' (1977), which initiated a series of popular car-chase films. Other notable movies include 'The Longest Yard' (1974), 'Cannonball Run' (1981), and 'Boogie Nights' (1997). His role in 'Boogie Nights' earned him a Golden Globe and multiple award nominations.
Related topics: Deliverance, Smokey and the Bandit, The Longest Yard (1974 film), Boogie Nights
Career Decline and Recovery
How did Burt Reynolds' career change in the 1980s and 1990s?
In the 1980s, Burt Reynolds experienced a decline in his career due to a series of commercially unsuccessful movies and a severe injury on set, which led to persistent rumors about his health. He became addicted to painkillers following this injury. However, he managed a comeback with his role in 'Boogie Nights' (1997), which earned him critical acclaim and several award nominations. Despite this resurgence, he struggled to regain his A-list status.
Related topics: Boogie Nights, City Heat

Health and Death
What were some of Burt Reynolds' major health issues and how did he die?
Burt Reynolds suffered from multiple health issues throughout his life, including hypoglycemia and temporomandibular joint dysfunction, the latter resulting from an injury on the set of 'City Heat'. His health was further compromised by a subsequent addiction to painkillers. He also underwent back surgery and quintuple heart bypass surgery. Reynolds passed away from heart failure on September 6, 2018, at the age of 82.
