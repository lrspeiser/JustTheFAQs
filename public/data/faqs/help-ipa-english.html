FAQs: Help:IPA/English

Introduction to IPA
What is the purpose of the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA) on Wikipedia?
The International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA) is used on Wikipedia to indicate the pronunciation of words. It provides a set of symbols to consistently represent the sounds of all spoken human languages. Wikipedia articles apply this system to transcribe the pronunciation of English words using these symbols, sometimes in ways specific to Wikipedia, which may differ from their usage in other contexts like dictionaries.
Related topics: International Phonetic Alphabet
Key Symbols and Usage
How can IPA symbols vary in their representation of English sounds on Wikipedia?
On Wikipedia, IPA symbols might be used differently than in traditional dictionaries to reflect English pronunciations. Some symbols are unique to Wikipedia and capture pronunciation distinctions across various dialects, such as the cot-caught merger in some American dialects or the father-bother merger. This shows how Wikipedia encapsulates dialectal variations, which helps in understanding pronunciations across global varieties of English.
Related topics: Cot–caught merger, Father–bother merger
Consonants and Vowels
What are some examples of IPA symbols used for English consonants and vowels?
The IPA symbols for English consonants include [b], [d], [g], and for vowels, symbols like [ɑː] and [ɪ]. For instance, the [b] sound appears in words like 'buy' or 'cab', and the vowel [ɑː] is found in words like 'palm' or 'bra'. These symbols are applied to preserve phonetic nuances across different English dialects.

Dialect Considerations
How does the IPA account for dialect variations in English on Wikipedia?
The IPA on Wikipedia represents diaphonemes, which are abstractions of speech sounds that can encompass varieties like General American, Received Pronunciation, and others such as Australian or Canadian English. It captures broad distinctions, such as the absence of certain sounds in non-rhotic accents like Received Pronunciation, which do not pronounce 'r' unless followed by a vowel. Furthermore, it allows flexibility to accommodate variations, such as the merger of 'cot' and 'caught' in some dialects.

Guidelines for Editors and Readers
How should pronounciations be formatted and added to Wikipedia articles?
When adding IPA transcriptions to Wikipedia articles, it is recommended to use the {{IPAc-en}} template. The template helps by providing tooltips for each symbol in the pronunciation. Additionally, maintaining consistency between the symbol key and transcriptions is crucial, and any changes should be agreed upon through consensus on the talk page.

Technical Assistance
Why are some IPA symbols not displaying correctly in my browser?
If IPA symbols do not display properly in your browser, you might need to install a font that supports these characters. Compatible fonts include Gentium and Charis SIL, while for monospaced font needs, Everson Mono is recommended.
Related topics: Gentium, Charis SIL, Everson Mono
