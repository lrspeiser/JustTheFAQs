FAQs: Curry & Cyanide: The Jolly Joseph Case

Introduction
What is 'Curry & Cyanide: The Jolly Joseph Case' about?
Curry & Cyanide: The Jolly Joseph Case is a true-crime documentary directed by Christo Tomy. It investigates six deaths within the same family, focusing on Jolly Joseph, who is at the center of the Koodathayi Cyanide killing case. This docuseries was released on Netflix on December 22, 2023.
Related topics: Netflix, Christo Tomy, Koodathayi cyanide killings
Background
What are the real events that inspired 'Curry & Cyanide: The Jolly Joseph Case'?
The series is based on real events that took place in the village of Koodathayi, Kerala, India, between 2002 and 2016. Jolly Joseph was accused of poisoning six of her family members, including her first husband, with cyanide-laced food to inherit family property and marry her lover, who was her husband's cousin. The documentary is based on first-person testimonials from individuals involved in the case.
Related topics: Koodathayi, Kerala, India, Jolly Joseph, Cyanide poisoning
Production Details
Who are the key figures behind the production of 'Curry & Cyanide: The Jolly Joseph Case'?
The docuseries is written by Shalini Ushadevi and directed by Christo Tomy, with music composed by Tushar Lall. Cinematography is handled by Shehnad Jalal and Hari K. Vedantam, and editing is done by Sruthy Sukumaran and Praveen Prabhakar. It is produced by India Today Originals and premiered on Netflix.
