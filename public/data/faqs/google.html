FAQs: Google

Company Overview
What is Google LLC?
Google LLC is an American multinational corporation and technology company that specializes in various sectors including online advertising, search engine technology, cloud computing, computer software, and artificial intelligence. It's known as one of the world's most powerful and valuable brands due to its market dominance and technological advancements.
Related topics: Google Search, Alphabet Inc., Googol, Google (disambiguation)
Company Overview
When was Google founded and by whom?
Google was founded on September 4, 1998, by Larry Page and Sergey Brin, who were PhD students at Stanford University at the time. The company initially started as a research project while they were students.
Related topics: Larry Page, Sergey Brin, Alphabet Inc., Stanford University
History
How did Google get its name and what was its original concept?
The name 'Google' originated from a misspelling of 'googol,' which refers to the number represented by 1 followed by 100 zeros. This was chosen to reflect the mission of providing vast amounts of information. The search engine initially used the concept of PageRank, which ranked pages based on their backlinks and importance, instead of just keyword frequency.
Related topics: PageRank, Relevance (information retrieval), Baidu, Googol
Company Growth
What was the significance of Google's Initial Public Offering (IPO)?
Google's IPO took place on August 19, 2004, and was significant because it marked the company's transition to a public entity. The IPO was conducted via an online auction and the company's stock immediately became popular, giving it a market capitalization of over $23 billion.
Related topics: Public company, Initial public offering, NASDAQ
Products and Services
What are some of the major products and services offered by Google?
Google offers a wide range of products and services including the Android operating system, Google Search, Google Cloud Platform, YouTube, Google Maps, Google Workspace, and Google Assistant. These services cater to various sectors such as web browsing, cloud computing, smart home automation, and artificial intelligence.
Related topics: List of Google products, Google Search, YouTube, Google Cloud Platform
Historical Milestones
What role did Google's acquisition of YouTube play in its growth?
Google acquired YouTube on November 13, 2006, for $1.65 billion in stock. This acquisition was key in establishing Google as a leader in the online video market, substantially increasing its digital media presence and offering a new revenue stream through video advertising.
Related topics: YouTube, Online video platform, Digital media
Innovation and Technology
How is Google involved in artificial intelligence?
Google is heavily involved in artificial intelligence through various initiatives such as TensorFlow for machine learning, Google Assistant as a virtual assistant platform, and strategic acquisitions like DeepMind. Google continues to integrate AI across its products to improve their functionality and user experience.
Related topics: Artificial intelligence, Machine learning, TensorFlow, Google Assistant
Corporate Identity and Philosophy
What is Google's mission statement and unofficial slogan?
Google's mission statement is 'to organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful.' Its unofficial slogan, historically, is 'Don't be evil,' which has been complemented by Alphabet's motto 'Do the right thing' following the formation of Google's parent company.
Related topics: Mission statement, Don't be evil, Alphabet Inc.
Workplace Culture and Ethics
What is unique about Google's workplace culture?
Google is known for its distinctive workplace culture that emphasizes innovation and employee well-being. They have a policy called '20% time' where employees can work on projects of their choice, which has led to the development of products like Gmail. Google has been consistently rated as a top place to work.
Related topics: Innovation Time Off, Gmail, Fortune (magazine)
Corporate Structure
Where are Google's main offices located?
Google's headquarters, known as the Googleplex, is located in Mountain View, California. The company also has numerous offices around the world, including major tech hubs like New York City, London, and Bangalore to support its vast international operations.
Related topics: Googleplex, Mountain View, California, London
Controversies and Criticisms
What major controversies has Google faced?
Google has faced several major controversies, including allegations of anti-competitive behavior, privacy concerns, and issues related to censorship. It has been criticized for its dominance of online search and advertising, leading to multiple legal challenges globally, including significant fines from the European Union.
Related topics: Criticism of Google, Censorship by Google, Privacy concerns regarding Google
