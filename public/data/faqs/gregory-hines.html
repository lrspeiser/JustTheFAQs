FAQs: Gregory Hines

Introduction
Who was Gregory Hines?
Gregory Hines was an American dancer, actor, choreographer, and singer, widely celebrated as one of the greatest tap dancers of all time. Renowned for his improvisational skills with tap dance rhythms, he was also a prominent actor, appearing in films such as 'Wolfen,' 'The Cotton Club,' and 'Waiting to Exhale,' and in television roles like 'Will & Grace.' Hines received numerous awards, including a Daytime Emmy, a Drama Desk Award, and a Tony Award.
Related topics: Tap dance, The Gregory Hines Show, Little Bill
Early Life
What influenced Gregory Hines' early passion for tap dancing?
Gregory Hines began tap dancing at the age of two in New York City under the influence of his father Maurice Robert Hines, a dancer, musician, and actor. Gregory, alongside his brother Maurice, performed together as The Hines Kids and later The Hines Brothers, honing their skills with celebrated artists such as Howard Sims and The Nicholas Brothers. Their act evolved with the inclusion of their father to become 'Hines, Hines, and Dad.'
Related topics: Harlem, Maurice Hines, Henry LeTang
Career in Dance
How did Gregory Hines contribute to the art of tap dance?
Gregory Hines was an innovative force in tap dance, known for his improvisational style that blended traditional and modern rhythms. His performances were characterized by a freeform experimentation with jazz and postmodern dance elements. Beyond performing, he was an advocate for tap dance, successfully petitioning for National Tap Dance Day in the U.S. and serving on boards like the American Tap Dance Foundation. He also hosted a PBS special that highlighted prominent tap dancers.
Related topics: American Tap Dance Foundation, National Tap Dance Day, Savion Glover
Film and Television Career
What were some notable films and television roles of Gregory Hines?
Gregory Hines' film debut was in Mel Brooks' 'History of the World, Part I' in 1981. He further gained prominence with roles in 'The Cotton Club' (1984), alongside his brother Maurice Hines, and 'White Nights' (1985) with Mikhail Baryshnikov. In 1986, he co-starred with Billy Crystal in the buddy cop film 'Running Scared.' Hines was also known for his television roles in 'The Gregory Hines Show' and as Ben Doucette on 'Will & Grace.'
Related topics: Running Scared (1986 film), White Nights (1985 film), The Cotton Club (film)
Musical Career
What was Gregory Hines' contribution to music?
In the mid-1970s, Gregory Hines was the lead singer and musician for the rock band Severance, which released a self-titled album in 1976. He found success in 1986 with the duet 'There's Nothing Better Than Love' with Luther Vandross, which hit No. 1 on the Billboard R&B charts. He continued to explore his musical talents, releasing a self-titled album that included the hit single 'That Girl Wants to Dance with Me.'
Related topics: Luther Vandross, Billboard R&B charts
Personal Life
Who were Gregory Hines' family and loved ones?
Gregory Hines was twice married, first to Patricia Panella with whom he had a daughter, Daria, and then to Pamela Koslow, with whom he had a son, Zachary. He was engaged to Negrita Jayde from 2000 until his death in 2003. His brother, Maurice Hines, was also a well-known dancer and performer.
Related topics: Negrita Jayde, Maurice Hines
Death and Legacy
What was Gregory Hines' legacy in the performing arts?
Gregory Hines passed away from liver cancer in 2003, leaving behind a powerful legacy in dance and entertainment. He was celebrated for bringing tap dance to a wider audience and inspiring a new generation of dancers. In recognition of his impact, the United States Postal Service honored him with a postage stamp as part of its Black Heritage Series in 2019.
Related topics: United States Postal Service
Awards and Honors
What awards did Gregory Hines receive during his career?
Throughout his career, Gregory Hines earned numerous prestigious awards, including a Tony Award for Best Actor in a Musical for 'Jelly's Last Jam' and a Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Performer in an Animated Program for 'Little Bill.' He also received a Drama Desk Award and various Image Awards, reflecting his versatility and impact in both performing arts and entertainment.
Related topics: Tony Award, Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Performer in an Animated Program, Image Awards
