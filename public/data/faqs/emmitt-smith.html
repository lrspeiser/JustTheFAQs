FAQs: Emmitt Smith

Early Life
Where was Emmitt Smith born and what was his high school football career like?
Emmitt James Smith III was born in Pensacola, Florida, and he attended Escambia High School, where he played high school football and ran track. Smith became the second-leading rusher in American high school football history, rushing for 8,804 yards and 106 touchdowns. He helped Escambia win two state championships. Despite this, some college recruiters questioned his potential, deeming him too small and slow for major college football. Nonetheless, he was honored as the USA Today and Parade magazine High School Player of the Year in 1986 and was later named the Florida high school football 'Player of the Century' by the FHSAA in 2007.

College Career
What college did Emmitt Smith attend and what records did he set there?
Emmitt Smith attended the University of Florida, where he played for the Gators from 1987 to 1989. He set numerous school records during his college career, including career rushing yards (3,928) and most rushing touchdowns (36). Smith was a unanimous All-American in 1989 and decided to forgo his senior year of eligibility to enter the NFL draft after Steve Spurrier became the head coach and planned to implement a pass-first offense at Florida.
Related topics: University of Florida, Florida Gators football, Steve Spurrier, All-America
Professional Career
How did Emmitt Smith perform in his NFL career with the Dallas Cowboys?
Emmitt Smith was selected by the Dallas Cowboys in the 1990 NFL draft. He became the all-time leading rusher in the NFL with 18,355 yards, and he holds records for career rushing touchdowns (164) and rushing attempts (4,409). Smith was part of the Cowboys' dynasty in the 1990s, winning three Super Bowls and earning Super Bowl MVP in Super Bowl XXVIII. He is known for his durability, vision, and leadership as part of 'The Triplets' alongside Troy Aikman and Michael Irvin.
Related topics: Dallas Cowboys, Super Bowl Most Valuable Player Award, NFL Most Valuable Player, The Triplets
NFL Records
What NFL records does Emmitt Smith hold?
Emmitt Smith holds several significant NFL records. He is the NFL's all-time leader in career rushing yards (18,355), rushing touchdowns (164), and rushing attempts (4,409). He also has the record for most consecutive seasons with over 1,000 rushing yards with 11 and is the first player to post eleven 1,000-yard seasons in a career. Smith also shares the total playoff touchdown mark of 21 with Thurman Thomas.

Life After Football
What has Emmitt Smith pursued after his football career?
After retiring from football, Emmitt Smith founded Pat & Emmitt Smith Charities and ventured into real estate development, teaming up with Roger Staubach to form Smith/Cypress Partners LP. Smith also won the third season of 'Dancing with the Stars' and has worked as a studio analyst for NFL Network and ESPN. Additionally, he is involved in various entrepreneurial and philanthropic endeavors, including serving as the CEO of ESmith Legacy, a commercial real estate development company.
