FAQs: Jake Browning

Early Life and High School
What were some of Jake Browning's achievements in high school?
Jake Browning attended Folsom High School in California, where he set several national and state records. He completed 1,191 of 1,708 attempts for 16,775 yards and 229 touchdowns, a milestone in California high school records. His 229 touchdowns also broke the national record previously held by Maty Mauk. In his senior year, Browning threw for a national-record 91 touchdown passes and passed for a California record of 5,790 yards, surpassing his own junior year record. He was named the Gatorade Football Player of the Year for two consecutive years during his junior and senior years.

Professional Career
What is notable about Jake Browning's tenure with the Cincinnati Bengals?
Browning joined the Cincinnati Bengals in 2021, initially signing to the practice squad. He was elevated to the backup quarterback role, eventually stepping in as starter after Joe Burrow's injury in 2023. Jake Browning's impressive performance included winning a memorable overtime victory against the Jacksonville Jaguars, with a passing completion rate of 86.5 percent, which is the highest completion rate for a quarterback in their first or second career start. This performance earned him AFC Offensive Player of the Week honors. He continued to perform admirably, securing victories and making history as a Bengals quarterback.
