FAQs: Kiribati

Geography
What makes the geography of Kiribati unique?
Kiribati is composed of 32 atolls and one raised coral island, Banaba. Its islands are spread over a vast maritime area but cover only a minimal total land area. This island nation spans three major island groups: Gilbert, Phoenix, and Line Islands. The vastness of the ocean between the islands underlines its environmental and logistical challenges.
Related topics: Geography of Kiribati, Banaba, Gilbert Islands, Phoenix Islands, Line Islands
Environment and Geography
Why is Kiribati prone to climate change effects?
Being a low-lying island nation with much of its land barely above sea level, Kiribati is particularly vulnerable to the effects of climate change, including rising sea levels and increased salination of soil and freshwater resources. The nation faces significant environmental challenges since these impacts threaten its very existence.

Politics
How is the government structure of Kiribati categorized?
Kiribati is a unitary parliamentary republic with an executive presidency. Its government features a president who is both the head of state and the head of government, supported by a vice-president and a cabinet. Laws and decisions are made by the Maneaba ni Maungatabu, the country's House of Assembly.
Related topics: Politics of Kiribati, President of Kiribati, House of Assembly (Kiribati)
Overview
What is the significance of the location of Kiribati?
Kiribati is unique because it's spread across all four hemispheres: Northern, Southern, Western, and Eastern. Its geographical location makes it the only country in the world placed in such a manner, positioned centrally in the Pacific Ocean. Moreover, the International Date Line swings eastward near Kiribati, positioning its easternmost islands in the most advanced time zone on earth, UTC+14.
Related topics: Kiribati
History
How did Kiribati gain independence?
Kiribati gained independence from the United Kingdom on July 12, 1979. Before independence, Kiribati was part of the Gilbert and Ellice Islands. Following independence, it became a sovereign nation and later joined the United Nations in 1999.
Related topics: History of Kiribati, United Nations
Environmental Issues
What environmental challenges does Kiribati face due to climate change?
Kiribati is extremely vulnerable to the effects of climate change, particularly sea level rise which threatens its low-lying atoll environments. It is anticipated that, within this century, many of its islands could become submerged due to rising sea levels. The island nation has been proactive in climate change diplomacy and preparing temporary measures such as land purchases abroad to counteract these threats.
Related topics: Effects of climate change on small island countries, Sea level rise
Health and Education
What are the main health challenges faced by Kiribati?
Kiribati faces significant health challenges, including high rates of smoking and diabetes. The country has limited healthcare facilities and resources, contributing to a lower life expectancy compared to other regions.

Culture
What are the main characteristics of Kiribati dance?
Kiribati dance emphasizes the movement of arms and sudden movements of the head, drawing inspiration from the native Frigate bird, which is also depicted on the national flag. Dances are traditionally used to tell stories, demonstrate skill, and express cultural heritage. Smiling during performances is not customary, reflecting the dance's serious and respectful cultural roles.
Related topics: Dance in Kiribati, Culture of Kiribati
Politics and Government
What type of government does Kiribati have?
Kiribati functions as a unitary parliamentary republic with an executive presidency. The government is democratically elected, and the President serves as the head of state and government. The legislative branch consists of a unicameral House of Assembly.

People and Society
What is the demographic composition of Kiribati?
The population of Kiribati is largely ethnic I-Kiribati, who are primarily of Oceanic descent. The official languages are Gilbertese and English, with Gilbertese being more commonly used.

Religion and Beliefs
What is the predominant religion in Kiribati?
The predominant religion in Kiribati is Christianity, with the majority of the population adhering to the Roman Catholic Church followed by various Protestant denominations.

Economy
What are the main economic activities in Kiribati?
Kiribati's economy is heavily reliant on fishing, copra production, and international aid. The country also benefits from fishing licenses and remittances. However, it is considered one of the least developed countries in the world due to limited resources and economic challenges.

Cultural Insights
What is unique about Kiribati's traditional music and dance?
Kiribati's traditional music is heavily based on vocal chanting and body percussion. Dances such as the standing dance ('Te Kaimatoa') and the hip dance ('Te Buki') are characterized by the performers' outstretched arms and bird-like head movements, which are unique to Kiribati's culture.

History
Why is Kiribati's name pronounced differently from its spelling?
The name 'Kiribati' is derived from 'Gilberts', referencing the Gilbert Islands, a part of Kiribati. It is pronounced as 'Kirr-i-bass' because the '-ti' in the Gilbertese language represents the 's' sound. This reflects the linguistic characteristics of the indigenous population and the modern adaptation of colonial influences.
