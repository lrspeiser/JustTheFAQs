Advocacy and Activism
What causes and political activities has Emma Thompson been involved in?
Emma Thompson is known for her vocal advocacy on numerous social and political issues. As an atheist and politically liberal individual, she has been a lifelong supporter of the Labour Party in the UK. She has campaigned for human rights and is the chair of the Helen Bamber Foundation for the Care of Victims of Torture. Additionally, she is a passionate environmentalist and supporter of Greenpeace, having participated in several initiatives, including the 'Save the Arctic' campaign. Thompson also openly criticized the Heathrow Airport expansion by purchasing land to prevent its development. Her activism extends to gender equality, having supported the Women's Equality Party.

Career Highlights
Has Emma Thompson received any notable awards for her work?
Emma Thompson is a highly decorated actress and screenwriter, having won two Academy Awards in her career. She received the Best Actress Oscar for her role in 'Howards End' (1992) and another for Best Adapted Screenplay for 'Sense and Sensibility' (1995). In addition to her Oscar wins, she has garnered widespread recognition with three BAFTAs, two Golden Globes, and a Primetime Emmy Award, among other accolades. These awards highlight her consistent excellence and versatility in both acting and screenwriting across different mediums.

Early Life and Education
What was Emma Thompson's early life like?
Emma Thompson was born on April 15, 1959, in London to Scottish actress Phyllida Law and Eric Thompson, an actor known for writing 'The Magic Roundabout.' She has a younger sister, Sophie, who is also an actress. Thompson was educated at Camden School for Girls and later studied English at Newnham College, Cambridge, becoming a member of the Footlights troupe. She spent much of her childhood in Scotland and had a strong interest in language and literature. Her 'seminal moment' came after discovering the book 'The Madwoman in the Attic', which inspired her towards feminism and performing. At Cambridge, she became the first female member of the Footlights, alongside Stephen Fry and Hugh Laurie.
Related topics: Phyllida Law, Eric Thompson, Footlights

Writing
Has Emma Thompson written books, and what inspired her?
Yes, Emma Thompson has ventured into writing by contributing to the beloved 'Peter Rabbit' series by Beatrix Potter. She wrote 'The Further Tale of Peter Rabbit' in 2012 to commemorate the 110th anniversary of the publication of 'The Tale of Peter Rabbit.' This marked the first authorized Peter story since 1930. Her writing falls in the middle of the original series plotline and expands the adventures into Scotland.

Career
How did Emma Thompson break into acting?
Emma Thompson's acting career began with her involvement in the Cambridge Footlights troupe. Her professional debut was in a stage version of 'Not the Nine O'Clock News' in 1982. She then moved into television, starring in BBC and ITV series like 'There's Nothing to Worry About!' and 'Alfresco'. Her breakthrough came with the West End revival of 'Me and My Girl' in 1985, for which she garnered significant acclaim. The late 1980s saw her gaining prominence with performances in BBC's 'Fortunes of War' and 'Tutti Frutti', leading to a BAFTA TV Award for Best Actress.
