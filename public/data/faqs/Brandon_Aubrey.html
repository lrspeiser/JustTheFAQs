Overview
Who is Brandon Aubrey?
Brandon Aubrey is an American multi-sport athlete born on March 14, 1995, in St. Louis, Missouri. He is known for playing as a placekicker for the Dallas Cowboys in the NFL and previously played professional soccer as a center back. In soccer, he was drafted by Toronto FC in 2017 and played for their reserve team before switching to American football, where he achieved success in the USFL with the Birmingham Stallions.

American Football Career
How did Brandon Aubrey transition to American football?
After being released by Bethlehem Steel FC, Aubrey used his Notre Dame degree to work as a software engineer. His transition to American football began in 2019 when he started training as a placekicker. He played for the Birmingham Stallions in the USFL and was successful, reaching double-digit field goals and earning league awards, which eventually led to his signing with the Dallas Cowboys in 2023.

Soccer Career
What was Brandon Aubrey's youth soccer career like?
Brandon Aubrey played youth soccer with Dallas Texans SC, where he was a team captain from 2008 to 2010. His team won five State Cup titles and were regional finalists in 2009. They also achieved several other tournament victories including the Gothia Cup in 2008 and the Dallas Cup in 2009. Aubrey was named to the USSDA All-Select Team in 2011–12 and won the U17 Disney Showcase twice.

American Football Career
How did Brandon Aubrey transition from soccer to American football?
After his release from Bethlehem Steel FC in 2018, Aubrey decided to become a software engineer but later switched careers after his wife suggested he try American football. He trained extensively with a former collegiate kicker before joining the Birmingham Stallions in the USFL in 2022, later signing with the Dallas Cowboys in 2023.
