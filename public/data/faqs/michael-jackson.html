FAQs: Michael Jackson

Introduction
Who was Michael Jackson and why is he known as the 'King of Pop'?
Michael Joseph Jackson was an American singer, songwriter, dancer, and philanthropist, regarded as one of the most significant cultural figures of the 20th century. Known as the 'King of Pop,' he transformed the music video medium into an art form, popularized dance moves like the moonwalk, and broke racial barriers, leading him to colossal global influence and popularity. Over four decades, his contributions to music, dance, and fashion, alongside his publicized personal life, made him a global icon.

Early Life
What was Michael Jackson's early life like?
Michael Jackson was born on August 29, 1958, in Gary, Indiana, to the Jackson family, the eighth of ten children in a working-class African-American family. He began his public music career at age six with his brothers in the Jackson 5, achieving success with them after they signed with Motown.
Related topics: Jackson family, The Jackson 5, Motown
Musical Breakthrough
How did Michael Jackson become a solo star?
Michael Jackson established his solo career in 1971 with Motown, but it was his 1979 album 'Off the Wall' that set him up as a global solo star. His 1982 album 'Thriller' became the best-selling album of all time and included iconic music videos such as 'Beat It' and 'Billie Jean,' which contributed to his immense popularity and ability to break racial barriers in the music industry.
Related topics: Off the Wall, Thriller (album), Racism against African Americans
Musical Innovations
What are some of Michael Jackson's notable contributions to music videos?
Michael Jackson pioneered the music video medium, turning them into elaborate short films. His 'Thriller' music video is particularly iconic, featuring a 14-minute mini-movie directed by John Landis. His videos like 'Beat It' and 'Billie Jean' helped break racial barriers on platforms like MTV and fundamentally altered how artists approach music videos.
Related topics: Thriller (album), MTV, Michael Jackson videography
Cultural Impact
What was Michael Jackson's influence around the world?
Michael Jackson's cultural impact was immense and global. He inspired many through his music and dance across various continents. In Africa, he was compared to Nelson Mandela for uniting diverse audiences. In India, Jackson was likened to Mahatma Gandhi in advocating for racial unity and humanitarian messages through his music. His influence reached as far as the Middle East and South Korea, shaping entertainment, fashion, and music trends globally.

Later Life and Controversies
What were some challenges Michael Jackson faced later in his career?
From the late 1980s, Michael Jackson's life was marred by controversies including allegations of child sexual abuse. These allegations became a recurrent and highly publicized issue, affecting his career and health. He was put on trial and acquitted in 2005, and despite these challenges, he continued to have a significant fanbase and musical impact. He planned a comeback with the This Is It concerts before his death in 2009.
Related topics: Trial of Michael Jackson, Death of Michael Jackson
Death and Legacy
How did Michael Jackson's death impact the world?
Michael Jackson died on June 25, 2009, due to cardiac arrest caused by propofol intoxication. His death sent shockwaves worldwide, spurred a huge surge in his music sales, and his memorial service was viewed by an estimated 2.5 billion people. Despite the controversies, his artistic contributions continue to influence music, dance, and artists globally.
Related topics: Death of Michael Jackson, Michael Jackson memorial service
Posthumous Releases
What are some posthumous releases and tributes to Michael Jackson?
After Michael Jackson's death, several albums and projects were released, maintaining his popularity. This includes 'This Is It,' a documentary detailing his planned comeback tour, and albums with unreleased tracks like 'Xscape.' There have been Cirque du Soleil shows and a Broadway musical, 'MJ the Musical', celebrating his legacy. His musical and entertainment influence continues to be cherished globally.
