FAQs: Priscilla Presley

Life with Elvis
How did Priscilla Presley meet Elvis?
Priscilla met Elvis Presley on September 13, 1959, when she was just 14 years old at a party at his rented villa in Bad Nauheim, Germany. Elvis was serving in the military at the time. Despite initial parental disapproval, Priscilla and Elvis became close and continued their relationship even after Elvis returned to the United States.

What was Priscilla Presley's experience moving to Graceland?
After nearly two years of corresponding and visiting, Priscilla was allowed to move to Memphis, Tennessee, in 1963, just before her 18th birthday, and lived with Elvis's father and stepmother temporarily. Marriage and Divorce
What was Elvis and Priscilla Presley's marriage like, and when did they get divorced?
Elvis and Priscilla married on May 1, 1967, at the Aladdin Hotel in Las Vegas. They had one child together, Lisa Marie Presley, born exactly nine months later. However, their marriage faced many challenges, including Elvis's touring and Priscilla's feelings of isolation. They separated in February 1972 and finalized their divorce in October 1973, opting to share custody of Lisa Marie but remaining close friends.

Following her divorce, Priscilla Presley embarked on several romantic relationships but never remarried. Early Life
When was Priscilla Presley born and what can you tell me about her early family background?
Priscilla Presley was born as Priscilla Ann Wagner on May 24, 1945, at Brooklyn Naval Hospital in Brooklyn, New York. Her maternal grandfather was Albert Henry Iversen from Egersund, Norway, and her mother was of Scots-Irish and English descent. Her biological father, US Navy pilot James Frederick Wagner, died in a plane crash when she was just a few months old. Her mother remarried US Air Force officer Paul Beaulieu, and her surname was changed to Beaulieu. The family moved frequently due to her stepfather's Air Force career.

How did Priscilla Presley become involved with the business side of Elvis's legacy?

Priscilla's leadership successfully preserved and capitalized on Elvis's legacy.
