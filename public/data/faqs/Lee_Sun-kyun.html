FAQs: Lee Sun-kyun

Early Life
Where was Lee Sun-kyun born and educated?
Lee Sun-kyun was born in Seoul, South Korea on March 2, 1975. He attended the Korea National University of Arts in 1994, graduating in the first ever class of its School of Drama with a Bachelor of Fine Arts.
Related topics: Seoul, Korea National University of Arts, Bachelor of Fine Arts
Personal Life and Death
What were the circumstances surrounding Lee Sun-kyun's death?
Lee Sun-kyun was found dead on December 27, 2023, in Seoul, South Korea. His death was ruled a suicide by carbon monoxide poisoning, involving a charcoal briquette in his vehicle. At the time of his death, he was under investigation for alleged drug consumption and had been facing blackmail and media scrutiny.
Related topics: Charcoal-burning suicide, Carbon monoxide poisoning, Suicide in South Korea
Career
How did Lee Sun-kyun contribute to the field of acting?
Aside from his roles in mainstream television and films, Lee Sun-kyun was active in musical theatre and supported the revival of the Drama Special (formerly Drama City), emphasizing its importance as a training ground for young talent. His dedication helped to highlight the value of single-episode dramas in nurturing emerging actors.
Related topics: Musical theatre, Drama City
Career
What were some of Lee Sun-kyun's most notable roles?
Lee Sun-kyun's most internationally recognized role was in the Academy Award-winning film Parasite (2019) by director Bong Joon-ho. On television, he gained fame from series like Coffee Prince (2007), Behind the White Tower (2007), and My Mister (2018). He also received critical acclaim for his role in films such as Paju (2009), Helpless (2012), and A Hard Day (2014).
Related topics: Bong Joon-ho, Parasite (2019 film), Coffee Prince (2007 TV series), Behind the White Tower, My Mister, Paju (film), Helpless (2012 film), A Hard Day
Awards and Nominations
What awards did Lee Sun-kyun win during his acting career?
Throughout his career, Lee Sun-kyun received various accolades including a Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture for Parasite. He also won the Best Actor award at the Las Palmas de Gran Canaria International Film Festival for his performance in Paju (2009) and was nominated at the International Emmy Awards for his role in Dr. Brain (2021).
Related topics: Screen Actors Guild Awards, Las Palmas de Gran Canaria International Film Festival, International Emmy Awards, Paju (film), Dr. Brain (TV series)
