FAQs: Thanksgiving (2023 film)

General Information
What is 'Thanksgiving (2023 film)' about?
'Thanksgiving' is a 2023 American slasher film directed by Eli Roth and written by Jeff Rendell. It is based on a story by Roth and Rendell, who also produced it with Roger Birnbaum. The film follows a small Massachusetts town that is terrorized by a killer in a John Carver mask around the Thanksgiving holiday one year after a Black Friday riot ended in tragedy. The film stars Patrick Dempsey, Addison Rae, Milo Manheim, Jalen Thomas Brooks, Nell Verlaque, Rick Hoffman, and Gina Gershon.
Related topics: Eli Roth, Jeff Rendell
Plot
What happens in the movie 'Thanksgiving'?
In 'Thanksgiving,' a mob gathers outside a Massachusetts superstore, RightMart, before a Black Friday sale. Chaos ensues, leading to multiple deaths. A year later, the town faces a new threat when a masked killer known as 'John Carver' targets residents involved in the prior year's turmoil. The killer, revealed to be the town sheriff, confesses his motive stems from a personal vendetta linked to the riot death of his lover and unborn child.

Production
How did 'Thanksgiving' come to be a feature film?
'Thanksgiving' originated from a fake trailer created by Eli Roth for the film 'Grindhouse' in 2007. Following interest in the concept, scripts were developed over several years. In 2023, Spyglass Media Group produced the film, and Eli Roth departed from another project to direct it. Principal photography took place in Toronto and Hamilton, Ontario, from March to May 2023.
Related topics: Grindhouse (film), Eli Roth
Release
When was 'Thanksgiving (2023 film)' released?
'Thanksgiving' was released in the United States on November 17, 2023, by TriStar Pictures. It was made available digitally on December 19, 2023, and on Blu-ray/DVD on January 30, 2024. The film began streaming on Netflix on February 17, 2024.

Box Office
How did 'Thanksgiving (2023 film)' perform at the box office?

Sequel
Is there a sequel to 'Thanksgiving (2023 film)'?
