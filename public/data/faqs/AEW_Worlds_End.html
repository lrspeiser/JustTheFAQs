FAQs: AEW Worlds End

Trademark and Announcement
When was the trademark for AEW Worlds End filed and announced?
AEW filed the trademark for "Worlds End" on October 25, 2023, and later that same night announced the inauguration of the event during an episode of Dynamite.

At the first AEW Worlds End event in 2023, the main event featured Maxwell Jacob Friedman (c) vs. Samoa Joe for the AEW World Championship, with Eddie Kingston winning the C2 tournament. General Information
What is AEW Worlds End?
AEW Worlds End is a professional wrestling pay-per-view (PPV) event produced by All Elite Wrestling (AEW). Established in 2023, it is held annually in late December around New Year's Eve. The event also hosts the finals of the annual Continental Classic (C2) tournament.
Related topics: Professional wrestling, Pay-per-view, List of All Elite Wrestling pay-per-view events
History
How did AEW Worlds End begin?
AEW Worlds End began after All Elite Wrestling (AEW) filed a trademark for the name "Worlds End" on October 25, 2023. The first event was held on December 30, 2023, at the Nassau Veterans Memorial Coliseum in Uniondale, New York. It marked AEW's first PPV in New York State and coincided with the finals of the inaugural Continental Classic tournament.
Annual Events
Is AEW Worlds End a recurring event?
Yes, AEW Worlds End is an annual pay-per-view event held in late December, serving as the final of the Continental Classic tournament.

Continental Classic
What is the Continental Classic tournament?
The Continental Classic (C2) is an annual wrestling tournament hosted by AEW. It starts after the Full Gear PPV event in mid-November and concludes at Worlds End in December. The tournament follows a round-robin format with two leagues: Blue and Gold, and the prize for the winner is the AEW Continental Championship. Matches have a 20-minute time limit, with no ringside interference allowed.
