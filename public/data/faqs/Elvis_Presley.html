FAQs: Elvis Presley

Comeback and Las Vegas Era
What was significant about Elvis Presley's '68 Comeback Special?
The '68 Comeback Special, officially titled 'Singer Presents...Elvis', aired in December 1968 and marked Elvis Presley's triumphant return to live performance. It featured a mixture of studio recordings and live music that highlighted his dynamic stage presence. It played a pivotal role in revitalizing his career, showcasing his versatile musical talent and energizing public interest in him.

Rise to Fame
What was the breakthrough moment for Elvis Presley's career?
Elvis Presley's career breakthrough came in 1954 when he recorded 'That's All Right' at Sun Records. This song, played by DJ Dewey Phillips, created a sensation and marked Presley as a rising star. This event led to a rise in popularity, characterized by sold-out shows and a distinctive rock and roll style that captivated audiences across America.
Related topics: That's All Right, Sun Records

Early Life and Career
Where was Elvis Presley born and what was his early musical inspiration?
Elvis Aaron Presley was born on January 8, 1935, in Tupelo, Mississippi. Presley was deeply influenced by gospel music at an early age, attending an Assembly of God church with his family where he found his initial musical inspiration. His close-knit relationship with his parents and his exposure to church choirs helped shape his formative years in music.
Related topics: Tupelo, Mississippi, Assembly of God
Military Service
How did Elvis Presley's military service impact his career?
Elvis Presley was drafted into the United States Army in 1958. His service, occurring at the peak of his early career, temporarily put a hold on his musical endeavors. However, this period allowed him to maintain his popularity through strategically released singles and albums. In addition, it afforded him a break from the intense pressures of his early fame.

Personal Life
Who was Elvis Presley's wife and did they have any children?
Elvis Presley married Priscilla Beaulieu in 1967, and they had one daughter, Lisa Marie Presley, born on February 1, 1968. Despite their eventual separation and divorce in 1973, Elvis and Priscilla remained amicable and were committed to co-parenting Lisa Marie.
Related topics: Priscilla Presley, Lisa Marie Presley
Health and Death
What were the circumstances surrounding Elvis Presley's death?
Elvis Presley died on August 16, 1977, at his Graceland home in Memphis, Tennessee. The official cause of death was ruled as cardiac arrest, though his extensive drug use was heavily implicated. His health had been in decline due to multiple ailments and prescription drug abuse, leading to his untimely death at the age of 42.
