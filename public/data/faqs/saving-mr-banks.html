FAQs: Saving Mr. Banks

Historical Accuracy
How accurate is 'Saving Mr. Banks' in portraying historical events?
While 'Saving Mr. Banks' depicts many real events, it takes some creative liberties. For instance, it fictionalizes a key plot point where Disney convinces Travers to hand over the film rights. In reality, Disney had acquired the rights before she arrived in Hollywood but needed her approval on the script. The film also suggests Travers was ultimately pleased with 'Mary Poppins', though in reality, she was displeased with the animated sequences and some character portrayals.
Related topics: Film rights, Walt Disney, P. L. Travers

The film's original score was composed by Thomas Newman. He incorporated elements reminiscent of the musical style of the Sherman Brothers from Mary Poppins without creating an adaptation score.

Flashbacks reveal her troubled childhood in Queensland, Australia, which inspired much of 'Mary Poppins'. Travers is resistant to Disney's whimsical adaptation ideas, especially regarding the portrayal of George Banks. However, through various breakthroughs, including a visit to Disneyland and script changes, Travers slowly leans towards collaboration, despite her initial resistance.

Historical Accuracy
How does 'Saving Mr. Banks' deviate from historical events?
The film fictionalizes several aspects, including the notion that Walt Disney had to convince P. L. Travers to grant the film rights, when in fact, the rights had already been secured subject to script approval. Additionally, the depiction of Travers' acceptance of Disney's changes is not entirely accurate; she was known to have strongly disapproved of certain elements like the animation.

What is 'Saving Mr. Banks' about?
'Saving Mr. Banks' is a 2013 biographical drama film directed by John Lee Hancock. The film details the behind-the-scenes story of the making of the 1964 film 'Mary Poppins'. It focuses on the conflict between Walt Disney and author P.L. Travers over adapting her 'Mary Poppins' stories into a movie. Production and Development
How was the film 'Saving Mr. Banks' developed?
Initially, 'Saving Mr. Banks' was an independent project developed by Essential Media Entertainment and BBC Films. In 2011, producer Alison Owen approached Walt Disney Pictures for permission to use copyrighted elements. This led Disney to acquire the screenplay and co-produce the film. The screenplay was written by Kelly Marcel and Sue Smith, with John Lee Hancock directing. The film's development involved access to audio recordings and letters between Walt Disney and P. L. Travers to accurately portray their relationship during the production of 'Mary Poppins.'

How does the film portray P. L. Travers and her relationship with Walt Disney?
