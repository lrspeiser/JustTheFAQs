What was Heather O'Rourke's upbringing like?
Heather O'Rourke was born to Kathleen and Michael O'Rourke, with her mother working as a seamstress and her father as a carpenter. She had an older sister named Tammy. After her parents' divorce in 1981, her mother remarried, and the family lived in a trailer park in Anaheim, California, before moving to a home in Big Bear Lake thanks to her acting success.

Biography
Who was Heather O'Rourke?
Heather Michele O'Rourke was an American child actress born on December 27, 1975, in San Diego, California. She gained fame for her role as Carol Anne Freeling in the supernatural horror film Poltergeist, which was critically acclaimed. She continued to act in its sequels and various television roles until her untimely death on February 1, 1988, at the age of 12.

How did Heather O'Rourke start her acting career?
Heather O'Rourke began her acting career after being discovered by Steven Spielberg while having lunch with her mother. Spielberg cast her for the role of Carol Anne Freeling in the horror film "Poltergeist" (1982), which became a critically acclaimed success. Illness and Death
What were the circumstances surrounding Heather O'Rourke's death?
Heather O'Rourke died on February 1, 1988, due to complications from congenital stenosis of the intestine and septic shock. Her health issues began with flu-like symptoms, leading to cardiac arrest and eventually emergency surgery. Her case was described as "distinctly unusual" due to the absence of prior symptoms of the bowel defect. A private funeral was held, and she was entombed at Westwood Village Memorial Park Cemetery.

Illness and Death
What caused Heather O'Rourke's death?
Heather O'Rourke died due to complications from congenital stenosis of the intestine, exacerbated by septic shock. She had been previously misdiagnosed with Crohn's disease in 1987, leading to treatments that may have masked the symptoms of her true condition. Her death was termed "distinctly unusual" because she exhibited no prior symptoms of the defect before her sudden illness.
