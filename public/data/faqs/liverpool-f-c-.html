FAQs: Liverpool F.C.

Overview
What is Liverpool F.C. and when was it founded?
Liverpool Football Club is a professional football club based in Liverpool, England, competing in the Premier League. It was founded on June 3, 1892, following a dispute between the Everton committee and John Houlding, the club president and owner of Anfield, where Liverpool has played its home games since its formation.
Related topics: Premier League, Anfield
History
How did Liverpool F.C. become a successful football club?
Liverpool F.C. established itself as a major force in domestic football in the 1960s under the management of Bill Shankly. It became perennial title challengers at home and abroad under managers Bob Paisley, Joe Fagan, and Kenny Dalglish, winning a combined eleven league titles and four European Cups during the 1970s and 80s. The club has since won a total of nineteen league titles and six European Cups.
Related topics: Bill Shankly, Bob Paisley, Kenny Dalglish
Achievements
What are the notable domestic and international achievements of Liverpool F.C.?
Domestically, Liverpool has won nineteen league titles, eight FA Cups, a record ten League Cups and sixteen FA Community Shields. Internationally, it has won six European Cups, three UEFA Cups, four UEFA Super Cups, and one FIFA Club World Cup, all being English records. Liverpool became the first English club to have won a league title in eight different decades as of 2020.
Related topics: FA Cup, UEFA Champions League, FIFA Club World Cup
Stadium
What is the history of Liverpool's home stadium, Anfield?
Anfield was built in 1884 and originally used by Everton until they relocated in 1892 after a rent dispute. Liverpool F.C. was then founded to play at Anfield, which has been its home ever since. Anfield's famous section, the Kop, was built in 1906 and is renowned for being the home of Liverpool's most vocal supporters.
Related topics: Anfield, Goodison Park
Rivalries
Who are Liverpool F.C.'s main rivals?
Liverpool's longest-established rivalry is with Everton, known as the Merseyside derby. Liverpool also has a fierce rivalry with Manchester United, regarded as one of the biggest in international football, which stems from the historical competition between the cities during the Industrial Revolution.
Related topics: Merseyside derby, Manchester United
Iconic Moments
What are some of the iconic moments in Liverpool F.C.'s history?
Some of Liverpool's iconic moments include winning the European Cup in 2005 under manager Rafael Benítez, known for the comeback where they triumphed over A.C. Milan, and claiming their 19th league title in 2020 under Jürgen Klopp, marking their first Premier League Era victory.
Related topics: 2005 UEFA Champions League Final, Jürgen Klopp
