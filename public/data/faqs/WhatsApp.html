FAQs: WhatsApp

Features and Functionality
What features does WhatsApp provide?
WhatsApp provides features like instant messaging, voice and video calling, location sharing, voice messages, and document sharing. Notably, it implements end-to-end encryption for security.
Related topics: Voice over IP, End-to-end encryption
WhatsApp Payments and Financial Features
How does WhatsApp Payments work and where is it available?
WhatsApp Payments is a peer-to-peer money transfer feature available in countries like India and Brazil. It uses the Unified Payments Interface (UPI) in India and similar systems in other regions.
Related topics: Unified Payments Interface
WhatsApp Business
What is WhatsApp Business?
WhatsApp Business is a separate app designed for small businesses to communicate with customers. It offers features for managing customer interactions and providing service through chat, enabling businesses to reach customers directly.
Related topics: Business app, Customer service
WhatsApp Business
What is WhatsApp Business and who is it targeted towards?
WhatsApp Business is designed for small to medium enterprises to facilitate customer interactions. It allows businesses to communicate with customers directly and offer services like support and notifications.
Related topics: Customer service, Conversational commerce
Platform Support
Which platforms is WhatsApp available on?
WhatsApp is available on various platforms including Android, iOS, Windows, macOS, web browsers (through WhatsApp Web), and has support for some smartwatch platforms.
Related topics: Android (operating system), IOS, MacOS, WhatsApp Web
Features
What are some of the main features of WhatsApp?
WhatsApp allows users to send text and voice messages, make voice and video calls, share images, documents, user locations, and other content. It supports a range of mobile and desktop operating systems.
Related topics: Text message, Voice message
Criticism and Controversies
What are some notable controversies surrounding WhatsApp?
WhatsApp has faced controversies regarding misinformation spread during elections, and security breaches such as spyware installations. The service also underwent scrutiny for sharing data with Meta (Facebook) and handling user privacy.
Related topics: WhatsApp snooping scandal, End-to-end encryption
Platform Support
What platforms are supported by WhatsApp?
WhatsApp supports various platforms, including Android, iOS, KaiOS, Windows Phone, Windows, macOS, and recently Meta Quest and Web applications. However, official iPad support is still not available.
Related topics: Android, iOS, Windows Phone
Usage Statistics
How many users does WhatsApp have globally?
As of February 2020, WhatsApp had over 2 billion users globally, with significant growth in new registrations monthly. It is one of the most widely used messaging applications worldwide.
Related topics: User statistics, Global user base
General Information
What is WhatsApp and who owns it?
WhatsApp is an instant messaging and voice-over-IP (VoIP) service owned by Meta Platforms. It allows users to send text, voice, video messages, make voice and video calls, and share images, documents, and locations.
Related topics: Instant messaging, Voice over IP, Meta Platforms
Security and Privacy
Does WhatsApp support end-to-end encryption?
Yes, WhatsApp provides end-to-end encryption for all user communications. This feature ensures that only the communicating users can read the messages, keeping them secure from interception from any third party.
Related topics: End-to-end encryption, Security and privacy
Controversies and Criticism
What controversies has WhatsApp been involved in?
WhatsApp has faced criticism for its role in the spread of misinformation, especially in regions like India and Brazil, and for security issues like the NSO Group spyware attack. The app was also part of the Facebook acquisition, which raised privacy concerns.
Related topics: Misinformation, WhatsApp snooping scandal
WhatsApp History and Milestones
When was WhatsApp founded and how did it start?
WhatsApp was founded in February 2009 by Brian Acton and Jan Koum, former employees of Yahoo!. It started as an app for updating statuses like 'at work' or 'on a call'. The app evolved into a messaging service over time.
Related topics: Brian Acton, Jan Koum, Yahoo!
General Information
When was WhatsApp launched and who were its original authors?
WhatsApp was launched in February 2009, created by Brian Acton and Jan Koum, former employees of Yahoo! They incorporated WhatsApp Inc. in California on February 24, 2009.
Related topics: Brian Acton, Jan Koum, Yahoo!
Major Acquisitions
How was Facebook's acquisition of WhatsApp structured financially?
Facebook acquired WhatsApp in February 2014 for approximately $19 billion, structured as $4 billion in cash, $12 billion in Facebook shares, and $3 billion in restricted stock units to WhatsApp's founders.
Related topics: Facebook, Meta Platforms
General Information
What is WhatsApp and who owns it?
WhatsApp is an instant messaging and voice-over-IP (VoIP) service owned by Meta Platforms, allowing users to send text, voice, and video messages, make calls, and share media.
Related topics: Instant messaging, Voice over IP, Meta Platforms
Security and Privacy
What encryption does WhatsApp use to secure messages?
WhatsApp uses end-to-end encryption for all communications. This encryption ensures that only the sender and receiver can read the messages, preventing external parties from accessing them.
Related topics: End-to-end encryption, Signal Protocol
