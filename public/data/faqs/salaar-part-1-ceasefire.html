FAQs: Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire

Overview
What is Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire about?
Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire is a 2023 Indian Telugu-language action drama film directed by Prashanth Neel. The film stars Prabhas as the exiled prince Deva and follows his return to help his friend Varadha, the current prince of the dystopian city-state Khansaar, in a bid to become its ruler amidst a coup. It is the first part of a two-part film series, drawing inspiration from Neel's earlier film 'Ugramm'.

Cast and Characters
Who stars in Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire and whom do they play?
The film features Prabhas as Devaratha 'Deva' Raisaar, and Prithviraj Sukumaran in a dual role as Vardha Raja Mannar, Deva's friend, and Shiva Mannar. Other notable actors include Shruti Haasan as Aadhya, Jagapathi Babu as Raja Mannar, and Sriya Reddy as Radha Rama.
Related topics: Prabhas, Prithviraj Sukumaran, Shruti Haasan
Production and Filming
What was unique about the filming and production of Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire?
Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire marked the first Indian film shot using Dark Centric Theme technology to ensure high visual standards. The film faced several production delays due to challenges like the pandemic and VFX requirements. Filming locations included Telangana, Italy, and Budapest.

Box Office and Reception
How successful was Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire at the box office?
Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire was a commercial success, grossing between ₹618-700 crore worldwide. It was the highest-grossing Telugu film of 2023 and the 15th highest-grossing Indian film of all time.

Future Installments
Is there a sequel to Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire?
Yes, a sequel titled Salaar: Part 2 – Shouryaanga Parvam was announced. The sequel's production began in August 2024 and is set to release in 2026.

Critical Reception
What was the critical reception of Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire?
The film received generally positive reviews, with critics praising its action sequences and the performances of Prabhas and Prithviraj Sukumaran. However, some criticism was aimed at its violence and complex narrative.

Soundtrack
Who composed the soundtrack for Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire and how was it received?
The soundtrack for Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire was composed by Ravi Basrur, marking his fourth collaboration with director Prashanth Neel. The music received a positive reception, with the first single "Sooreede" described as 'pure goosebumps' by fans.
