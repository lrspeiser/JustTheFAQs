FAQs: Murder of Dee Dee Blanchard

Background and Abuse
What kind of abuse did Gypsy-Rose Blanchard endure from her mother?
Gypsy-Rose Blanchard was subjected to severe abuse by her mother, Dee Dee, who forced her to pretend to have numerous physical and developmental disabilities. Dee Dee's behavior was driven by a mental disorder, Munchausen syndrome by proxy, where a caregiver exaggerates or induces health problems in someone else to gain attention and sympathy. Gypsy was made to use a wheelchair and undergo unnecessary medical treatments and surgical procedures.

Overview of the Murder
Who was Dee Dee Blanchard and how was she murdered?
Dee Dee Blanchard, born Clauddine Pitre, was found stabbed to death in her Springfield, Missouri home in June 2015. Her murder was orchestrated by her daughter, Gypsy-Rose Blanchard, and executed by Nicholas Godejohn. The motive included Gypsy's desire to escape abuse and Godejohn's homicidal ideations. Gypsy had been forced to feign severe illnesses by Dee Dee as part of Dee Dee's factitious disorder imposed on another, more commonly known as Munchausen syndrome by proxy.
Legal Proceedings
What were the legal outcomes for Gypsy-Rose Blanchard and Nicholas Godejohn?
Gypsy-Rose Blanchard was charged with second-degree murder and pleaded guilty, receiving a ten-year prison sentence. Nicholas Godejohn, who carried out the stabbing, was found guilty of first-degree murder and received a life sentence without the possibility of parole plus 25 years. Godejohn's trial highlighted his role in the murder and his prior knowledge of the plot.

Discovery and Arrests
How were the authorities alerted to Dee Dee Blanchard's murder?
The authorities were alerted to Dee Dee Blanchard's murder after friends noticed alarming Facebook posts from her account and contacted the police. Her body was subsequently discovered in her home, leading to the arrest of Gypsy-Rose and Nicholas Godejohn in Wisconsin. The investigation revealed that Gypsy had been forced by Dee Dee to pose as seriously ill, even though she was able to walk and was healthy.

Media and Public Reaction
How did the public and media react to the revelations about Gypsy-Rose's abuse?
The public and media reaction to the revelation of Gypsy-Rose's abuse by her mother was one of shock and sympathy. Initially perceived as a participant in a premeditated murder, Gypsy was later seen as a victim of long-term abuse. Her case inspired numerous documentaries and dramatic adaptations, shedding light on issues of Munchausen syndrome by proxy and child abuse.
