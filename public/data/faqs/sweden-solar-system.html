FAQs: Sweden Solar System

Introduction to Sweden Solar System
What is the Sweden Solar System?
The Sweden Solar System (SSS) is the world's largest permanent scale model of the Solar System. Established in Sweden, it represents the Sun through the Avicii Arena in Stockholm, which is one of the largest spherical buildings in the world. The model scales down the Solar System to a scale of 1:20,000,000, where one meter equates to 20,000 km.
Related topics: Solar System model, Sun, Avicii Arena, Stockholm
The System Components
Which celestial bodies are included in the Sweden Solar System?
The Sweden Solar System includes representations of the Sun, all planets and several of their moons, dwarf planets, and small bodies such as comets and asteroids. It even includes abstract concepts like the Termination Shock zone. As small bodies continue to be discovered, the model can be further expanded.
Related topics: Solar System, Planet, Natural satellite, Dwarf planets, Comet, Asteroid, Trans-Neptunian, Termination Shock
Inner Planets
How are the inner planets represented in the Sweden Solar System?
In the Sweden Solar System, Mercury is represented by a 25 cm sphere situated at the Stockholm City Museum; Venus by a 62 cm model at Vetenskapens Hus in Stockholm; Earth is depicted with a 65 cm model at the Swedish Museum of Natural History along with a Moon model; and Mars is symbolized by a 35 cm model at Mörby Centrum. These models are crafted by various artists and are placed at scaled distances from the Sun's representation, the Avicii Arena.
Related topics: Mercury (planet), Venus, Earth, Mars
Gas Giants
Where can one find the gas giants in the Sweden Solar System model?
In this model, Jupiter is represented by a 7.3m ring light located at the Clarion Hotel in Stockholm Arlanda Airport. Saturn is a 6.1m representation, though currently removed due to vandalism, was placed in Uppsala. Uranus is behind the Stora magasinet in Lövstabruk, featuring a 2.6m model. Neptune is modelled at a 2.5m size, shining with blue light, by the river Söderhamnsån in Söderhamn.
Related topics: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune
Trans-Neptunian Objects
What trans-Neptunian objects are included in the model?
The Sweden Solar System features trans-Neptunian objects like Pluto and Charon near the Dellen lakes, designed in reference to the mythology of the respective celestial bodies. Haumea, Quaoar, Ixion, and Makemake are also represented, distributed in various locations across Sweden. These models range in size and are often influenced by mythological or scientific themes related to their names.
Related topics: Pluto, Charon (moon), Haumea, 50000 Quaoar, 28978 Ixion, Makemake
Interesting Features
What unique features distinguish the Sweden Solar System from other models?
This model distinguishes itself by its vast scale, distributed widely across Sweden, and its inclusion of various celestial entities beyond the planets. Notably, the model features abstract concepts such as the Termination Shock, a boundary of the heliosphere, and includes representations of less commonly known objects like ʻOumuamua and Eris, placing emphasis on mythology and artistic representation.
Related topics: Termination shock, Heliosphere, Solar wind, ʻOumuamua, Eris
