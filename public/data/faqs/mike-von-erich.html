FAQs: Mike Von Erich

General Overview
Who was Mike Von Erich?
Mike Von Erich, born Michael Brett Adkisson, was an American professional wrestler who was part of the famous Von Erich wrestling family. He was born on March 2, 1964, in Dallas, Texas, and died on April 12, 1987. He wrestled under the ring name Mike Von Erich and was part of a wrestling dynasty that included his brothers David, Kerry, Kevin, and Chris. He was the son of Fritz Von Erich, a revered Texas wrestler and promoter.
Related topics: David Von Erich, Kerry Von Erich, Kevin Von Erich, Chris Von Erich, Von Erich family
Early Life
What were Mike Von Erich's aspirations before wrestling?
Before getting into wrestling, Mike Von Erich aspired to work for his father's company, World Class Championship Wrestling, as a cameraman. He also had a passion for playing the guitar and wanted to pursue a career as a guitarist. Several wrestlers, including his brother Kevin, King Kong Bundy, Gentleman Chris Adams, Gary Hart, and Jake Roberts, mentioned that Mike never wanted to be a wrestler.
Related topics: Fritz Von Erich, World Class Championship Wrestling, Guitarist, Kevin Von Erich, King Kong Bundy, Chris Adams (wrestler), Gary Hart (wrestler), Jake Roberts
Professional Wrestling Career
How did Mike Von Erich's wrestling career unfold?
Mike Von Erich's professional wrestling career began on November 24, 1983, with a debut match against Skandor Akbar during the 'WCCW Wrestling Star Wars' event at the Reunion Arena. He became known for his involvement in matches and feuds, notably against The Freebirds, alongside his brothers. Following the death of his brother David in February 1984, Mike stepped in to fill David's role in matches. Unfortunately, his wrestling career was cut short due to his untimely death in 1987.
Related topics: Professional wrestling, World Class Championship Wrestling, Skandor Akbar, The Fabulous Freebirds, Kevin Von Erich, David Von Erich
Personal Life
What were some significant personal life events for Mike Von Erich?
Mike Von Erich married Shani Danette Garza on February 14, 1985, but they divorced later that year. He was a born-again Christian. Mike experienced significant health challenges, including a life-threatening shoulder injury in 1985, followed by toxic shock syndrome, resulting in brain damage and weight loss. These health issues, combined with pressures related to his family legacy in wrestling, significantly impacted his life.
Related topics: Born-again Christian, Toxic shock syndrome, Brain damage
Death and Legacy
How did Mike Von Erich's life come to an end, and what is his legacy?
Mike Von Erich died by suicide on April 12, 1987, after leaving a note for his family and later taking an overdose of a sleeping aid at Lewisville Lake. He was found four days later and buried in Grove Hill Memorial Park in Dallas. He was arrested for DUI and marijuana possession shortly before his death. Posthumously, Mike became an unlockable character in the 'Legends of Wrestling' video game series and was inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame in 2009 along with his family. His story was featured in a 2019 'Dark Side of the Ring' episode and was portrayed in the 2023 film 'The Iron Claw'.
Related topics: Suicide note, Placidyl, DUI, Cannabis (drug), Legends of Wrestling (series), Dark Side of the Ring, The Iron Claw (film)
Championships and Accomplishments
What were Mike Von Erich's major achievements in wrestling?
Mike Von Erich was awarded the Pro Wrestling Illustrated 'Most Inspirational Wrestler of the Year' in 1985 and 'Rookie of the Year' in 1984. He held the NWA American Heavyweight Championship once and the NWA World Six-Man Tag Team Championship (Texas version) four times with his brothers. Additionally, he was ranked #288 in the PWI 500 of the PWI Years and #23 in the top 100 tag teams alongside his brothers. He was posthumously inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame in 2009 as part of the Von Erich family.
